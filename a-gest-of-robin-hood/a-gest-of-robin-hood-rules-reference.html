Game Flow & Turn Structure
Turn Sequence & Initiative Core Turn Loop

Play follows a strict loop each turn. Follow this sequence to resolve actions and track eligibility:

  1. Draw an Event card from the shared deck and place it face up.
  2. Move Carriages along roads toward Nottingham as indicated by the carriage symbol on the card.
  3. The First Eligible player chooses an action option, places their eligibility cylinder on the Initiative track, and resolves it.
  4. The Second Eligible player chooses from the remaining options, places their cylinder, and resolves it.
  5. Update initiative by moving the leftmost cylinder to First Eligible and the other to Second Eligible.
  6. Repeat from step 1 until a Royal Inspection or King Richard’s Return card is drawn.
Action Selection Initiative Options

Players may choose from three primary options on the Initiative track:

  1. Single Plot: Perform one Plot in a single map space.
  2. Event: Execute either the unshaded or shaded effect on the current Event card.
  3. Plots & Deeds: Perform one Plot in up to three spaces (paying per space), then optionally perform one Deed.
Passing Mechanics

Either player may instead choose to Pass. Placing their cylinder in any available box on the Initiative track grants Robin Hood 1 Shilling, or grants the Sheriff between 1 and 3 Shillings as indicated on the track boxes.

Fortune Event Handling Immediate Resolution

When a Fortune Event is drawn, resolve it immediately without changing the Initiative order. Execute the text on the card, then draw the next card and continue the standard turn sequence.

Carriage Movement Exception

No Carriages are moved on most Fortune Events, as indicated by the 0 numeral. The Day of Market Fortune Event is the sole exception: move two Carriages when this card is drawn.

Setup Checklist
Board, Pieces & Decks Map Placement

Prepare the game board using this sequence:

  1. Place 2 Henchmen in Nottingham.
  2. Place 1 Henchman and set Submissive markers in Blyth, Mansfield, and Bingham.
  3. Set Submissive markers in Retford, Tuxford, and Newark.
  4. Set a Revolting marker in Remston.
  5. Place 1 Camp in Shire Wood.
  6. Robin Hood places the Robin Hood piece and 3 additional Merry Men in any of the two Forest spaces or Remston, distributed as desired. All placed Merry Men must be Hidden.
Component Distribution Deck Construction
  1. Place remaining wooden pieces in each player’s Available Forces box. Place Carriages on the Sheriff’s mat.
  2. Give the Sheriff 9 Shillings and Robin Hood 5 Shillings.
  3. Place the red Royal Favour cylinder on the silver 1 space on the Royal Favour track.
  4. Place the green eligibility cylinder on First Eligible and the silver cylinder on Second Eligible on the Initiative track.
  5. Separate the two Royal Inspection cards, the King Richard’s Return card, and the five Fortune Events. Shuffle the remaining 24 Event cards.
  6. Deal three piles of four regular Events, shuffle a random Fortune Event into each pile, then deal two more regular Events onto the top of each pile.
  7. Place King Richard’s Return on the bottom of one pile and a Royal Inspection on the bottom of the other two. Stack the piles with King Richard’s Return at the bottom to form the Event deck.
  8. Set aside the Bishop of Hereford and Guy of Gisborne Traveller cards. Shuffle the remaining Traveller cards to form the Travellers deck.
Board & Component Reference
Map Spaces & Terrain Rules Space Classification
Space Type Key Rules Modifiers
Parish Rural spaces with peasant populations. Can hold 1 Camp, any number of Henchmen, Merry Men, and Carriages. Status is either Submissive or Revolting. Submissive: Easier for Sheriff to reveal/capture Merry Men. Enables Confiscate and Hire. Revolting: Harder for Sheriff to capture. Enables Turncoat and Donate. Passive: Treated as neither state except for Rob and Capture, where it counts as Submissive.
Forest Uninhabited green-shaded spaces adjacent to Nottingham. Never Submissive or Revolting. Can hold 1 Camp (2 allowed by Will Scarlet Event), plus any Henchmen, Merry Men, and Carriages. Harder for Sheriff to reveal Merry Men with Patrol. Easier to capture once revealed. Camps here grant extra Shillings during Mischief Phase.
Nottingham Urban space loyal to the Sheriff. Always treated as Submissive. Cannot hold a Camp. Easiest for Sheriff to reveal and capture Merry Men. Carriages reaching here are immediately resolved and removed. Can be targeted by Rob for the Treasury.
Ollerton Hill Intersection space between Forests and Parishes. Inaccessible until Major Oak Event places a Camp there. Treated as a Forest for income. Grants adjacency between Shire Wood/Tuxford and Southwell Forest/Blyth for Robin Hood only. No other pieces may enter or be placed here.
Adjacency & Rivers

Adjacency is determined by gray land borders. Blue river borders only provide adjacency where a bridge is marked. The Boats and Bridges Event may place a bridge marker to add adjacency across a river border.

Pieces, Markers & States Force Pieces

Merry Men are green octagonal cylinders marked with a feather symbol. One special piece is marked RH for Robin Hood. Merry Men may be Hidden (symbol end down) or Revealed (symbol end up). Always set up and place new Merry Men as Hidden. Robin Hood behaves identically to other Merry Men while Hidden and is indistinguishable from them. The Robin Hood player may ask the Sheriff to look away when moving or placing Robin Hood while Hidden. Once Revealed, Robin Hood may always participate in Rob attempts.

Camps are green discs marked with a deer symbol. They are always Revealed and may usually only be removed once no Merry Men remain in the same space. Henchmen are silver wedges.

Shillings are round coin markers. Silver coins are worth 1 Shilling, gold coins are worth 3 Shillings. Players may exchange them as desired. Shilling counts are public information. Draw from the shared pool when instructed; substitute other components if the pool is exhausted.

The red Royal Favour cylinder tracks Justice and Order on a track from 7 Justice to 7 Order. Shift the cylinder when instructed. There is no zero value; one side is always ahead. Shifting beyond either end has no further effect.

Available Forces boxes on player mats hold inventory limits. Keep pieces in these boxes when not on the map or in Prison. Replace pieces using those in the Available Forces boxes. Remove pieces to their respective boxes unless otherwise indicated.

State Management

Spaces selected for Plots and other actions may be marked with white and red pawns as a memory aid. Remove any pawns at the end of each player’s turn.

Prison Rules

The Prison box holds Merry Men removed by the Sheriff’s Capture Plot or certain Event effects. Merry Men may only leave Prison during the Mischief Phase of the Royal Inspection Round, by specific Event effects, or by using the Swashbuckle Deed to remove the special Robin Hood piece.

Road Movement Rules

Carriages are placed face-down by the Confiscate Deed. When an Event card is drawn, move 1 or 2 Carriages one space each along roads toward Nottingham. The Sheriff may move 1 Henchman with each moving Carriage. The Sheriff must move as many Carriages as possible up to the indicated number. Carriages may only move to the adjacent space closer to Nottingham along the road, or into Nottingham once in an adjacent Forest. Carriages reaching Nottingham are immediately resolved and removed to the Used Carriages box. If revealed by a Rob attempt but unsuccessful, the Carriage remains revealed and continues moving. Removed Carriages stay in the Used Carriages box until the Redeployment Phase.

Rob Plot Resolution Flow Target Selection
  1. Select up to three target spaces containing Hidden Merry Men and/or Robin Hood.
  2. In each space, choose a target: a random Traveller, a Carriage, or the Sheriff’s Treasury (if in Nottingham).
  3. Reveal at least one Hidden Merry Man in the space, unless Robin Hood is already Revealed. You may Reveal additional Merry Men as desired.
  4. If targeting a Traveller, draw the top card of the Traveller deck and select one of the two options. If targeting a Carriage, flip it face-up if not already revealed.
Success Calculation
  1. Roll the appropriate die: white die in Submissive (and Passive) Parishes and Nottingham; green die in Revolting Parishes and Forests.
  2. Calculate success: (Total Revealed Merry Men participating + die roll) > (Henchmen in space + target Defense Value). Merry Men already Revealed before the attempt do not count, except Robin Hood who always counts.
  3. Resolve outcome: On success, execute the selected Traveller option, take the Carriage reward, or take 2 Shillings and shift Royal Favour toward Justice if targeting the Treasury. On failure, execute the selected Traveller option, immediately Capture all involved Merry Men if a Trap Carriage was targeted, or do nothing for other targets.
Events & Travellers
Event Card Resolution Regular Events

Regular Events feature two options: one unshaded and one shaded. Both players may choose either effect when executing the Event. Some effects make permanent adjustments to the game state; keep these cards by the board for reference. Fortune Events have only one option and resolve immediately without changing Initiative order.

Inspection Triggers

Royal Inspection cards mark the end of a Ballad. Move one Carriage if possible, then proceed immediately to the Royal Inspection Round. If King Richard’s Return is drawn, conduct only the Unrest Phase, then determine victory.

Traveller Deck & Victims Pile Drawing & Routing

Draw from the Traveller deck only when attempting a Rob Plot targeting a random Traveller. Each card shows a Defense Value (0–3) and typically two options for success or failure. After being Robbed, route Traveller cards to either the Traveller discard pile or the Victims Pile as instructed by the card text.

Pile Management

Keep these piles strictly separate from each other and from the Event discard pile. Shuffle the Traveller discard pile back into the main deck during the Reset Phase of the Royal Inspection Round. Do not shuffle the Victims Pile back into the deck. The Queen Eleanor Fortune Event effect depends on the number of cards in the Victims Pile.

Royal Inspection Round
Phase Checklist Unrest & Mischief
  1. Unrest Phase: Count Submissive Parishes (exclude Nottingham). Shift Royal Favour: 5–7 Submissive → 1 step toward Order; 3–4 Submissive → 1 step toward Justice; 1–2 Submissive → 2 steps toward Justice; 0 Submissive → 3 steps toward Justice. If Justice or Order reaches 5 or more, the game ends immediately. If this is the final inspection, the game ends regardless; Robin Hood wins if Justice is positive, Sheriff wins if Order is positive.
  2. Mischief Phase: Robin Hood gains 1 Shilling per Camp in a Forest (including Ollerton Hill). Robin Hood may conduct a Single Rob Plot followed by a Donate Deed in up to two spaces. Return half (rounded down) of Merry Men in Prison to Available Forces.
Governance & Redeployment
  1. Governance Phase: Sheriff gains 1 Shilling per Submissive space (including Nottingham). Remove half (rounded down) of Henchmen in each Revolting space to Available Forces. Set any Revolting Parish where Henchmen outnumber Merry Men to Submissive.
  2. Redeployment Phase: Sheriff moves all Henchmen in Revolting Parishes and Forests to Submissive spaces, and may move other Henchmen to Nottingham. Return all Used Carriages to Available Forces. Flip all Merry Men on the map to Hidden. Move all Merry Men in Submissive Parishes to any Forest or Parish with a Camp. Other Merry Men may stay or move to a Forest or Camp Parish. If Robin Hood is in Available Forces, place him in any Forest. Unless in Prison, Robin Hood may secretly swap with any other Merry Man on the map.
Reset Procedures
  1. Reset Phase: Shuffle Traveller discard pile into main deck. Place Robin Hood’s cylinder on First Eligible and Sheriff’s cylinder on Second Eligible. Draw the next Event card to begin a new Ballad.
Victory Conditions
Win Triggers & Royal Favour Shifts Immediate Victory

Victory is determined by the position of the red Royal Favour cylinder. If the cylinder reaches 5 or more toward Justice or Order during the Unrest Phase, the game ends immediately with the corresponding winner. If the final Royal Inspection occurs without reaching 5, the player whose side the cylinder currently favors wins.

Quick Reference Tables & Reminders
Initiative Costs, Dice Modifiers & Key Exceptions Track & Die Rules

Initiative Track Shilling Rewards: Robin Hood receives 1 Shilling for passing. The Sheriff receives 1, 2, or 3 Shillings depending on which available box on the track they place their cylinder.

Die Selection for Rob: Roll the white die in Submissive (and Passive) Parishes and Nottingham. Roll the green die in Revolting Parishes and Forests.

Edge Cases & Glossary

Prison Exceptions: Merry Men in Prison do not count toward Available Forces limits. They may only leave during the Mischief Phase, via specific Event effects, or via the Swashbuckle Deed (for Robin Hood only). The Robin Hood player may choose to leave Robin Hood in Prison during the Mischief Phase.

Ollerton Hill Adjacency: Only accessible after the Major Oak Event places a Camp there. Grants adjacency between Shire Wood/Tuxford and Southwell Forest/Blyth for Robin Hood only. Treated as a Forest for income. No other pieces may enter or be placed here.

Free Actions: Events granting free Plots or Deeds cost no Shillings, but all other requirements and procedures still apply unless explicitly modified by the Event text.

Mini-Glossary: Available: Forces and Carriages on a player mat ready for placement. Ballad: Sequence of 6 regular Events and 1 Fortune Event leading to a Royal Inspection. Passive Parish: A Parish permanently altered by the Weapons of the Weak Event, treated as neither Submissive nor Revolting except for Rob and Capture calculations. Treasury: The Sheriff’s funds in Nottingham, targetable by Rob for 2 Shillings and a Justice shift.
