Game Objective

Agents of SMERSH is a cooperative game wherein players portray UN Secret Service spies in an international effort to stop SMERSH from world domination. Players must have between 3 and 5 successful encounters with Henchmen at locations with Location Tokens AND have the correct Intel in the Intel Pool to win the game. Intel in the Pool must match the Intel symbols at the last position of Dr. Lobo’s pawn on his track. Players also immediately win if they defeat all 4 Henchmen. Players lose immediately once Dr. Lobo’s track reaches “9.” If the Players reach the requisite number on the Location Track but do not reveal the correct symbols on the Yellow Intel Tokens in the Intel Pool, they are unable to defeat Dr Lobo and lose the game.

Setup
Base Setup (3–4 Players)

Place the map in a central location with the 4 Henchmen Cards on the right or left side of the map. Place a black cube on Level 1 for each Henchman. Players choose their Character Mats and place 5 white cubes on the starting numbers of the basic skills indicated by the stars. Take 1 additional white cube and place it on the starred number under Resolve on the right side of the Character Mat. Use the cubes to adjust all numbers as you play the game. Take any starting Status Cards and Advanced Skills. Each character starts with 2 Advanced Skills unless otherwise stated on the Character Card. Players pick Advanced Skills. Each player draws 2 Fate Cards from the top of the Fate Deck and slides these cards under location 1 and location 2 indicated at the top right of the Character Mat, with only the names of the cities visible. Place your player token on the starting city or region indicated at the bottom right of the Character Mat. Place the Close Airport tokens in a pile nearby. In a separate pile, place the Yellow Intel Tokens face down and mix them up. Do the same with the 4 Location Tokens. Keep Location and Intel Tokens shuffled at all times. Separate and shuffle the Encounter Decks by continent. There should be 6 decks in the core game. The last person to watch a spy film goes first.

Player Count Adjustments

For 2 Players, place 2 random Yellow Intel Tokens on the board by revealing 2 random Fate Cards. Each player starts with an additional 2 UN Transport Tokens. If playing with Calli, she receives 1 additional UN Transport and 1 Top Secret Card. Choose one Henchman to start the game at Level 2. Remove the Villain Advancement Cards with Injury and [-1] inside the icon of the book on the Villain Advancement side. For 1 Player, add 3 random Intel Tokens to random locations on the board. Choose 2 Henchmen to start the game at Level 2. The other 2 start the game at Level 1. Your character starts the game with a total of 3 UN Transports and Top Secret cards combined. These are in addition to any your character normally receives. Calli may only choose 1 additional UN Transport and 2 Top Secret Cards OR 3 Top Secret Cards. Remove the Villain Advancement Cards with Injury and [-1] inside the icon of the book on the Villain Advancement side, as well as three more random cards with "1" inside the book icon.

Turn Structure

The turn order follows this sequence: Movement → Encounters → Villain Phase → Next Player. Taking a Rest action skips directly to the Villain Phase.

Phase 1: Movement Movement Options Rest Action

If a player has 2 or more Injury Tokens on their Character Card, they may take a Rest Action to Heal 2 Injuries. They skip their encounter phase and go directly to the Villain Phase. If playing solo, the player must also roll the BLUE Die. If you fail the roll, you must draw a Villain card and advance Dr Lobo's track by the number of steps shown.

Standard Movement

Players may move a maximum of 2 spaces but may choose to move only 1 space or remain in their previous location. Players must always stop if they move into an ocean location. Players may use maximum 1 Resolve each turn to gain an additional Movement point but cannot use Resolve to skip an ocean space.

Train Travel

The Trans-Siberian Railway in the Soviet Union can move a player from a city with a Train icon to any other city with a Train icon using only 1 Movement point. Players may move before using the train and after if they still have Movement points.

Airplane Travel

If traveling by airplane, players must already be on a city with an open airport and end on another city with an open airport. That is their full movement. After using an airport, a player may still choose to spend 1 Resolve to gain 1 Movement point.

UN Transport

Return a UN Transport token to the supply to move directly to any city or ocean location on the board.

Phase 2: Encounters Token Priority & Resolution No Token Encounters

If a player stops on a city or ocean with no tokens, draw an Encounter Card from the appropriate deck. The encounter listed under the NO INTEL section should be read out to all players. Resolve any tests associated with the encounter. Unless the Encounter Card says otherwise, if players fail an encounter, players must ALWAYS draw a Villain Card and apply the Villain Advancement side of the card, adding the number to Dr. Lobo’s track. These cards stay in play next to Dr. Lobo. Encounters may also carry additional rewards or penalties listed in italics after the PASS or FAIL text.

Yellow Intel Token Encounters

If a player stops on a city or ocean with a Yellow Intel Token, the player must choose what to do with the token. The player may leave the token on the board and have a NO INTEL encounter. The player may move the Yellow Intel token into the Intel Pool and have an encounter using the WITH INTEL section. The player may exchange the Yellow Intel Token for a Green Location Token. The Yellow Intel Token is returned to the supply, and a face-down Green Location token is placed on a random city determined by drawing a Fate Card. The player then has an encounter using the WITH INTEL section.

Green Location Token Encounters

If a player stops on a city or ocean with a Green Location Token, flip the token to see which Henchman the player is to encounter. Take that Henchman's Card from next to the board and conduct the Test on his card according to the Henchman's level. If successful against the Henchman, advance 1 spot on the Location Track. The Henchman then Levels up 1 level. If the Henchman is defeated at Level 3, players flip his card and remove that Henchman's Green Location Token from the game. If the Player fails their Test, henchmen do not Level up, and the Villain is advanced in the same way as for a failed Encounter. Regardless of whether the encounter with the Henchman was successful or not, remove the Green Location token from the board and shuffle it face down with the others.

Reading Protocol & Secret Missions

For added gameplay value, a player should not read their own encounters unless playing solo. The current player should not be able to see the PASS/FAIL results. Having another player read the encounter aloud lends to the suspense and cooperative play. If a player begins the Encounter phase on a Location that matches one of their Secret Mission locations, they may flip over the appropriate Fate card under their Character Mat. If both cards are flipped over, the player may claim a reward at the end of their current turn. It does not matter the order in which you visit the cities nor does it matter if you fail an encounter at those locations. If a space has more than one token, only one may be encountered per turn.

Phase 3: Villain Phase End-of-Turn Checklist Villain Card Resolution

Regardless of the result of the encounter, at the end of each player's turn, draw a Villain Card and read the End Of Turn Event side of the card, adding and removing Intel on the board. Be sure to close the Airport indicated on the card when drawing it as an End of Turn Event. Airports close and remain closed for the duration of the game. Airports with RED airplane icons never close for any reason. If a player rested during their turn, draw 2 Villain Cards and apply the End of Turn events one after the other.

Secret Mission Rewards

If the Player has flipped over both of their Secret Mission Fate cards, they may now claim one of the following rewards: 1 Top Secret Card drawn from the top of the deck, 1 Resolve (or 2 Resolve if playing solo), a UN Transport Token, or up any Basic Skill by 1. A negative Status Card can be discarded as a reward for completing a Secret Mission. Discard the Fate cards and draw two more for a new Secret Mission. After completing the Villain Phase, the next player in a clockwise direction starts their turn with the Movement Phase.

Resolving Tests & Dice
Dice Pool & Rolling Core Mechanic Reference Pulling & Committing

The grey die with yellow icons is always in play. It never goes into the draw bag, and you must always roll it. Players blindly pull a number of dice from the bag determined by their basic skills. Players must roll all dice pulled from the bag, including the YELLOW Die. They cannot decide to not roll available dice. However, they may choose not to pull their allowed dice from the bag. The player must choose which Skill they want to test before pulling dice. You may draw as many dice as allowed from the bag, inspecting each as you draw them, as long as you have enough skill to draw them. If you still do not like your chances, you may spend a Resolve to add +1 and draw an additional die out of the bag. YOU MUST ROLL ALL DICE DRAWN, INCLUDING THE YELLOW DIE.

Negative Skill Handling

If your Test results in a negative number, you must factor in that negative number when using Resolve or adding +1 in any way to your Test. If a Test brings your skill level to -2, you must somehow add +3 to be able to pull 1 Die from the bag.

Success, Failure & Injuries Icon Values & Consequences Passing & Failing

The YELLOW Die contains 1 Success, 1 Injury, 1 Heal Injury and 3 Failures. The Injury and Heal Injury sides are also considered failures, but players always receive the consequence. All symbols other than the Gun are considered failures as far as it concerns passing encounters. Injuries from dice are received even if you pass the encounter.

Injury Thresholds

Each time you are Injured, take an Injury Token. When you reach Injuries equal to or more than your Life Point Number, discard all Injury Tokens and immediately draw a Villain Advancement Card and advance Dr. Lobo's Track. You may then continue as normal even if in the middle of an encounter. Injury status does not affect pass or failure of the encounter. If you roll multiple dice on a skill test and roll 1 injury and 1 heal, they cancel each other out.

Resolve & Test Modifiers Spending Limits & Options Resolve Usage

Each player has a starting number of Resolve. Resolve generally allows you to add 1 Movement, reroll a single die or receive +1 to an Encounter Test. Be careful how you spend it since it is not easy to get back. Adding dice is not always the best option. You may want to roll and see if you can save your Resolve, spending it to reroll one of the dice if necessary. If you have not spent 1 Resolve to add +1 to your test, you may spend 1 Resolve to reroll 1 die. Unless you have a card or ability that states otherwise, YOU MAY ONLY SPEND MAX 1 RESOLVE PER TURN TO MANIPULATE DICE.

Board State & Token Management
Intel Tokens & Pool Yellow Intel Rules Placement & Pool Management

Intel in the Pool represents the UN's accumulating knowledge about Dr Lobo's intentions, and is essential to win the game. Players can never look at the icons under the Yellow Intel Tokens unless they have a card, power or encounter instruction that allows them to flip one over. Flipped up tokens remain flipped up even if placed back in the Supply. If you need to place new Intel on the board and the supply is empty, remove Intel from the Intel Pool to add to the Board. If you are instructed by a paragraph to add Intel to the Intel Pool and Supply is empty, you are out of luck.

Location Tokens & Henchmen Green Location Rules Henchman Encounters & Tracking

Encountering Green Location tokens is essential to move the Location Track and end the game. If you pass a Henchman Encounter due to a Book or Card Encounter, you do not advance the Location Intel Track, but you always advance the Henchman’s level after a successful Henchman test. If you defeat a Henchman at Level 3 as part of a Book or Card Encounter, you immediately remove the defeated Henchman’s token by flipping up all unrevealed Location Tokens. You then reshuffle all remaining unrevealed tokens and return a random token to each of the previous token locations except where the defeated Henchman was found.

Intel Overflow Hard Limits & Resolution Overflow Procedure

There is a limit to how many Yellow Intel Tokens that can be present on the board. For 1–3 players, the maximum is 5 Yellow Intel Tokens. For 4+ players, the maximum is 4 Yellow Intel Tokens. If resolving the Villain End of Turn Event(s) takes the number of Yellow Intel Tokens over that limit, players must remove all Yellow Intel from the board. If there is more than 1 Location Token, remove the excess. There should be no Intel Tokens and 1 Location Token at most left on the board in the event of Intel Overflow. Removal comes before adding Intel. When an End of Turn event removes Intel from the board, it removes all Intel from that region.

Win & Loss Conditions
  • Win: Location Track reached + Intel Pool matches Dr. Lobo’s symbol, OR all 4 Henchmen defeated.
  • Loss: Dr. Lobo track = 9, OR Intel mismatch at endgame.

Common Rewards & Penalties

1 or 2 Intel: Take either 1 or 2 Intel from Supply and place it face down in Pool. Do not look at the Intel unless it is already flipped up.

Top Secret: Draw a Top Secret Card.

1 or 2 Resolve: If possible, increase your Resolve by 1 or 2.

Up Any Skill: Raise any one of the five skills on your Character Mat by 1.

Up (Basic Skill): Raise the specified skill by 1, if possible.

UN Transport: Gain a UN Transport Token.

Flip Up 1 or 2 Intel In Pool: If available, flip face up 1 or 2 Intel Tokens in Intel Pool.

Flip Up 1 or 2 Intel Anywhere: Flip face up 1 or 2 Intel Tokens in either Pool, Map or Supply. Tokens flipped up remain flipped up the remainder of the game even if returned to Supply.

Do Not Advance Villain: If you fail an encounter, this instructs you not to draw a Villain Advancement Card.

Advanced Skill: Take an Advanced Skill of your choice.

(Specific) Skill: Take the named Advanced Skill, if available.

Lose 1 or 2 Intel from Pool: If available, remove 1 or 2 Intel from Pool. Do not look at the Intel unless it is already flipped up.

1 or 2 Injuries: Player is injured. Take 1 or 2 Injury Tokens.

Lower (Basic Skill): Reduce by 1, if possible, the specified skill.

V-1: Shuffle any Villain Advancement Cards in play on Dr. Lobo. Randomly remove 1 and discard. Adjust Dr. Lobo's track.

Named Status Card: Take the appropriate card and follow the instructions.

No Basic Skill or Resolve may be increased or decreased beyond the limits shown on the Character Mat.

Key Symbols & Terms

Heart: Life Points.

Star: Base Skill.

Book icon: Villain advancement.

Red airplane: Never closes.

Train icon: Trans-Siberian Railway movement.

Close Airport tokens: Used to mark closed airports.

Yellow Intel Tokens: Face-down knowledge tokens.

Green Location Tokens: Face-down Henchman encounter tokens.

Fate Cards: Used for Secret Missions and random city placement.

Villain Deck: Contains End of Turn Event and Villain Advancement sides.

Top Secret Deck: Powerful cards awarded by encounters or missions.

Encounter Deck: Contains encounters written directly on cards.

Status Deck: Gadgets, abilities, or third persons that help or hinder players.

Regional areas and Islands: Starting spaces or encounter locations. Players cannot choose to stay on regional areas or islands. They must leave on the turn after their encounter there.

If any deck is exhausted, shuffle to make a new draw pile.

If you have been rewarded a positive Status Card and all copies are in play, you may gain a Top Secret Card or Gain 1 Resolve instead. If rewarded a negative Status Card, you must Lower Any Skill instead. Third Person Status Cards do not reward anything additional if gained a second time.

Agents cannot trade cards. With the exception of some Status Cards that are common to all players, all cards are personal to the player.

If you receive a penalty or reward that is not specifically listed, it will almost certainly be a Status Card. Search the Status Deck for the correct card.

Cities in the USSR are also considered to be in Asia.
