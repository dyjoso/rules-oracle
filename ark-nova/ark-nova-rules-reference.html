Setup
Global Setup
  1. Place the game board in the center of the table. Place the Break token on the start space of the Break track corresponding to your player count.
  2. Shuffle the 9 Bonus tiles and place 4 face up on the blank yellow Bonus spaces on the board. Do not place tiles on the two yellow spaces with icons. Return the remaining 5 tiles to the box.
  3. Shuffle the Zoo cards to create a face-down deck on the board. Deal 6 face-down cards to the display spaces.
  4. Place the 2 organizers next to the game board as the general supply.
  5. Place the Association board next to the game board, leaving room for one row of cards above and one row below.
  6. Place 1 partner zoo of each continent and 1 university of each kind on the spaces provided on the Association board.
  7. Shuffle the Base Conservation Project cards and place them face-down as a deck next to the Association board. 4P: place the 4 topmost cards face up below the board. Otherwise: place the 3 topmost cards face up below the board.
  8. 2P: take 6 player tokens of any unused color and cover the left level on the left face-up Base Conservation Project card, the middle level on the middle card, and the right level on the right card. Also cover the three spaces in the left column of the donation area on the Association board. Covered levels and spaces are blocked and cannot be used.
  9. Shuffle the Final Scoring cards and place them face down next to the Association board.
  10. Randomly determine a start player. Turn order is clockwise.
Personal Setup
  1. Take a random Map A zoo map and place it face up in front of you. Return remaining zoo maps to the box.
  2. Take one set of Action cards (Build, Cards, Animals, Association, Sponsors). Place the Animals card with Side I up under slot 1. Randomly distribute the remaining 4 cards under slots 2 through 5.
  3. Choose a color and take all components of that color.
  4. Place your appeal, conservation, and reputation counters on the first spaces of their respective tracks. Place your appeal counter according to turn order: start player on space 0, second player on space 1, third on space 2, fourth on space 3.
  5. Place 7 of your player tokens on the designated spaces at the left edge of your zoo map. Keep remaining player tokens next to your zoo map.
  6. Place 1 association worker on the notepad of your zoo map as your active worker. Place the remaining 3 association workers lying down on the designated spaces below the notepad.
  7. Take 25 money from the supply and place it on the notepad of your zoo map.
  8. Draw 2 Final Scoring cards and place them next to your zoo map. Keep them hidden from other players.
  9. Draw 8 Zoo cards. Choose 4 to keep as hand cards and discard the remaining 4 face up to the discard space on the board. Keep hand cards hidden.
  10. After all players have chosen hand cards, reveal the 6 cards on the display.
Turn Structure & Core Mechanics
Taking a Turn

The game proceeds in turns until the Break token reaches the end of its track. On your turn, choose one Action card to perform its action. Shift the chosen card to slot 1, shifting all other Action cards to the right. Perform the action with a strength of X. After completing the action and moving the cards, resolve any after finishing effects. You may not do nothing with your action.

Action Strength & X-Tokens

The number on the card slot indicates the strength X of the Action card located there. Slots range from 1 on the far left to 5 on the far right. You may use X-tokens to increase the strength X of an action by 1 per token used. Return used X-tokens to the general supply. You may use as many X-tokens as you like, though some actions cap at strength 5. You may never hold more than 5 X-tokens. Set the strength of an action before performing it. You cannot use X-tokens gained during the action to increase its strength.

Action Card Upgrades

At the start of the game, all Action cards are displayed on Side I. When you receive a card upgrade, you may flip one Action card of your choice from Side I to Side II. The card remains in its current slot. Upgrades are gained by reaching milestones on the Conservation track or Reputation track, or by gaining your second partner zoo or second university. One upgrade option allows you to choose an alternative, meaning you cannot upgrade all of your Action cards.

Action Reference
Cards Action Side I

Advance the Break token 2 spaces. Then draw cards from the deck or snap.

Drawing from the Deck

Compare strength X with the table on the card. Draw the indicated number of cards (maximum 3) from the deck. You may then discard 1 card from your hand, which can be a newly drawn card or an existing hand card.

Snapping

If strength X is at least 3, you may take exactly 1 card from the display into your hand regardless of your reputation. Replenish the display at the end of your turn.

Side II

Advance the Break token 2 spaces. Then draw cards from the deck or within reputation range, or snap.

Drawing Within Reputation Range

You may draw cards from the display (up to your reputation counter) and/or from the deck. The table on the card is upgraded. Draw in any order. Do not replenish the display until your turn ends.

Snapping

Snapping starts at strength X of 3. Strength above 3 does not change the snap option.

Reputation Progression

You may progress past 9 reputation on the Reputation track.

Build Action Side I

Build exactly 1 building of your choice with a maximum size of X. Pay 2 money per space. Available buildings: kiosk, pavilion, standard enclosure, and Petting Zoo.

Building Rules

Size equals the number of spaces covered. Cost equals size multiplied by 2 money. Pay into the general supply before placing. Build adjacent to an existing building, or on a border space for your first building. You may only build on empty spaces. Buildings cannot extend beyond the map, overlap, or cover water or rock spaces. You cannot build on spaces with the icon until you upgrade this action. A kiosk must be at least 3 spaces from every other kiosk. If you cannot build the desired building legally, choose a different one. Place standard enclosures with the empty side face up. Each zoo may contain only 1 Petting Zoo. Covering a bonus icon grants the placement bonus immediately.

Side II

Build as many different buildings as you like with a maximum total size of X. Newly available: Large Bird Aviary and Reptile House.

Multi-Building & Special Enclosures

Each zoo may contain only 1 Reptile House and 1 Large Bird Aviary. When building either, you may immediately move animals from a standard enclosure to the new special enclosure. You cannot build unique buildings with this action. Pay for each building separately before placing it. Take placement bonuses immediately. Resolve after finishing bonuses only after the action completes. Upgrading allows building on icon spaces. Covering every space on your zoo map (except water and rock) grants 7 appeal immediately.

Animals Action Side I

Play Animal cards from your hand. Compare strength X with the table on the card to determine the maximum number you may play.

Play Sequence
  1. Check conditions: fulfill all requirements on the left side of the card.
  2. Pay cost: pay the cost in the upper-left corner. Reduce cost by 3 money per continent icon if you have a partner zoo from that continent. Pay into the general supply. Money gained from other cards is taken later.
  3. Fill empty enclosure: place the animal in a free enclosure meeting the size requirement. Standard enclosures must be flipped to the occupied side. Some animals require adjacency to water or rock spaces. Petting Zoo animals require the Petting Zoo. Reptiles and some birds may use standard or special enclosures.
  4. Place Animal card: place it next to your zoo map.
  5. Execute effects: advance appeal, conservation, and reputation counters as indicated. Execute immediate effects. Resolve after finishing effects only after completing the entire Animals action and moving the Action card. Choose the order of simultaneous effects.
Multiple Animals

Play multiple animals sequentially, completing all steps for the first before starting the second. You may choose not to play additional animals.

Side II

Play Animal cards from your hand or within reputation range.

Display Playing

Cards played from the display must be within your reputation range. Pay an additional cost equal to the folder number. Do not replenish the display until your turn ends.

Reputation Increase

If strength X is at least 5, increase your reputation by 1 at the start of the action.

Icon Condition

You may play animals that have the icon as a condition.

Association Action Side I

Perform exactly 1 association task. You need at least 1 active association worker on your notepad. Place it on the task. If 1 of your workers is already on the task, place 2. If 3 are on the task, you cannot perform it. Strength X must be at least the task value.

Task 2: Increasing Reputation

Increase reputation by 2. Advance your counter and take any bonus.

Task 3: Gaining a Partner Zoo

Take a partner zoo from the Association board and place it on the lowest empty space on your zoo map. Take any bonus immediately. You cannot duplicate continents. Maximum 4 partner zoos. Requires Side II upgrade for the third or fourth. Each partner zoo reduces animal costs by 3 money per continent icon and provides 1 continent icon.

Task 4: Gaining a Partner University

Take a university from the Association board and place it on the lowest available space. Take any bonus or reputation increase immediately. You cannot duplicate universities. Universities grant research icons and increase hand limit to 5.

Task 5: Conservation Project Work

Support an existing project or play a new Conservation Project card from your hand. Place new cards leftmost above the board, shifting others right. Maximum cards above board: 2P: 2, 3P: 3, 4P: 4. Discard the rightmost if exceeded. Return tokens on discarded cards to the player's supply. Base projects below never move. Fulfill a condition with no token. Place 1 player token from your left edge on the condition. Advance conservation and reputation counters as awarded. Activate the corresponding bonus immediately. Some bonuses recur during breaks.

Side II

Perform multiple different association tasks. Total task value may not exceed strength X. You may make 1 donation.

Conservation Project Display Play

You may play a new Conservation Project card directly from the display if within reputation range. Pay an additional cost equal to the folder number. Do not replenish the display until your turn ends.

Donation

Pay the smallest visible amount in the donation area into the general supply. Cover the space with a player token from your supply. Gain 1 conservation point. If all spaces except the last are occupied, subsequent donations cost 12 money and require no token. You must perform at least 1 task before donating. Maximum 1 donation per action.

Sponsors Action Side I

Play exactly 1 Sponsor card or advance the Break token and take money.

Playing a Sponsor

Strength X must be at least the card level. Fulfill all conditions. Execute instant and/or recurring effects. Place the card next to your zoo map.

Alternative Option

Advance the Break token X steps and take X money. You still take X money even if the track ends early.

Side II

Play multiple Sponsor cards or advance the Break token and take money.

Multi-Sponsor Playing

Strength X plus 1 must be at least the sum of all played card levels. Play cards sequentially, executing instant effects before the next card. You may play from the display if within reputation range. Pay an additional cost equal to the folder number. Do not replenish the display until your turn ends.

Alternative Option

Take X money twice.

Icon Condition

You may play Sponsor cards that have the icon as a condition.

X-Token Action Side I / Side II

Take 1 X-token.

Alternative Action Rules

Use any Action card. The action is independent of slot strength X. You cannot use X-tokens to increase strength. Upgrade status does not matter. You must still select an Action card, move it to slot 1, and shift intervening cards right. You cannot perform this action if you already hold 5 X-tokens.

Key Tracks & Board Reference
The Break Track & Trigger

The Break track counts down to the next break. The Break token starts on the space matching your player count. Advance the token by action effects. When the token reaches the last space, a break is triggered. Stop on the final space. Gain 1 X-token. Ignore additional steps. Reset the token to the starting space after the break concludes.

Reputation & Display

Advance your reputation counter 1 step when allowed. Cards in folders equal to or lower than your counter value are within your reputation range. Take bonuses on bonus spaces immediately and only once. If your counter reaches space 15 and you gain more reputation, the counter stays on 15 and you gain 1 appeal per remaining step. You must upgrade the Cards action to Side II to reach 10 or more reputation. The display holds 6 cards. Replenish the display after your turn by discarding folders 1 and 2, shifting remaining cards down, and filling empty spaces from the deck. Do not look through the discard pile.

Appeal & Conservation Tracks

Appeal determines income during breaks. The Conservation track provides milestone bonuses:

  1. 2 conservation points: upgrade 1 Action card or activate an additional association worker.
  2. 5 or 8 conservation points: take 5 money or 1 bonus token. Remove bonus tokens after use. The money option remains available.
  3. 10 conservation points: the first player to reach or exceed 10 triggers all players to discard 1 Final Scoring card face down. If the game ends before anyone reaches 10, all players discard 1 Final Scoring card before scoring.
Association Board & Workers

The Association board contains a blue task area, a pink donation area, and spaces above and below for Conservation Project cards. Association workers start on your notepad (active) or below it (lying down). Hire workers from below to the notepad. Some zoo maps award conservation points for hiring the last worker. Partner zoos and universities are placed on the right side of your zoo map from bottom to top. Maximum 4 partner zoos and 3 universities. Replenish supplies during breaks, skipping types all players already possess. The donation area costs increase as spaces are covered. Base Conservation Project cards below the board never move.

Zoo Map Layout & Building Rules

The zoo map contains a central building area, left edge bonus spaces, bottom card slots 1 through 5, right side spaces for partner zoos and universities, and a notepad for workers, money, and X-tokens. Map A features a pre-placed 3-space enclosure on the indicated spaces. Advanced maps feature unique special abilities. Universal building restrictions apply: do not build on rock or water spaces. Buildings must be adjacent to existing structures or border spaces. Kiosks require 2 empty spaces between them. Icon spaces are blocked until the Build action is upgraded. Covering bonus icons grants immediate placement bonuses. Buildings cannot overlap or extend beyond the map.

Break Procedure

When the Break token reaches the end of the track, complete your current turn, then all players execute the following steps in order:

  1. Hand card limit: discard surplus cards down to your hand limit. The limit is normally 3, but increases to 5 if you possess a qualifying university.
  2. Tokens on Action cards: return Multiplier, Venom, and Constriction tokens to the supply. They lose all effect.
  3. Association board: return all your association workers to your notepad. Replenish partner zoo and university supplies, skipping types all players already own.
  4. Replenish display: discard the two bottom cards of the display, shift remaining cards down, and fill empty spaces from the deck.

  5. Take income: collect income simultaneously unless effects conflict, in which case proceed clockwise starting from the break initiator and replenish the display after each player. a. Collect appeal income: take money indicated by your counter on the Appeal track. b. Collect kiosk income: take 1 money per unique building, special enclosure, occupied standard enclosure, and pavilion adjacent to each kiosk. Empty standard enclosures do not count. c. Collect recurring bonuses: take income from activated conservation bonuses and Sponsor cards with the recurring income icon.

  6. Break track: return the Break token to the starting space for your player count. The next player in clockwise order takes their turn.

End Game & Final Scoring

The game ends when your conservation and appeal counters are in the same scoring area or have passed each other. If triggered at the end of your turn, all other players take 1 more turn. If triggered during a break, all players take 1 more turn. Proceed to final scoring:

  1. Score cards: gain conservation points or appeal from Final Scoring cards and cards with end-game icons. If no player reached 10 conservation points, discard 1 Final Scoring card before scoring.
  2. Determine Victory Points: add the white number on your conservation space to your appeal counter value.
  3. The player with the highest total wins. Ties are broken by the most Conservation Projects supported (count player tokens removed from your left edge). Remaining ties share the victory.
Quick Reference Tables
Icon & Bonus Reference

Placement bonuses, track icons, card condition icons, and recurring effect symbols are defined by the following rules:

  1. Rock and water icons indicate adjacency requirements for enclosures.
  2. Continent icons reduce animal costs by 3 money per icon when a matching partner zoo is present.
  3. Animal category icons interact with conservation projects and card effects.
  4. Recurring income icons on Sponsor cards and conservation bonuses trigger during breaks.
  5. Donation area icons indicate escalating costs for conservation points.
  6. End-game icons on Sponsor and Animal cards trigger during final scoring.
  7. Interactive effect icons require 5 or more appeal to activate.
Enclosure & Animal Category Reference Enclosure Sizes & Shapes

Standard enclosures come in sizes 1 through 5. Special enclosures include the Petting Zoo (size 3), Reptile House (size 5), and Large Bird Aviary (size 5). Each zoo may contain only 1 of each special type. Special enclosures are never flipped. Mark occupied spaces with player tokens.

Animal Categories

The 7 categories are Bird, Herbivore, Predator, Primate, Reptile, Bear, and Petting Zoo animal. Bear and Petting Zoo animal are special cases with black icons. Bears function as a subcategory. Game mechanics implicitly include bears and Petting Zoo animals when asking for categories unless explicitly excluded.

Card Layout Guides Animal Cards
  1. Upper-left: minimum enclosure size.
  2. Adjacent icons: rock/water adjacency requirements.
  3. Right of size: special enclosure requirements (if applicable).
  4. Below size: money cost.
  5. Left side: additional conditions.
  6. Upper-right: category and continent icons.
  7. Lower-right: appeal increase, plus optional conservation or reputation gains.
  8. Effect text: immediate or after finishing resolution.
  9. Blue box: solo alternate effect.
Sponsor Cards
  1. Upper-left: level requirement.
  2. Adjacent icons: rock/water adjacency for special buildings.
  3. Left side: conditions.
  4. Upper-right: provided icon.
  5. Top blue/purple background: recurring effect.
  6. Lower-left yellow background: instant effect.
  7. Lower-right brown background: end-game effect.
  8. Self-explanatory effects are noted on the card. Only upper-right icons count for triggering effects.
Final Scoring Cards

Tables or text indicate how to gain 1 to 4 additional conservation points. Consider your universities, displayed cards, and their icons. Maximum 4 conservation points per card.

Solo Play & Variants (Appendix)
Setup Modifications

Lay out 3 base Conservation Project cards without blocking tokens. Do not use the Break token. Place 7 unused player tokens on the left column of the solo tile. Start with 20 appeal by placing the solo tile on space 45 of the Appeal track.

Turn & Card Adjustments

Interactive effects are replaced by the blue stripe solo effect. Treat "max. 25 appeal" conditions as "max. 45 appeal". Maximum 2 Conservation Project cards may be above the Association board. Advancing the Break token does nothing, but you still take the associated money. You do not gain an X-token for triggering a break.

Gameplay & Break Adjustments

At the end of each turn, move the topmost token from the solo tile's left column to the right. Moving the last token reveals the break icon and triggers an immediate break. At break start, place the topmost token on the smallest visible donation space (or next to space 12 if full). Return remaining tokens to the left column. Perform break steps 1 through 5. The game ends after 6 rounds when 2 tokens remain and you move the bottom one. Do not trigger a final break. Proceed to scoring. You win with 100 or more Victory Points.

Difficulty Levels

Beginners should start with 20 appeal. Experienced players may start with 10 appeal (place solo tile on space 35, treat max appeal conditions as 35) or 0 appeal (do not use the solo tile).

Solo Challenge

Play 3 consecutive games without shuffling cards between games. Remove all used cards, maps, and bonus tiles after each game. Reuse 3 random bonus tiles for the third game. You lose access to out-of-play cards except for Assertion and Dominance abilities. Record each score. A combined total of 300 or more passes the challenge.
