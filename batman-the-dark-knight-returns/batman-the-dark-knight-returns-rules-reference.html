Core Game Loop
Round Structure

Each Book consists of up to 4 rounds. Your objective is to survive 4 rounds, discover & defeat the boss. Each round follows this 3-phase flow:

Start of Round
  1. Choose Action Cards: Deal the round deck, select cards to keep, and add the rest to the event deck.
  2. Shuffle Decks: Shuffle the event deck and your fight deck.
  3. Write Adversary Numbers: Distribute the round's adversary count across the turn slots on the GCPD Track.
Batman Turns

Take 4 turns in a row, resolving the full turn sequence for each.

End of Round
  1. Place Riots: Check locations for riots and place them if triggered.
  2. Draw Path: Draw a new path connecting any two locations.
  3. Advance Doomsday: Move the Doomsday Clock tracker one space.
Batman Turn Sequence

Each turn follows this strict 7-step sequence:

  1. Resolve Event: Draw and resolve an event card.
  2. Move Batman: Move to a location.
  3. Activate Ally: Command an ally to move and use one of their actions.
  4. Fight or Sneak: Fight or sneak past enemies.
  5. Use Action Space: Use the action printed on your space.
  6. Add Adversaries: Place new adversaries on the map based on the GCPD Track.
  7. Advance Turn Tracker: Move the turn tracker one space on the GCPD Track.

Note: Detective Cards and Fight Cards may be played during steps 2.1 through 2.5, but never during a fight.

Combat & Resolution
Starting a Fight

You may start one fight per turn during the Fight or Sneak step. You can only fight an enemy in the action space you occupy, or a Boss at your location.

Draw Fight Card

At the start of each fight, draw 1 Fight Card from your fight deck and add it to your hand. You only draw one card for the entire fight. If your fight deck is empty, you draw nothing.

Build Enemy Dice Pool

Determine how many dice your enemies will roll by consulting the Enemy Dice Pool Reference table. Take the appropriate dice from the enemy tokens card and place them in front of you.

Enemy Dice Limit

Enemies can roll no more than 6 dice total in a fight. Even if the pool calculation exceeds 6, they only roll 6. This also limits you to defeating a maximum of 6 adversaries in a single fight.

Fight Resolution Flow

Resolve fight rounds using this strict sequence until you win, end the fight, or lose:

  1. Batman Roll: Choose up to 3 Batman Dice from your utility belt and roll them. If an active ally used an Assist Batman action, you may also roll all ally dice on their card. This does not count toward your 3-die limit.
  2. Enemy Roll: Roll all dice in the enemy dice pool.
  3. Rerolls and Flips: Resolve each Reroll and Flip you rolled, one at a time, in your chosen order. Reroll lets you reroll 1 Batarang and use the new result. Flip lets you flip 1 Batarang to its opposite face. Flip x2 and Reroll x2 function identically but affect up to 2 dice.
  4. Blocks: Resolve your Blocks first, then enemy Blocks. A Block changes an opposing die to a Miss. Block x2 changes up to 2 opposing dice to Misses.
  5. Pows and Rams: Resolve all Pows and Rams simultaneously. A Pow deals 1 damage. A Ram deals 1 damage and you take 1 damage of your choice. Pow x2 deals 2 damage. If a single Block is applied to a Pow x2, it becomes a single Pow instead of a Miss.
  6. Cooldown: Place each Batman Die or ally die showing Pow or Pow x2 onto the cooldown card. These dice are unavailable for the rest of the fight.
  7. End or Continue: If no enemy dice remain, proceed to cleanup. If dice remain, you may end the fight or continue to another round.
Damage & Cooldown Dealing Damage

For each point of damage dealt to enemies, remove 1 enemy die from the pool and set it aside. Removed dice are not rolled again. When fighting riots, damage is always dealt to mutant dice first. Cop dice are only removed when no mutant dice remain.

Taking Damage

For each point of damage taken, move one damage marker down 1 space. You may choose which track (Grit, Health, or Sanity) to apply damage to, unless a specific type is mandated. You may split damage from a single source across tracks. Allies and vehicles cannot take damage.

Unblockable Attacks

Some attacks are unblockable. You cannot use Blocks or other effects to change unblockable damage to a Miss or negate it.

Cooldown

Dice placed on the cooldown card represent depleted energy. They remain unavailable until the end of the fight.

Fight Cleanup

Count the removed enemy dice to determine defeated enemies. Return all used dice to their proper areas. Enemy dice go to the enemy tokens card. Batman Dice go to your utility belt. Ally dice go to their cards.

Defeating Adversaries

If you removed at least 1 die, defeat the adversary under your figure. For each additional die removed, defeat another adversary of the same type in your location or an adjacent location. You can only defeat adversaries matching the type you fought.

Defeating Riots

To defeat a riot, you must remove all enemy dice during the fight. If successful, place your figure and any allies on empty action spaces there. If any dice remain, the riot stays and will roll all its dice in the next fight.

Movement & Board Management
Map & Locations

The map is organized hierarchically to manage movement and placement:

Regions

The map is divided into 3 regions: Uptown, Midtown, and Downtown.

Districts

Each region contains 2-3 colored districts. Each district has 4-6 location circles. The two light blue islands belong to the same district.

Locations

Each location circle has 3 action spaces. The colored ring around a location indicates its district. Locations connected by Printed Paths or Drawn Paths are considered adjacent.

Action Spaces

Moving to an action space allows you to use its printed action, provided it is not covered by an enemy token. Only 1 figure can occupy each action space at a time.

Path Rules

Printed Paths are permanent routes. Bridges connect Gotham to Wayne Manor and can only be used by Batman. Deep Water surrounds the city. Paths cannot be drawn through it.

Drawing Paths

At the end of each round, you may draw a new path connecting any two locations. Drawn paths cannot cross other paths, bridges, or locations. They cannot connect more than two locations or create intersections. Once drawn, they function identically to printed paths for movement and adjacency. Card effects may destroy drawn paths. Erase them to clear the space for future paths. You cannot destroy a drawn path unless an effect specifically commands it.

Action Space Modifiers

Card effects may permanently upgrade or destroy action spaces. These modifiers persist until the end of the playthrough.

Upgrading

Draw an x2 on an empty action space. When used, you may take its action twice. Upgraded spaces cannot be upgraded again. Wayne Manor cannot be upgraded unless a card specifies otherwise.

Destroying

Cross out the icon on an empty action space. Figures can no longer move to or use it. Adversaries cannot be added or moved to destroyed spaces. Destroyed spaces do not count toward riot triggers. A destroyed space cannot be destroyed again.

Riot Interaction

When determining if a riot starts, only undestroyed action spaces must be filled with adversaries. If a location has a destroyed space, it requires one fewer adversary to trigger a riot.

Wayne Manor & Vehicles Access Rules

Wayne Manor is a separate region outside Gotham with only 1 location. Only Batman can access it via Bridges. Allies, adversaries, and bosses cannot enter unless a special effect allows it. When moving to or from Wayne Manor, you must immediately end your movement.

Healing

Wayne Manor has a single action space with 3 icons. Using it allows you to take all 3 actions and regain 1 Grit, 1 Health, and 1 Sanity.

Vehicle Acquisition

Each time you enter Wayne Manor, you may acquire 1 vehicle from the slots above it. Place the acquired vehicle card and figure in your available vehicles slot. There is no limit to how many vehicles you can store here. Unused vehicles carry over to the next Book.

Vehicle Usage

Each vehicle can be used once per Book. You may use available vehicles at any time on your turn, except during a fight. Resolve the effect as described on the card.

Placement Restrictions

Once placed, a vehicle cannot be moved for the rest of the Book. You cannot move to its action space to use it again. Its presence prevents riots from starting at that location.

Cards, Goals & Tracking
Detective & Fight Cards Detective Cards

You may play any number of Detective Cards from your hand during your turn to take special actions. If a card does not specify timing, it may be played at any time on your turn, except during a fight. After resolving a Detective Card, place it in the action card discard pile. There is no hand limit. Unused cards carry over to the next round. Some may carry over to the next Book.

Fight Cards

During a fight, you may play any number of Fight Cards from your hand to activate their effects. Each card indicates when it may be played. After resolving a Fight Card, place it in the action card discard pile. It will not be drawn again for the rest of the current Book. Any Fight Cards drawn but not played during a fight remain in your hand and can be used in future fights or Books.

Goals & Progression Goal Completion

Goal Cards are secondary objectives. When you meet the conditions to complete a goal, flip it over and place it in front of you.

Reward Application

You immediately gain the reward on the back of the completed Goal Card. These rewards are permanent and usable for the rest of your playthrough.

Story Card Flipping

The Story Card outlines objectives and special rules. When instructed, flip it to the next part. Only the rules and objectives on the face-up side apply. Reference cards provide Book-specific rules. Only the face-up side applies.

Carryover Rules

Uncompleted goals remain in play across Books. Partial progress does not carry over. You must start each goal fresh in the new Book.

GCPD Track & Adversaries

The GCPD Track functions as your police scanner and turn tracker. It tracks the current turn, adversary distribution, and placement zones.

Track Components
  1. Turn Tracker: Marks the current turn.
  2. Adversaries Per Round: Total adversaries added each round. Written in the top white box.
  3. GCPD District/Region: Indicates where adversaries are added this turn.
  4. Adversary Type: Indicates the type added this turn.
  5. Adversary Number: Where you write how many adversaries are added this turn.
Writing Adversary Numbers

At the start of each round, distribute the round's total adversary count across the flame symbols for the upcoming turns. Write a number between 1-4 in each slot. The sum must equal the total in the top box. Do not place adversaries yet. They are added at the end of each turn.

Final Round Exception

In round 4, only 3 flame symbols are available. Treat the final turn as a 0. Ensure the other 3 turns sum to the top box total.

Placing Riots

At the end of each round, check locations where every undestroyed action space contains an adversary. Place a riot on each triggered location. When placing a riot, remove all adversaries from that location and return them to the supply. Riots cannot be placed at locations with friendly figures. If a location fills with adversaries during a round, you still have until the end of the round to prevent a riot.

Win/Loss & Game End
Loss Conditions

You lose immediately if any of the following occur:

  1. Any of your damage tracks (Grit, Health, or Sanity) reaches 0
  2. The Doomsday Clock reaches 12
  3. A 12th riot would be placed on the map
  4. The Boss is not defeated by the end of round 4

If you lose at any time, you must restart from the beginning of Book 1.

Completing a Book & Saving Progress End of Book Cleanup

When you defeat the Boss and complete a Book, perform these steps:

  1. Return the Boss card, Boss figure, story card, round sequence card, enemy tokens card, and Batman card to the box.
  2. Discard all Standard (S) action cards in your hand. Keep cards with a Book number icon.
  3. Clear tokens from in-progress goals and return them to the supply. Discard action cards underneath in-progress goals.
  4. Gather all action cards from the deck, discard pile, event deck, and fight deck. Sort Standard (S) cards from Book-numbered cards. Return Book-numbered cards to the box. Place Standard (S) cards back on the action deck space.
  5. Return vehicle figures on the map to their cards, and return these cards to Wayne Manor. Leave unused acquired vehicles in your available vehicles area.
  6. Erase the number in the top white box of the GCPD Track, all adversary numbers, and modifiers inside the flames. Do not erase modifiers outside the flames.
  7. Erase the Boss's HP.
  8. Return all clue tokens to the box.

Do not clear, discard, or erase anything unless explicitly instructed. Many components and map drawings carry over.

Save State

To save progress after a Book, mark the following on the board with your marker: Batman's location (Bat), ally locations (initials), mutant locations (M), cop locations (C), press locations (P), riot locations (R), current damage track values, and Doomsday Clock position. Store acquired action cards, completed goals, unused vehicles, ally cards, and utility belt/ally dice in an Acquired container. Store in-progress goals and unacquired vehicles in an Unlocked container. Return all other components to the box.

Resume State

To resume, unpack components and lay them out as recorded. Follow setup instructions for the next Book. To pause mid-Book, additionally store collected clue tokens in Acquired, your fight deck in Unlocked, completed goals with remaining Bat-Signal tokens separately, and in-progress goals with their tokens/cards separately. Mark the Boss location, vehicle locations, unrevealed clue locations, GCPD token regions/districts, and turn tracker position on the board.

Enemy Dice Pool Reference
Enemy Type Enemy Dice Pool Calculation
Mutant 1 mutant die per mutant in your location + 1 mutant die per mutant or riot in adjacent locations + 1 mutant die if the Boss is in your location
Cop 1 cop die per cop in your location + 1 cop die per cop or riot in adjacent locations + 1 cop die if the Boss is in your location

Book-Specific Rules (Book 1)
Setup & Story Hooks Initial State

Set up using Standard (S) and Book 1 components. Place the board, set the Doomsday Clock to 2, and set Grit, Health, and Sanity tracks to 8. Place the turn tracker at turn 1 on the GCPD Track. Prepare adversary, riot, and Bat-Signal token piles. Place Book 1 cards in matching slots: Boss (Two-Face), enemy tokens, ally (Commissioner Gordon), Batman, and cooldown card (1-2 side up). Place 3 Book 1 goal cards face-up in goal slots. Place 2 Book 1 vehicle cards in vehicle slots with matching figures. Place the Book 1 round sequence card and story card (Part 1 side up) for reference. Shuffle Standard and Book 1 action cards for the action deck. Place 6 blue Batman Dice in your utility belt, Two-Face's 2 Boss dice on his card, and all mutant/cop dice on the enemy tokens card. Write 7 in the top white box of the GCPD Track. Flip 10 GCPD tokens marked 1 facedown, place 1 random on each of the first 8 spaces, return 2 to the box. Do the same for tokens marked 2 on the next 8 spaces. Flip all track tokens face-up. Place Commissioner Gordon at Gordon's House and Batman at Wayne Tower. Place Two-Face and 1 mutant at Arkham Asylum TF (Two-Face in the center). Place 3 mutants in each district, 3 cops in each region, and 3 press in each region (press must be in locations with at least 1 mutant or cop). Choose different action spaces for each placement.

Clue & Guard Rules

Flip 8 Book 1 clue tokens facedown and randomize them. Place 1 on the mutant at Arkham Asylum TF. Place the remaining 7 on mutants of your choice in other districts. Mutants with clues are Guards. To reveal a clue, defeat its Guard in a fight, then use the action space where the clue is located. You cannot remove Guards by any means other than fighting. If you defeat a Guard from an adjacent location, leave the clue facedown until you move there and use it. If a riot triggers at a Guard's location, remove the Guard and place the clue on the riot. Defeating the riot reveals the clue.

Boss Discovery Trigger

When you reveal a clue, discard it if it is a Dead End. If it is a coin, place it at Wayne Manor. Upon revealing a 4th coin, move Two-Face from Arkham Island to that location, discard all unrevealed clues, and flip the story card to Part 2.

Character & Enemy Abilities Batman Movement

Whenever you move through a location with 2 or more adversaries or a riot, immediately take 1 damage of your choice. You do not take damage if you start or end your movement at such a location.

Fights

At the start of each fight with Cops, lose 1 Grit.

Commissioner Gordon Ally Movement

May travel up to 3 paths in one movement. If placed on a Press token at the destination, immediately remove it. May use movement to switch action spaces at his current location.

Ally Action: Quell Riot

Requires both Gordon and Batman at the same location with a riot. Spend 1 Grit to remove the riot. Afterwards, move Gordon to any action space at GCPD Headquarters.

Cops Special Ability

At the start of each fight with Cops, lose 1 Grit.

Dice Modifiers

Two specific die faces count as a Miss.

Mutants Special Ability

None.

Dice Modifiers

One specific die face counts as a Miss.

Riots Special Ability

None.

Dice Modifiers

Three specific die faces count as a Miss.

Two-Face Both Sides Match

If Two-Face rolls the same non-Miss result on both his dice, immediately lose 1 Sanity (unblockable).

See a Reflection

Each -1 Two-Face rolls deals 1 unblockable Sanity damage.

Vehicles & Special Rules Batcopter
  1. Acquisition: Visit Wayne Manor to acquire.
  2. Usage: Use once per Book at any time except during a fight. Immediately move to Wayne Manor, then place the Batcopter on any empty action space in the location you left.
  3. Placement Effect: Adversaries cannot be added or moved to its action space. Riots cannot start at that location.
  4. End of Book: If on the map, return its card and figure to Wayne Manor.
Remote Control Bat
  1. Acquisition: Visit Wayne Manor to acquire.
  2. Usage: Use once per Book at any time except during a fight. Remove 1 mutant from each location adjacent to yours, then place the Remote Control Bat in an action space where you removed a mutant.
  3. Placement Effect: Adversaries cannot be added or moved to its action space. Riots cannot start at that location.
  4. End of Book: If on the map, return its card and figure to Wayne Manor.
