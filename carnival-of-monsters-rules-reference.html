Game Overview & Setup

Objective: Score the most Victory Points after four Seasons of play.

Components: Main Board, Carnival Cards, Season Cards, Loan Cards, Player Boards, Gold Crowns, Hunter Tokens, Royal Hunter dice, Starting Land Cards.

Setup Checklist:

  1. Place the Main Board in the center of the table.
  2. Shuffle all Carnival Cards together and create a face-down draw deck on the matching space of the Main Board.
  3. Shuffle the Season Cards and draw four at random. Place them face-down on the matching space of the Main Board. Return the remaining three Season Cards to the box without revealing them.
  4. Stack the Loan Cards on the ledger space of the Main Board.
  5. Give each player a Player Board and four Gold Crowns. Place the remaining Gold Crowns, all Hunter Tokens, and the Royal Hunter dice in the center of the Main Board to form the bank.
  6. Shuffle the ten Starting Land Cards and deal two to each player. Place your Starting Land Cards face-up next to your Player Board. Return any unused Starting Land Cards to the box.
Season Flow Overview

Carnival of Monsters is played over four Seasons. Each Season follows the same four-phase structure. All players act simultaneously during the drafting phase. The direction that cards are passed changes each Season: pass to the left during the first and third Seasons, and pass to the right during the second and fourth Seasons. Repeat Phases 1 through 4 until the fourth Season ends.

Phase 1: Reveal Season Card Season Bonus & Aerie Exception

Turn over the next Season Card on the Main Board. The Season Card indicates one type of Monster that is in demand this Season. The first time each player displays a Monster that matches the Season Card, they receive a Season Bonus of two Gold Crowns from the bank. This bonus can only be received once per Season.

If the revealed Season Card is the Aerie Season, each player gains two Gold Crowns the first time they play an Aerie Monster.

Trophy Tracking

The player who displays the most impressive collection of Monsters matching the current Season Card will earn the Victory Points shown on the Season Card. This Trophy is tracked from Phase 1 but officially awarded during Phase 4.

Phase 2: Draft & Play Cards Drafting Loop

Deal eight Carnival Cards to every player. All players keep their cards secret. Players will capture cards one at a time until all cards in their hands have been captured. Each capture cycle follows three steps:

  1. Capture one card by placing it face-down on the journal space of your Player Board.
  2. Pass all remaining cards in your hand to the next player in the current Season direction. When you receive cards from your neighbor, place them face-down on your Player Board without looking at them.
  3. Decide whether to play or keep the captured card.
Playing or Keeping Cards

After all players have resolved their captured card, pick up the cards passed to you, choose one to capture, pass the rest, and repeat the cycle until all Carnival Cards for the Season have been captured.

If you choose to keep a captured card, you must pay one Gold Crown to the bank. Place the kept card face-down in the Kept Cards space on your Player Board. There is no limit to the number of cards you can keep. You may play any of your Kept Cards at any time, except during the Danger Check, as long as you meet the requirements to play that card.

Phase 3: Danger Check Hunter Sources

Tally the total number of Danger symbols on the Monsters you displayed during the current Season. Do not count Danger symbols on Kept Cards or in your Menagerie. You must have Hunters equal to or greater than this total. You may draw from the following Hunter sources:

  1. Royal Hunters: One player rolls all three Royal Hunter dice once per Season. Add the cage values to determine the total Royal Hunters available. All players may use these Hunters. Unused Royal Hunters do not carry over to the next Season.
  2. Staff Hunters: Provided by played Staff Cards. Each Staff Hunter can be used once per Season.
  3. Hunter Tokens: Awarded by certain Event Cards. Each Hunter Token can be used only once and must be returned to the supply after use. Unused Hunter Tokens can be saved for later Seasons.
Resolution & Fines

Add your available Staff Hunters and Royal Hunters together. If the sum equals or exceeds your Danger symbol total, your Monsters are secure. If you do not have enough Hunters, you may discard Hunter Tokens to cover the difference. If you still lack Hunters after using all available sources, you must pay a fine of three Gold Crowns for each remaining Danger symbol. If you cannot pay the fine, you must take out enough Loans to cover the cost. You cannot play any cards from your Kept Cards during this phase.

Phase 4: Season End Trophy Award

Players compare all Monsters they displayed this Season that match the current Season Card. The player with the highest combined sum of Victory Points or Danger symbols on matching Monster Cards takes the Season Card as a Trophy and places it face-down in their Menagerie. If there is a tie for the highest sum, no one takes the Trophy and the Season Card is discarded.

Cleanup & Game Continuation

Move all Monsters you played this Season face-down into the Menagerie space on your Player Board. All Land Cards remain in play for the upcoming Seasons. If you have just completed the fourth Season, the game ends. Otherwise, begin the next Season at Phase 1.

Card Reference
Land Cards

Land Cards provide Land Points for a specific Region. Land Points are spent to play Monster Cards. Some Land Cards feature Distant Lands symbols. If a Land Card shows one Distant Land symbol, you may only play it if you already have at least one Land Card of the same Region in play. If it shows two Distant Land symbols, you must already have at least two Land Cards of the same Region in play. The same matching Land Cards can qualify for any number of Distant Land Cards.

Monster Cards

Monster Cards depict creatures worth Victory Points at game end. To play a Monster Card, you must spend Land Points from matching Region Land Cards equal to the Monster's Level. Each Land Point can only be spent once per Season. You may split a single Land Card's Land Points across multiple Monsters.

Some Monsters are Dangerous and feature Danger symbols, which trigger the Danger Check. Monsters with the Monstrous Lore symbol allow you to immediately draw Carnival Cards from the deck, which you may play immediately or keep for free. Dreamlands Monsters can be paid for using any type of Land Points. However, Land Points from Dreamlands Land Cards can only be used to play Dreamlands Monsters.

Event & Staff Cards

Event Cards provide a one-time benefit when played and must be discarded afterward. Staff Cards represent hired experts. You must pay Gold Crowns to play a Staff Card, as shown in its top corner. Staff Cards provide permanent ongoing effects until the end of the game. The four Intrepid Explorers function as both Staff Cards and Land Points. When played, choose a Land Type. The Explorer functions as one Land Point of that chosen type for the rest of the game and is turned sideways to indicate this.

Secret Goal Cards

Secret Goal Cards are never played. They must always be kept until the end of the game. At game end, reveal all your Secret Goal Cards and earn the Victory Points shown if you have fulfilled the listed requirements.

Economy & Loans

Gold Crowns spent during the game always go to the bank, and Gold Crowns earned are taken from the bank. You may make change with the bank at any time. If you are required to spend Gold Crowns you do not have, you must take a Loan Card from the Main Board. When you take a Loan Card, you also take three Gold Crowns from the bank. You may use these Gold Crowns normally. Loans cannot be repaid and must be kept until the end of the game. If you run out of standard Loan Cards, you may exchange three Loan Cards for one Consolidated Loan Card. Consolidated Loan Cards do not provide additional Gold Crowns and penalize you fifteen Victory Points. All Loan penalties are applied at game end.

End Game & Scoring

The game ends immediately after the fourth Season concludes. Reveal all Secret Goal Cards among your Kept Cards and award Victory Points for any fulfilled requirements. Tally your final score using the following checklist:

  1. Add the Victory Point value of all Monsters in your Menagerie. Monsters among your Kept Cards score zero points.
  2. Add Victory Points earned from completed Secret Goals.
  3. Each Gold Crown you possess is worth one Victory Point.
  4. Each unused Hunter Token is worth one Victory Point. Staff Hunters do not provide points.
  5. Each Season Trophy you collected is worth three Victory Points.
  6. Subtract five Victory Points for every standard Loan Card you hold, and fifteen Victory Points for every Consolidated Loan Card.

The player with the highest total Victory Points wins. If there is a tie, the tied players share the win.

Variants & Optional Rules
Two-Player Drafting

Two-player games use all standard rules with the following drafting modification. After you choose which card to capture, draw one card from your own draw stack and add it to your opponent's hand. Then, choose one Carnival Card in your hand to discard from the game before passing the stack to your opponent. When the draw stacks run out, discard one final time and pass the remaining card in your hand to your opponent.

Simplified Play

For new players, remove all Secret Goal Cards from the deck before play. Also remove all Staff Cards and Event Cards except those that award Hunters, Hunter Tokens, or Gold Crowns. This modification allows players to focus on the core drafting mechanics before engaging with deeper strategy elements.
