Setup
Board Assembly
  1. Assemble the spaceship board using the three fixed modules: Cargo Bay, Command, and Corridor.
  2. Place the three variable modules in any of the three remaining positions, with either side face up.
Component Placement
  1. Place the six Artifacts (valued 5 to 30) face up on the corresponding spaces in the Command module. If playing with fewer than four players, shuffle the Artifacts face down and randomly exclude one for three players or two for two players before placing the rest.
  2. Shuffle the Major Secrets face down and place one at random on each Major Secret space.
  3. Shuffle the Minor Secrets face down and place two at random on each Minor Secret space. Return any extra Major or Minor Secrets to the box unseen.
  4. Place the Market Board next to the spaceship and arrange the Market Items on it: two each of Contraband, Master Key, Med Kit, and TelePass. If playing with two players, place only one of each item.
  5. Place the four Escape Pod tokens in the Cargo Bay module.
  6. Create a Bank containing the five Power Crystals, one Command Code token for each player, and all Credit tokens (valued 1, 5, and 10).
  7. Prepare the Rage Track by placing the four Bounty Hunter (red) cubes on three different spaces: one on each of the first two spaces of the red zone, and two on the plaid space.
  8. Place the Blockade token on the last space of the red zone.
  9. Place the Boss Marker on the space corresponding to the number of players.
  10. Place the 24 black boss cubes in the Boss Bag and set the bag next to the game board.
  11. Place the GØB-L1N enemy card in Reserve, along with the piles of FAZR, Boldly Go, and Memory Core cards.
  12. Shuffle the Adventure Deck and deal six cards face up to form the Adventure Row. If any dealt cards show the Boss Attack symbol along the right edge, replace them with other cards until none show the symbol, then shuffle the replaced cards back into the deck. Leave room next to the deck for an Adventure discard pile.
Player Setup
  1. Each player chooses a pawn, takes 30 Clank! cubes and two Data cubes of their matching color, and places them in a personal supply.
  2. Each player takes a 10-card starting deck containing six Hacks, two Stumbles, one Access, and one Scramble.
  3. Each player places their pawn on the starting space in the Cargo Bay module, shuffles their deck, and draws five cards.
  4. Determine the first player (sneakiest player or randomly). Play proceeds clockwise. The first player places 3 Clank! cubes in the Clank! area, the second player places 2, the third player places 1, and the fourth player places 0.
Game Goal & Win Condition
  • Objectives: Hack the spaceship computer to obtain a two-part Command Code allowing entry to the Command module. Steal one Artifact from the Command module. Reach an Escape Pod in the Cargo Bay module to escape. You may carry only one Artifact at a time.
  • Scoring: At game end, tally points from your Artifact value, accumulated Credits, acquired tokens, and cards in your deck (point value shown in the top right corner of each card).
  • Tiebreaker: In the event of a tied score, the player with the most valuable Artifact wins.
Turn Structure

You start each turn with five cards in hand. You must play all cards before ending your turn, though you may play them in any order. You may take actions between playing cards or after playing all of them. You may take each action as many times as you like during your turn, provided you have the required resources. Unused resources are wasted at turn end.

When your turn ends, follow this sequence:

  1. Place all played cards into your discard pile.
  2. Draw five new cards.
  3. Refill any empty spaces in the Adventure Row with new cards from the Adventure Deck. Refilling the row may trigger a boss attack.
Resources & Actions

Skill is used to acquire new cards. Pay the Skill cost shown in the lower right corner of a card with a blue banner from the Adventure Row or a yellow banner from Reserve. Place the acquired card in your discard pile to add it to your deck. Do not immediately replace acquired cards in the Adventure Row.

Swords are used to defeat enemies. Pay the Swords cost shown in the lower right corner of a card with a red banner in the Adventure Row. Gain the reward listed in its DEFEAT text, then place the card in the Adventure discard pile. Do not immediately replace defeated enemies. You may also fight the GØB-L1N or Eradibot in Reserve multiple times per turn; these cards are not discarded when defeated.

Boots are used to move through the spaceship. Each Boot allows movement along a marked path to an adjacent space.

Market Purchases may be made when you are in a Market space. All items cost 7 Credits. Place purchased items in your play area. You may buy only one of each item type.

Movement & Navigation
Path Modifiers Standard Paths

Each Boot moves you along a marked path to an adjacent space. Spaces in different modules can be adjacent.

Special Path Icons Footprints

Paths with footprint icons require two Boots to traverse instead of one.

Enemy Icons

Moving along a path with enemy icons causes you to take one damage per icon. You may use one Sword to avoid one point of this damage, but using Swords is optional.

Lock Icons

Paths with lock icons cannot be traversed unless you possess a Master Key token.

Arrow Paths

Arrow-shaped paths are one-way and cannot be used in the opposite direction.

Special Movement Hyperlift

The Hyperlift runs through the center of the ship. From a Hyperlift space, you may move to any other Hyperlift space without using Boots. Some spaces may be blocked by locks or Rage Track progression. Using a Hyperlift causes dizziness, preventing Boot use until your next turn. You may move through a Hyperlift space using Boots without triggering dizziness.

Telepad

Telepads are not physically connected. From a Telepad space, you may move to any other Telepad space if you possess a TelePass. Using a Telepad causes dizziness, preventing Boot use until your next turn.

Teleport

Certain cards allow you to teleport to an adjacent space. Teleporting requires no Boots, bypasses enemies, locks, and one-way arrows, and does not cause dizziness. Hyperlift spaces are not considered adjacent for teleportation.

Space Effects Secret Tokens

When you first enter a space, you may take one Secret token (Major or Minor) face down, then reveal it.

Security Checkpoints

Entering a security checkpoint forces you to stop. You cannot use any more Boots this turn. You may still teleport, but Boot use remains blocked until your next turn.

Reward Spaces

Entering a reward space allows you to take the indicated Credits, healing, or Power Crystal.

Hacking & Artifacts
  1. Place Data Cube: When in a space with a data port, place one of your two Data cubes on it. Immediately resolve the shown bonus or penalty. The port becomes unusable for all players. You cannot place your second Data cube in the same module as your first.
  2. Complete Code: After placing two Data cubes in different modules, take a Command Code token from the Bank and advance the Boss Marker one space on the Rage Track. You cannot enter the Command module by any means without this token.
  3. Enter Command & Steal: Once you possess a Command Code, you may enter the Command module. If you are in a space with an Artifact, you may pick it up and place it in your play area. You may only carry one Artifact. Advancing the Boss Marker one space on the Rage Track occurs when you take an Artifact.
Combat & Health Management

Track health using your colored cubes on the Health Meter, starting from the left.

Damage Sources Boss Attacks

Place the cubes drawn from the Boss Bag on your meter. If a red cube is drawn, use a cube from your personal supply instead.

Enemy Paths

If you choose to take damage while moving through enemies without using Swords, place cubes from your personal supply on your meter. You cannot choose to take damage if it would completely fill your Health Meter.

Healing

When an effect heals you, remove one of your colored cubes from the Health Meter and return it to your personal supply. These cubes can be used for Clank! later.

Running Out of Clank!

If your personal supply is empty, you cannot choose to take damage from enemy paths. You also cannot be forced to add Clank! cubes. If a red cube is drawn during a boss attack and you have no personal cubes, use a set-aside black cube to mark the damage.

Lord Eradikus & The Rage Track
Clank! Management

Add Clank! cubes to the Clank! area when card effects require it. Negative Clank! removes cubes from the area or prevents future Clank! addition later in the turn. Any leftover negative Clank! is lost when your turn ends.

Boss Attack Resolution
  1. Place all cubes from the Clank! area into the Boss Bag.
  2. Shake the bag and draw a number of cubes equal to the number shown on the Boss Marker's current space.
  3. Set aside any black cubes. Each player-colored cube drawn deals one damage to that player.
  4. Undrawn cubes remain in the bag for future attacks.
Rage Track Milestones Progression Triggers

Advance the Boss Marker one space when a Command Code is completed, an Artifact is picked up, or an Archive is discovered.

Bounty Hunters

When the Boss Marker reaches a space with red cubes, take those cubes and place them in the Clank! area. If a red cube is drawn during an attack, every player takes one damage, and the red cube returns to the Clank! area for the next attack.

Blockade

When the Boss Marker reaches the last space in the red zone, take the Blockade token and place it in the Cargo Bay module. This severs the Hyperlift connection to the last space in the Cargo Bay for the remainder of the game.

Card Keywords & Edge Cases

Acquire: The effect resolves once when you acquire the card from the Adventure Row, not when you play it later.

Arrive: The effect resolves when the card is revealed, before any boss attack triggered by refilling the Adventure Row.

Danger: Each Danger card in the Adventure Row adds one extra cube drawn during each boss attack.

Discard: You may only discard unplayed cards from your hand. The discarded card has no effect. You must actually discard a card to receive a benefit.

Emptying Adventure Deck: If the Adventure Deck is exhausted and you cannot fill all six Adventure Row slots at the end of a turn, all players still aboard are knocked out immediately and the game ends.

Limited Components: Most components are limited. Credits are unlimited; substitute them with any suitable tokens if they run out.

Order of Card Plays: Card effects that depend on items or actions during your turn apply regardless of the order you play your cards.

Play Area: Played cards are placed in your play area to track turn usage. These cards are not included when reshuffling your discard pile.

Power Crystal: Some reward spaces grant a Power Crystal. Cards with Power Crystal bonuses function like faction bonuses. You only receive the bonus once per turn, regardless of how many Power Crystals you possess.

Factions: Cards display Outlaw, Resistance, or Science icons. To use faction text on a card, you must play a different card of that same faction during your turn. You may use the text only once per turn.

Trash: Trashing removes a card from the game entirely. Choose a card from the specified location and return it to the box at the end of your turn. Benefits for trashing "any cards this turn" trigger only once per turn. You may trash the card itself to receive the benefit.

Token & Item Reference

Tokens kept until used are returned to the box after activation. Tokens kept until game end remain in your play area for scoring.

Major Secrets

Mega Health Boost: Heal 2 damage during your turn. Return to box after use.

5 Credit Chip: Counts as 5 Credits. Keep until game end or spend normally.

Flash of Brilliance: Immediately draw three cards. Return to box after use.

Spaceship Plans: Worth 7 points at game end. Not an Artifact.

The Sixth Crystal: Counts as a Power Crystal. Worth 3 points at game end for every Power Crystal you have, including this token.

Sonic Grenade: Each opponent immediately makes +3 Clank!. Return to box after use.

Minor Secrets

Health Boost: Heal 1 damage during your turn. Return to box after use.

Speed Boost: Gain 1 Boot during your turn. Return to box after use.

Combat Boost: Gain 2 Swords during your turn. Return to box after use.

2 Credit Chip: Counts as 2 Credits. Keep until game end or spend normally.

MultiPass: Use all faction-related text on your cards during this turn. Does not work for text requiring a Power Crystal or Contraband. Return to box after use.

Vaporizer: At the end of this turn, trash a card from your discard pile or play area. Return both the card and this token to the box.

Archive: Worth 3 points at game end. Advance the Boss Marker one space on the Rage Track.

Market Items

Master Key: Allows movement through spaces separated by a lock icon. Worth 5 points at game end.

TelePass: Allows use of Telepads to move through the spaceship. Worth 5 points at game end.

Med Kit: Immediately heal 2 damage upon purchase. Worth 5 points at game end.

Contraband: Allows activation of card bonuses requiring Contraband. Worth 10 points at game end.

Game End & Scoring
Escape & Knockout Conditions

If your Health Meter is completely filled, you are knocked out. If you have an Artifact and are in the Cargo Bay module, you are rescued and may count your points. If you lack an Artifact or are not in the Cargo Bay, you lose the game.

You may escape by reaching an Escape Pod in the Cargo Bay while carrying an Artifact. Remove the pod, your pawn, and all your Clank! cubes from the board. You gain 20 additional points. You cannot escape without an Artifact.

Inactive Player Resolution

Players who escape or are knocked out no longer take normal turns, add Clank!, or take damage from the Boss Bag. Cards affecting all players do not affect them. When their turn would occur, they place all Clank! cubes in the Boss Bag, shake it, and draw exactly four cubes (six cubes in a two-player game). Ignore the Boss Marker position and Danger cards. Apply damage normally.

Game End Trigger

The game ends when all players have escaped or been knocked out.

Final Scoring Checklist
  • Add the point value of your Artifact.
  • Add points equal to your accumulated Credits.
  • Add points from any acquired tokens.
  • Add points from cards in your deck (top right corner value).
  • The player with the most points wins. Ties are broken by the most valuable Artifact.
