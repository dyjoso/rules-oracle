Setup

Place the gameboard in the center of the table with the cooperative side face up. Identify the cooperative icon around the globe on the board.

  1. Each player chooses a color and takes the matching player board, a 2-piece player aid, and all wooden pieces in that color: Infrastructure tokens, discs, Control markers, Control cubes, and an Action marker.
  2. Stack 3 discs on the Executive action stack space (marked "3") of your player board.
  3. Place the pentagonal Action marker on the Main action space (marked "1") of your player board.
  4. Take 1 scientist of your color. Place the remaining 3 scientists in the recruitment pool.
  5. Take 2 Carbon Emission Permits (CEPs) and 2 Tech cubes.
  6. The player who most recently planted a tree takes the Start Player token and goes first.
  7. Place the Cooperative Score marker on space 0 of the score track on the gameboard.
  8. Place the Decade marker on the first decade (2010).
  9. Place the Phase marker on the leftmost space of the Phase track.
  10. Create a bank outside the board. Distribute money in clockwise order starting with the Start Player: 1st player receives $3, 2nd and 3rd players receive $4, 4th player receives $5. Money is public information.
  11. Place the remaining Tech cubes on their designated space on the gameboard to form the general pool. All components except money are limited to the existing amount.
  12. Separate projects by type and place them on their appropriate spaces on the board with the proposed side up. Sort with level-1 projects on top and level-2 projects on the bottom.
  13. Separate green power plants by type and place each type near the board next to the matching project tiles.
  14. Shuffle the Energy Summit tiles to form a face-down deck. Place 1 face-up Energy Summit tile in each designated space on the board.
  15. Place 1 random Regional Agenda tile on each region's space. Return the remaining tiles to the box.
  16. Shuffle the Environmental Goal tiles in the bag. Fill the 21 goal spaces as follows: place 8 random Power Plant goal tiles (green) face up in the first group, 5 random Knowledge goal tiles (pink) face up in the second group, and 8 random Summit goal tiles (blue) face up in the third group. Return the bag to the box.
  17. Shuffle both UN Goal card decks separately. Create a face-up display near the board. The number of cards in the display depends on the player count per the matching Configuration card. Return the unused Configuration card to the box.
  18. Give 2 secret Cooperative Private Goal cards to each player. Return the rest to the box. Players may not discuss cards in their hands during the game.
  19. Shuffle all Lobbyist cards and give 5 to each player. Return the rest to the box. Players may not discuss cards in their hands during the game.
  20. For a 3-player game, return 6 Gas ("20") Fossil Fuel Power Plant tiles to the box. Shuffle the remaining Fossil Fuel Power Plant tiles and form face-down stacks next to the board. Place the Global CO2 Pollution Level marker on the 400 ppm space of the CO2 track. Place 1 CEP in each marked space in every region. Pile the remaining CEPs near the board as a CEP bank. Place 2 CEPs in the middle of the CEP Market. Place the CEP Price marker on the "3" space of the CEP Market.
Game Flow & Phase Order

A cooperative game lasts 4 decades. Each decade is divided into 5 phases played in the following order:

  1. Operations phase
  2. Income phase
  3. Environmental Goals phase
  4. Energy Supply phase
  5. End of Decade phase

Advance the Phase marker along the Phase track after completing each phase. The game ends after the 4th decade unless a loss condition is triggered earlier. See Game End & Scoring for resolution steps.

Player Turn & Actions

The Operations phase consists of multiple rounds. The number of rounds depends on player count: 1–2 players play 4 rounds, 3 players play 3 rounds, and 4 players play 2 rounds. Starting with the Start Player and proceeding clockwise, every player takes 1 turn per round.

On your turn, you must take exactly 1 Main action. You may take any number of Executive actions, but you may only take each Executive action once per turn. You may perform actions in any order. At the end of your turn, check whether any Energy Summits have been completed, then reset the Action markers on your player board before the next player begins.

Executive Actions

You may perform the following Executive actions during your turn. Each may be taken only once per turn.

Move a Scientist

Move 1 of your scientists according to the movement rules. See Scientists & Knowledge Tracks for detailed movement paths and knowledge gain triggers.

Visit the CEP Market

Buy or sell CEPs according to market rules. See CEP Market & CO2 Pollution for pricing, restocking, and transaction procedures.

Play a Lobbyist Card or Claim a UN Goal Card

Play 1 Lobbyist card from your hand or claim 1 UN Goal card from the display if requirements are met. See Card & Tile Reference Tables for card effects and claim conditions.

Main Actions

You must perform exactly 1 Main action during your turn.

Propose a Project

Place a project tile in a region according to project rules. See Core Mechanics & Reference Rules for knowledge and infrastructure interactions.

Prepare Infrastructure

Place an Infrastructure token in a region. See Region Control & Infrastructure for placement rules and scientist movement interactions.

Build a Power Plant

Construct a power plant in a region. See Core Mechanics & Reference Rules for knowledge requirements, Tech cube costs, and region control effects.

Phase Details
Income Phase

Move the Phase marker to the Income phase space.

Income Distribution

In turn order, resolve income for each energy source. Players with discs in the two stacks furthest along a knowledge track receive income. In a 2-player game, only the player in the furthest stack receives income. Each eligible player receives income as indicated by the column. When receiving income, choose how much to take as money and how much as victory points. Money cannot be shared between players.

Victory Point Management

You may spend victory points required by later phases before choosing your income split to simplify calculations. See Environmental Goals / Events Phase and Energy Supply Phase for spending requirements.

Environmental Goals / Events Phase

Move the Phase marker to the Environmental Goals phase space.

Goal Resolution

Flip any achieved Environmental Goal tiles face down. For each remaining face-up Environmental Goal tile, the group loses victory points equal to the current decade value: 2010 costs 1 point, 2020 costs 2 points, 2030 costs 3 points, and 2040 costs 4 points.

Loss Condition Check

If the group's victory point total falls below zero at the end of this phase, the game ends and all players lose. You may flip Environmental Goal tiles face down immediately upon achievement to simplify tracking.

Energy Supply Phase

Move the Phase marker to the Energy Supply phase space.

Fossil Fuel Placement

For each region whose energy requirements are not met by green power plants, place 1 random Fossil Fuel Power Plant tile from the face-down stacks. The region must pay 1 CEP to the bank. If the region cannot pay a CEP, the controlling player must pay a fine. See CEP Market & CO2 Pollution for fine resolution.

CO2 Pollution Tracking

Advance the Global CO2 Pollution Level marker on the CO2 track by the total amount depicted on the newly placed Fossil Fuel Power Plant tiles.

Pollution Reduction

After pollution increases, you may reduce the CO2 pollution level by spending victory points. You must reduce pollution to 500 ppm or lower. The cost per 10 ppm reduction depends on the decade: 2010 costs 1 point, 2020 costs 2 points, 2030 costs 3 points, and 2040 costs 4 points.

Loss Condition Check

If the group's victory point total falls below zero or the CO2 pollution level exceeds 500 ppm at the end of this phase, the game ends and all players lose.

End of Decade Phase

Move the Phase marker to the End of Decade phase space.

Cleanup Steps

Advance the Decade marker to the next decade. Pass the Start Player token to the player on the right (counterclockwise). Check game end conditions.

3-Player Cooperative Adjustment

Place the 3-Player Co-Op token on the 4th decade space during setup. When the 4th decade begins, flip and move the token to cover the 3rd Operations space on the Phase track. Play only 2 Operations rounds in the final decade.

Core Mechanics & Reference Rules
CEP Market & CO2 Pollution Market Transactions

Every CEP that enters play is taken from the CEP Market. When the CEP Market empties, move 2 CEPs from the CEP bank to the market and increase the CEP Price marker by 1. Every CEP paid or sold goes to the CEP bank.

Paying CEPs

When you need to pay a CEP, you may take it from any region you control or from your hand. If you do not have any CEPs to pay, you cannot take the action. You may only sell a CEP from your hand. You cannot sell a CEP from a region, even if you control it.

Fines

If you must pay a fine, take 1 CEP from the CEP Market in exchange for victory points equal to the current market price.

Knowledge Track CEP Movement

Certain spaces on the knowledge tracks allow you to move 1 CEP from the CEP Market to a region of your choice, provided the region's CEP spaces are not full.

Scientists & Knowledge Tracks Scientist Movement

Your scientist may move from your player board to any unoccupied proposed project or any unoccupied Lobbyist card played by any player. From a proposed project, your scientist may move to any unoccupied empty proposed project, any unoccupied Lobbyist card, any unoccupied Energy Summit talk of the same type, or back to your player board.

Knowledge Gain Triggers

You earn 1 point of knowledge in an energy type of your choice when you recruit a scientist or reach the generic energy icon on a knowledge track. You earn 1 point of knowledge in a specific energy type when you propose a project to a region, 1 of your scientists leaves a proposed project for any reason, or you reach an energy type icon on the knowledge track. When a Summit is completed at the end of a turn, you earn 2 points of knowledge in any energy type matching the summit topics for each of your participating scientists.

Knowledge Benefits

Players with discs in the 1st and 2nd stacks of each energy type receive money and/or victory points during the Income phase. You must have sufficient knowledge in an energy source to build a matching power plant. Knowledge serves as a tiebreaker when determining region control. When your knowledge disc covers specific spaces on the track, you gain the indicated bonus.

Region Control & Infrastructure Control Determination

Control of a region's energy supply is checked only when a power plant is built.

Control Effects

When you control a region, you may spend its CEPs as your own, though you cannot sell them. If the region builds a Fossil Fuel Power Plant, you must pay the required CEP or pay a fine. Some goal cards require region control to be achieved.

Infrastructure Preparation

If you have your own scientist on a project and decide to prepare Infrastructure in that region, you do not need to use your Executive action to move the scientist out. The movement is mandatory and free. You may still use your Executive action to move the same or a different scientist.

Game End & Scoring

The cooperative game ends at the conclusion of the 4th decade.

Loss Conditions

The group loses if the group does not finish the 4th decade, any player has completed neither of their Cooperative Private Goal cards, or more than 3 UN Goal cards remain unclaimed.

Victory Resolution

If no loss condition is met, the group wins. Refer to the Configuration card to determine your victory epithet based on final performance.

Competitive Variant

Apply the following changes for competitive play. All base rules remain unchanged unless explicitly modified here.

Setup Changes

Use the competitive side of the board. Place 1 disc of your color on space 0 of the score track to track individual victory points. Replace Cooperative Private Goal cards with Company Private Goal cards. Each player receives 2 random secret Company Private Goal cards, immediately chooses 1, and discards the other to the box. Remove cards marked with the 2-player symbol in a 2-player game. Place the Global CO2 Pollution Level marker on 50 ppm. Replace Environmental Goal tiles with Event tiles. Shuffle all Event tiles into a face-down deck on the orange circle. Reveal the top tile to the white circle, then flip a new tile from the deck so 2 events are visible. Create a display of 10 face-up UN Goal cards near the board: 5 cards depicting 2 energy types from one deck, and 5 cards depicting 3 energy types from the other deck.

Phase Order & Structure

A competitive game lasts 5 decades. The Environmental Goals phase is replaced by an Events phase. The Energy Supply phase becomes the first phase of each decade. The phase order is: 1. Energy Supply phase, 2. Operations phase, 3. Income phase, 4. Events phase, 5. End of Decade phase. If the CO2 pollution level exceeds 500 ppm at any time, all players immediately lose.

Action & Phase Modifications

During your turn, you may discard your Company Private Goal card face-down to receive $8. In the first decade Energy Supply phase, no green power plants exist on the board. All regions receive 1 random Fossil Fuel Power Plant tile, each region pays 1 CEP to the bank, and the CO2 marker advances by the total amount depicted. During the Energy Supply phase, you cannot spend victory points to reduce pollution. Fines are deducted from the specific player responsible. If the CO2 level exceeds 500 ppm, all players lose immediately. When claiming a UN Goal card, place it next to your player board. You receive no immediate points; scoring occurs at game end. When building a power plant, you may use 1 Tech cube from the region to help cover the cost if the region has any. During the Income phase, each player earns their own points in turn order.

Events Phase Resolution

Move the Phase marker to the Events phase space. Discard 1 Tech cube from each region that has any to the bank. Trigger the Event tile on the white circle. Each player without a power plant or Infrastructure supporting a power plant in the affected region must contribute 1 Tech cube to the region and pay 1 CEP on its behalf, or pay a fine. Players unable to provide a Tech cube lose 2 victory points. You cannot choose to keep the Tech cube and lose points instead. If the CO2 pollution level is at or above 400 ppm, also trigger the face-up Event tile in the orange circle using the same procedure. Discard triggered Event tiles. Reveal the top tile from the deck to the white circle, then flip the next deck tile face up so 2 events remain visible.

Scoring & End Game

If the CO2 level exceeds 500 ppm, all players lose immediately. Otherwise, the game ends after the 5th decade. Perform final scoring in the following order: collect all CEPs from regions whose energy supplies you control. Sell all your CEPs at the current CEP Market price without reducing the price. If you have not discarded your Company Goal card, discard it now for $8 or reveal it to gain the indicated victory points. Gain 1 victory point for each $2 you hold. The player with the most Tech cubes gains 3 victory points. Tied players each receive the 3 victory points. Gain victory points for claimed UN Goal cards: 1 card grants 6 points, 2 cards grant 10 points, 3 cards grant 15 points, and 4 or more cards grant 21 points. The player with the most victory points wins. Tiebreakers are applied in order: most UN Goal cards, most green power plants built, most Infrastructures prepared, most money. If a tie remains, tied players share the victory.

Lobbyist Draft Variant

Once familiar with the game, draft your initial hand of Lobbyist cards. Choose 2 of your 5 Lobbyist cards and pass the remaining 3 to the player on your right. From the 3 received, take 1 and pass 2 to the right. From the 2 received, take 1 and pass 1 to the right.
