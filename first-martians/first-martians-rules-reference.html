Quick Reference Guide: First Martians

Table-side reference for gameplay mechanics, phase order, and component management. This document is designed for quick lookup during play and does not replace the full rulebook.

Core Concepts & Components

Astronauts are represented by a player ID board and two Action Pawns of your color. The ID board displays your astronaut's profession, base skill, and health track. Insert one red marker in the portrait slot to track wounds. Each astronaut can sustain five wounds before dying. If any astronaut dies, the mission is immediately lost.

Skills allow you to spend Morale tokens to perform a unique feat, such as rerolling a die or gaining a sample. Each skill can only be used once per round. Your base skill is printed on your ID board and is available in every game. Additionally, each astronaut has three Skill cards. In standalone missions, these are available from the start. In campaigns, you must unlock them first.

The App tracks objectives, prompts phase transitions, manages mission logs, and provides event text. It informs you when the mission ends and handles save/load states for campaigns.

The Goal of the Game is to meet all mandatory victory conditions for a given mission within the specified number of rounds (Sols) while keeping all astronauts alive. Some missions include a Threshold value. If provided, meeting that threshold is always mandatory. You must be at or below the threshold value when the mission ends to win.

Setup Checklist Board & Tracks

Place the board in the center of the play area. Place one white marker on the 0 space of the Morale track. Place one white marker on the 00 space of the Stress track. Place one white marker on the top space of the Round Order track.

Facilities & Supplies

Place all 13 Facility tiles on their respective spaces. Insert white markers in the top slots of the Oxygenator and Solar Panel productivity tracks. Insert one black marker in the Farm Greenhouses column for each astronaut in the game, then add one white marker next to each built greenhouse. Insert one green marker in each slot of the status markers column for every facility. Place one red marker on the 0 space of each Block's Malfunction track. Place the oxygen, energy, and food trackers on the appropriate spaces of the General Supply track as directed by the app.

Decks & Tokens

Separate the ROI tiles by size into three face-down stacks. Shuffle each stack and place them near the board. Separate the Shutdown tiles by their backs into two face-down stacks. Shuffle each stack and place them near the board. Shuffle all Condition tokens into one face-down stack. Place the remaining tiles, tokens, markers, and dice within easy reach as the general pool.

Player Boards & Pawns

Each player chooses an astronaut (Scientist, Geologist, Engineer, or Medic). Take the accompanying ID board and two action pawns. Insert one red marker in the slot on your astronaut's portrait. Add your chosen astronauts to the Flight Crew Debriefing panel. For fewer than four players, begin with the Auto-scanner Upgrade tile already in play and discard its card. If playing with three astronauts, choose one AOM to help. If playing with two, choose two AOMs. If playing solo, use two chosen astronauts and may use up to two AOMs per round.

App & Mission Selection

Launch the app and select your mission type and difficulty level. Place the chosen mission sheet near the board. Put one white marker on the leftmost space of the Sol counter. Decide who the First Player will be for the first round. Mission sheets and app instructions override general setup rules.

The Round Loop

Each round consists of six phases resolved in the following order. Advance the Sol Counter and pass the First Player token clockwise after the Clean-Up phase.

  1. Events: Advance one Malfunction track, resolve any Adventure or Follow-Up events, then resolve the current Mission Event.
  2. Morale: The First Player gains or discards Morale tokens based on the current Morale level.
  3. Production: Calculate resource production and consumption, resolve shortages, increase Stress, and apply Working Block penalties.
  4. Actions: Declare and resolve actions in a fixed order using assigned pawns.
  5. Malfunction: Assemble and roll Malfunction dice, advance tracks, and resolve triggered cards.
  6. Clean-Up: Rotate facility tokens, update the Cargo Bay, reset tracks, pass the First Player token, and advance the Sol Counter.
Phase I: Events Malfunction Advance

Before resolving the round's event, advance one Malfunction track by one space as directed by the app. Move the corresponding marker up one space and resolve any immediate consequences.

Adventure & Follow-Up Events

Adventure and Follow-Up events always resolve before the regular Mission Event. Adventure events are triggered by past actions or in-game effects. Follow-Up events are unique, player-specific consequences that may occur randomly based on past mission history. Resolve these events by following the app instructions.

Mission Events

The First Player reads the story text and event description aloud. Resolve the event's effects immediately. The event may offer an Event action that can be taken this round to prevent delayed consequences. If you do not take the action, the event remains in the Mission Log for three consecutive rounds. After three rounds, or if consequences occur, the app discards the event. You can no longer take its action, but you are also protected from its delayed consequences.

Delayed Consequences & Tracking

When deciding whether to take an Event action, remember that unattended events persist in the Mission Log. To take a past Event action, place pawns and resources on the Past Event Action space. Click Completed in the app upon success. Event actions can only be taken once unless stated otherwise.

Phase II: Morale Track Range & Limits

The Morale track ranges from -2 to +3. Morale can never exceed these limits. If the group's Morale is at 0, nothing happens during this phase.

Token Gain & Loss Math

The First Player gains or discards Morale tokens equal to the current Morale value. For example, gain two tokens at +2 or discard one token at -1.

Wound Conversion Rule

If the First Player must discard more Morale tokens than they possess, discard all available tokens, then suffer one wound for each missing token. Players cannot choose to suffer wounds instead of discarding Morale tokens.

First Player Duties

The First Player resolves and experiences all Morale phase effects, resolves disputes among players, and is the target of specific in-game effects that reference the First Player.

Phase III: Production Oxygen & Energy

Oxygenators produce oxygen (min 1, max 4 each) and Solar Panels produce energy (min 1, max 5 each). Add the values from both facilities to determine total production. Facilities requiring constant supply are marked with the appropriate icon. If production exceeds consumption, add half of the surplus (rounded up) to the General Supply. Green or red status markers do not indicate whether supply should be provided, only whether the supplied resource can be used.

Food & Farm

If a new seed is planted, move its marker up one space on the Growth track. If it reaches space #6, move it to an empty greenhouse. Produce one food portion for each fully grown plant. Each astronaut must eat one food portion. If production is insufficient, use reserves from the General Supply. If reserves are also insufficient, decide who will not eat. Each astronaut that does not eat suffers one wound per missing portion. If food production exceeds consumption, add half of the surplus (rounded up) to the General Supply.

Resource Shortages & Shutdowns

If oxygen or energy production is less than required, place Shutdown tiles face down on facilities until production matches consumption. You cannot shut down facilities to gain reserves or prevent malfunctions. Shut down facilities are immune to effects targeting them. While a Shutdown tile is on a facility, resolve its +2 Stress effect each round. To remove a Shutdown tile, place one pawn on it during the Action phase, then flip it at the end of the Action phase to resolve its effect immediately. Discarded Shutdown tiles return to their stack. Note that a facility with an oxygen Shutdown tile may still consume energy, and a facility with an energy Shutdown tile may still consume oxygen.

Stress & Working Block Penalties

Increase the Stress track by one space for each astronaut in the game, plus any additional effects. If the marker reaches or passes space #18, each astronaut suffers one wound, Morale decreases by one, and the Stress track resets to #00. Resolve active penalties from red ⚙ status markers in the Working Block. Roll the green Wound die. If you roll a wound symbol, place a Blocked token on the Froggy rover space. Remove one chosen sample from any ROI. Increase Stress by one. Place the Working Block penalty token on the Working Bay ! space.

Phase IV: Actions Declaration & Pawn Assignment

Players cooperate to declare actions. You must use both of your action pawns unless an effect states otherwise. You may use additional pawns if available. Assign pawns by placing them on the board, mission sheet, or specific locations as indicated by the action type. The pawn on top of a stack takes the action and suffers consequences; other pawns only assist.

Costs & Penalties

An action cost is what you must pay to declare the action. A penalty is the consequence of resolving it. If you cannot pay the cost or handle the penalty, you cannot take the action. Supplies from the General Supply paid as a cost are spent even if the action fails. Tokens on the ! space of a facility add costs/penalties only for that facility. Tokens on the x space of a rover add costs/penalties for that rover.

Resolution Order & Success Handling

Resolve actions in this fixed order: Events, A Quiet Place, Crew Quarters, Med Lab, Control Center, Garage Hall, Probe Bay, Lab, Working Bay. If an action is automatically successful (using the required base pawns), apply the effect and take back pawns. If unsuccessful, take back pawns, return unspent resources, and do not apply the effect. The app will prompt you to mark actions as completed.

Phase V: Malfunction Dice Pool Assembly

Consult the mission sheet and the Malfunction box to assemble the dice pool. A red marker in the box means add that die; a green marker means do not add it.

Rolling & Penalty Addition

Roll all dice in the pool. For each Block, add +1 to the corresponding die result for each active penalty in that Block. If a die was not rolled, the base result is 0, but you still add all active penalties from that Block.

Track Advancement

Move the red markers on the corresponding Malfunction tracks up by the total rolled value.

Card Resolution & Keywords

If a track reaches or passes 5, draw one Malfunction card from the corresponding deck, resolve its effect, place it in the discard pile, and reset the track to 0. Change the next Block's marker in the Malfunction box to red in this sequence: Working Block → Living Block → System Block → Working Block. If a deck runs out, shuffle its discard pile to form a new deck. If a card has a keyword requirement, draw cards one by one until you find a matching keyword, resolve it, and discard the others.

Phase VI: Clean-Up Facility Tokens & Cargo Bay

In each facility, discard tokens from the ! space. Move the topmost token from the >> space to the ! space. Move everything from the Pending area of the Cargo Bay to the Available area. Install any Upgrade tiles to their respective facilities.

Round Order & Sol Counter

Move the white marker on the Round Order track back to the top space. Move the white marker to the next space on the Sol counter.

Skills & First Player

Discard all black markers from skills used this round. Flip any used Skill cards face up. Pass the First Player token clockwise to the next player.

App Prompt & Modifier Tokens

The First Player clicks Next Round in the app. The app will indicate if the mission has ended. Modifier tokens trigger in-game effects and are placed per instruction. They do not act retroactively on declared actions but apply to all future actions until discarded at the end of the phase.

Action & Pawn Reference Action List & Requirements
  1. Events: Facility: Current/Past Event Action space. Base Pawns: As specified by app. Dice: None. Effect: Prevent delayed consequences, gain rewards. Special Rules: Can only be taken once per event.
  2. A Quiet Place (Rest): Facility: A Quiet Place. Base Pawns: 1. Dice: None. Effect: Gain 1 Morale token. Special Rules: No dice or Adventures can be forced here.
  3. Crew Quarters (Unwind): Facility: Crew Quarters. Base Pawns: 1. Dice: Gray. Effect: Increase Morale by 1 or reduce Stress by 6. Special Rules: None.
  4. Med Lab (Heal): Facility: Med Lab. Base Pawns: 1 + 1 Medic pawn (or 1 with Auto-scanner). Dice: Blue. Effect: Heal 1 wound or 1 condition. Special Rules: Medic needs only 1 pawn to heal herself.
  5. Control Center (Manage): Facility: Control Center. Base Pawns: 1. Dice: Brown. Effect: Move a red marker on a chosen Malfunction track down 1 space. Special Rules: Can target different tracks each round.
  6. Garage Hall (Explore): Facility: Garage Hall / Map spaces. Base Pawns: 1 (roll) or 2 (auto). Dice: Green. Effect: Place new ROI tile, add samples/POI tokens. Special Rules: Add pawns for distance. Terrain effects apply in future rounds only.
  7. Probe Bay (Gather): Facility: Probe Bay / ROI spaces. Base Pawns: 1 (roll) or 2 (auto). Dice: Gray. Effect: Move gathered item to Pending Cargo Bay. Special Rules: Add pawns for distance. Cannot gather from unexplored ROIs.
  8. Lab (Research & Planting): Facility: Lab / Farm. Base Pawns: 1 (roll) or 2 (auto). Dice: Blue. Effect: Examine/discover samples or plant seed. Special Rules: Add pawns for distance. Research tracks are unlimited. Planting limited to once per round.
  9. Working Bay (Build): Facility: Working Bay / Depot / Upgrade cards. Base Pawns: 1 (roll) or 2 (auto). Dice: Brown. Effect: Repair equipment, install upgrades, repair rovers. Special Rules: Add pawns for distance. Repairs operational at end of Action phase.
AOMs & Rovers

AOMs provide additional color-coded pawns for specific actions. They can assist or act alone. The player using an AOM takes its card as operator. AOMs can combine with rovers. Actions with no type icon can still use AOMs. Rovers require at least one pawn to operate. Place the rover miniature with declared pawns. Only the top pawn resolves consequences; others assist. Froggy counts as 2 additional pawns for Explore actions. Scorpio provides 1 additional pawn for Explore/Gather/Research/Build actions outside the HUB. Return rovers to the Depot after use. Blocked rovers cannot be used until repaired.

Mission Actions & Pawn Requirements

Mission actions follow sheet instructions and apply all modifiers affecting that action type. Base pawn numbers are modified by in-game effects. Use 1 pawn to roll dice, or 2 pawns for automatic success. Additional pawns may be required for distance or penalty tokens.

Dice, Tracks & Modifiers Action Dice Results

Action dice come in color-coded sets of three. The Success die (check) applies the action effect on success, or grants 2 Morale tokens on failure. The Wound die (crosshair) causes 1 wound or nothing. The Adventure die (?) triggers an Adventure or nothing. Results are independent. Resolve all die effects before carrying on with the action.

Modifier Tokens

Modifier tokens trigger effects like extra pawns or rerolls. Place them where instructed. They do not apply retroactively to declared actions. They remain active until discarded at the end of the phase.

Track Limits & Resets

The Morale track is capped between -2 and +3. The Stress track resets to #00 after reaching #18. Malfunction tracks reset to 0 after reaching 5. The Round Order track resets to the top space during Clean-Up.

Condition Effects

Anemia: Astronaut eats 2 food portions per round. Suffers 1 wound per missing portion. Anxiety Attack: Decrease group Morale by 1 immediately, then shuffle token back. Cardiac Disorder: One pawn can only be used to Heal this condition. The other pawn is unrestricted. Concentration Difficulties: If taking an action, resolve an Adventure. If assisting, the top pawn's owner resolves an Adventure. Migraine: Cannot use skills, but can spend Morale tokens for other purposes. Poisoning: Suffer twice as many wounds whenever wounds are taken.

General Supply & Health

The General Supply tracks oxygen, energy, and food reserves. Adjust trackers as resources are gained or spent. Astronaut Health is tracked on the ID board. The marker moves right for wounds. An astronaut dies only if the marker must move beyond the ID board.

End Game & Objectives Victory Conditions

You win by completing all mandatory mission objectives before the Sol Counter runs out. If a mission has a Threshold, you must be at or below that value when the mission ends. The app will declare victory upon meeting conditions.

Defeat Conditions

You lose if at least one astronaut dies, if the Sol Counter runs out before mandatory objectives are complete, or if you fail to meet the Threshold value at mission end.

Objective Tokens & Thresholds

Objective tokens track progress. Red tokens are mandatory, orange are optional, black are for other uses. Place tokens where the mission sheet directs, or in the Pending Cargo Bay if unspecified. Tokens can be replaced with samples or spare parts anytime unless instructed otherwise. Token numbers do not indicate hierarchy. Threshold values are mandatory limits on broken parts or other metrics that must be met to win.

Campaign Mode Adjustments Setup Differences & State Carryover

Campaigns consist of five linked missions. Setup relies on the end state of the previous mission. Do not discard conditions, do not repair facilities, keep explored areas explored, retain unlocked skills, and do not replenish supplies. Adventures with unresolved delayed consequences may still trigger.

Save & Load Process

The app records game status after successful missions. Click Begin to save. To resume, select Continue Campaign, choose a saved game, and follow app prompts to recreate the board state before starting the next mission.

Resource & Legacy Rules

Supplies and spare parts persist across missions. Plan resource usage carefully. Growing plants and unlocking skills require time and discoveries. The Welcome to Mars campaign is legacy-style and cannot be replayed. The Labyrinth of the Night allows replaying failed missions or restarting the campaign. Play with the same group to maintain narrative continuity.

Icon & Token Glossary Action Costs & Penalties

Malfunction phase: move corresponding Malfunction track marker up 1 space. Wilting: plants are dying, resolve wilting effects. No rover assistance: rovers cannot assist in Explore actions. Oxygen cost: spend 1 oxygen from General Supply to declare action. Energy cost: spend 1 energy from General Supply to declare action. Forced roll: must roll corresponding die even with enough pawns for auto-success. Working Bay penalty: place token on Working Bay ! space.

ROI Effects

Rover damage: roll green Wound die at end of Action phase when driving to/through ROI. Add Blocked token on damage. Impassable edge: cannot move directly to external ring from this ROI. Adventure trigger: resolve an Adventure when going to/through ROI. Morale decrease: decrease Morale by 1 when going to/through ROI. Wound trigger: suffer 1 wound when going to/through ROI. Extra pawn: need 1 additional pawn for any action involving this ROI. Forced die roll: roll corresponding die for action. Reroll success: reroll Success die once if rolled. Wound penalty: suffer 1 wound to take action. Rover damage (general): roll green Wound die at end of Action phase when using rover.

Facility Parts

Energy Connectors, Oxygen Filters, Hardware, CPU.

Tracking Icons

Condition, Morale, Stress, Sample, Malfunction, Morale token, Wound, General Supply resources.

Tokens & Conditions

Blocked: facility or rover unusable this round. Counter pawn: negates 1 pawn on action. Adventure trigger: resolve corresponding Adventure. Spare parts: Energy Connectors, Oxygen Filters, Hardware, CPU. Mission tokens: used for specific mission purposes. Conditions: Anemia, Anxiety Attack, Cardiac Disorder, Concentration Difficulties, Migraine, Poisoning.

Quick Rulings Actions & Pawns

When multiple effects copy the same result, only tokens add up. Other tokens, cards, costs, and penalties cannot multiply a single effect. You never resolve more than one Adventure per action and never reroll a success die more than once. If instructed to place a token on a space that already has one, ignore the instruction. If instructed to place a token on a >> space, always do so, as only the top token becomes active when moved to !.

If an action has a penalty, using more than the minimum pawns may still be beneficial. Penalties often only apply to dice rolls. Using two pawns for automatic success avoids rolling the penalty die entirely, whereas using one pawn requires rolling all three dice.

When you want to flip over a Shutdown tile, you do not take into account the costs or penalties of the facility it is on. You always need only one pawn to flip a Shutdown tile over.

Resources & Tracks

If you produce less energy or oxygen than needed but have reserves, you cannot use reserves to make up the difference. You must use Shutdown tokens to switch off facilities you cannot supply. Reserves are only used to pay costs for broken parts of available facilities.

If a Shutdown tile instructs you to add +1 to production but you are already at maximum capacity, do nothing. Shutdown tiles cannot boost production beyond maximum capacity.

Events & Malfunctions

When you draw a Complication card, check the second keyword to identify the facility. Change the corresponding green status marker to red. If that part is already broken, discard the card.

Each action taken in the Control Center during the same round can target a different Malfunction track.

Components & Setup

When Objective tokens are referred to by a range, "Objective tokens #1-3" means three tokens. "An Objective token #1-3" means one token.

If an effect allows you to redraw a card, discard the unwanted card and draw another. You must resolve the new card.

If you run out of markers or tokens, use substitutes like multiplication tiles or other available markers. Running out of components does not stop you from applying effects.

When you get a condition token for passing a health track icon, get healed, and suffer another wound, you get another condition for passing the same icon again. The rule applies every time the icon is passed.

If you have the Power Generator Upgrade installed, it only affects the penalty cost. The part remains broken and counts toward the Threshold value.

When the red marker is in the last slot of the health track, the astronaut is alive. The astronaut dies only if the marker must move beyond the ID board.
