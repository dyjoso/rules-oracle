Setup
1. Distribute Components

Select a player colour and take the matching sprinteur and rouleur rider models, their corresponding energy cards, and a player board.

2. Form Decks

Shuffle your sprinteur energy cards to create a deck, and place it face down on the designated space of your player board. Repeat this process for your rouleur energy cards. Place all sprinteur exhaustion cards face up within easy reach of all players, then do the same for the rouleur exhaustion cards.

3. Lay the Track

Select a stage card and arrange the track tiles according to the letter sequence printed at the bottom of the card. Track tiles are marked with lowercase and uppercase letters in the lower-left corner. The start and finish tiles are highlighted in yellow, and the start tile is marked with a ► symbol.

4. Place Riders

The player who most recently rode a bike (or the youngest player if tied) places their two riders one at a time in any square with a free lane behind the start line. Continue placing riders clockwise around the table until all riders are positioned.

Turn Sequence Overview

• Energy Phase: Simultaneous card draw and play for both riders.

• Movement Phase: Sequential card reveal and rider movement resolution.

• End Phase: Sequential card removal, slipstreaming chain resolution, and exhaustion assignment.

Phase 1: Energy Phase
Draw and Play Cards

All players act simultaneously. Choose one of your riders and draw four cards from their energy deck. Select one card to play face down next to the matching rider's energy deck. This card determines the rider's movement value for the round.

Conditional Drawing Rules

You cannot draw cards for your second rider before playing a card for your first rider. If your energy deck runs out of face down cards, shuffle the recycled cards and place them back on your player board face down. Continue drawing until you hold four cards. If your total energy deck contains fewer than four cards, draw all remaining cards.

Repeat for Second Rider

After completing the draw and play steps for your first rider, repeat the process for your second rider. Once all players have played a card for both riders, proceed to the Movement Phase.

Phase 2: Movement Phase
Reveal Cards

Turn all played cards face up to reveal their movement values.

Determine Movement Order

Identify the frontmost rider and resolve movement starting with that rider, proceeding sequentially to the next rider in line.

Resolve Movement

Move each rider forward the exact number of squares indicated by their played card. Riders may change lanes at no additional cost during movement.

Movement and Placement Rules

A rider landing in an empty square must always be placed in the right-hand lane. Riders may move through other riders but cannot end their movement on an occupied square. If a rider would end their movement on a fully occupied square, they must stop behind it in the first square with a free lane.

Phase 3: End Phase
1. Remove Played Cards

Remove all played cards from the game. Cards are used only once per game.

2. Apply Slipstreaming

Starting with the backmost pack of riders, scan forward along the track. If there is exactly one empty square between two packs, move the rear pack forward one square so the two packs merge. Immediately check if slipstreaming applies to this newly merged pack. Repeat this check sequentially until all packs have been evaluated. Riders may gain slipstreaming multiple times in a single round.

3. Assign Exhaustion Cards

Check each rider for exhaustion. If a rider has an empty square directly in front of them, give them a matching exhaustion card. Recycle the assigned exhaustion card immediately. Note that an empty lane in front of a rider does not trigger exhaustion.

Key Mechanics & Definitions
Board Layout

Square vs. Lane: Thick white lines divide the track into squares. Each square contains two lanes: left and right. One rider occupies one lane. If a square is empty, riders are always placed in the right lane, which is marked with a double line.

Rider Positioning

Frontmost Rider: The rider closest to the finish line in the right-hand lane.

Backmost Rider: The rider farthest from the finish line.

Pack: A group of one or more riders with no empty squares between them.

Card Management

Recycle: Place unplayed cards face up at the bottom of the matching rider's energy deck.

Card Visibility: Played cards remain face down during the Energy Phase and are revealed face up during the Movement Phase. Recycled cards are kept face up. Energy decks are kept face down. Exhaustion decks are kept face up.

Card Values: Played energy cards indicate movement distance. Exhaustion cards function as energy cards with a fixed value of 2.

Reactive Mechanics

Slipstreaming: Triggered when exactly one empty square separates two packs. The rear pack moves forward one square to merge with the front pack. This check repeats sequentially from back to front until no further merges occur.

Exhaustion: Triggered when a rider has an empty square directly in front of them. The rider receives a matching exhaustion card, which is immediately recycled.

Special Track Features: Mountains
Ascent Tiles

Ascent squares are marked with red arrow road edges. These tiles apply during the Movement Phase and End Phase.

Movement Cap

A rider starting on, moving onto, or crossing any red Ascent square can never move more than 5 squares. If a played card shows a higher value, the excess movement is wasted. If the sixth or subsequent move would place the rider into the first square of an Ascent, stop the rider on the last free lane before the Ascent begins.

Slipstreaming Restriction

During the End Phase, a rider positioned on an Ascent square cannot give or receive slipstreaming to or from other riders.

Descent Tiles

Descent squares are marked with blue arrow road edges. These tiles apply during the Movement Phase.

Movement Minimum

If a rider starts a turn on a blue Descent square, their played card always counts as a minimum value of 5. If a lower value card is played, the movement value is increased to 5. Slipstreaming is resolved normally for riders on Descent squares.

General Mountain Rules

Exhaustion cards are assigned according to the standard End Phase rules regardless of mountain terrain.

Variants & Handicaps
Alternative Stage Setups

Track tiles are double-sided to support varied layouts. Six suggested stages are provided, each with a dedicated stage card. Players may also design custom track layouts using the available tiles.

Experience-Based Handicaps

Adjust the starting energy decks during setup to balance experience levels.

New Players

Play with standard energy decks as described in the Setup phase.

Experienced Players

Add one exhaustion card to both the sprinteur and rouleur energy decks for each experienced player.

Veteran Players

Add two exhaustion cards to both the sprinteur and rouleur energy decks for each veteran player.
