Game Setup
Board Setup Main Board Placement

Place the game board in the center of the table and the sea routes board above it. Shuffle the harbourmaster tiles and place one face-up on each of the five harbourmaster spaces on the sea routes board. Return the remaining harbourmaster tiles to the box. Place the neutral building tiles face-up on the eight neutral spaces on the game board. Place the bonus tiles market token on the top dashed round space in the bonus tiles market. Place the neutral buildings overview tile next to the game board.

Market & Supply Placement

Sort the worker tiles, hazard tiles, and bonus tiles by the letters on their backs and place them into their corresponding Wellington supply bags next to the game board. Place the market sheep cards as a face-down market stack beneath the lower right of the game board. Shuffle the objective cards and place them as a face-down stack to the right of the game board. Reveal four cards from this stack and display them face-up in the general objective display. Place the deck-building supply cards on the left side of the game board. Place the bonus cards supply next to the sea routes board according to set numbers. Place coins and exchange tokens within reach of all players as the bank.

Player Setup Component Distribution

Each player chooses a player colour and places the corresponding player board in front of them. Place the player count tile and fee tile in the Phase A box of the player board, with the fee tile showing the two coins with -1 and -2. Place the ten private building tiles above the player board in ascending order of required craftsmen. All players must use the same side for each numbered tile. Place sixteen discs on the coloured disc spaces, ten storehouses on the coloured square spaces, one ship on a starting water-space on the sea routes board, one certificate marker on the top space of the certificate track, one gold marker on the second space of the gold track, and one runholder next to the player board.

Deck & Starting Resources

Shuffle the fourteen player sheep cards to form the flock deck and place it as a face-down draw stack to the left of the player board. Determine a starting player. Receive starting capital, one exchange token, and draw cards to hand according to player order. At the beginning of your very first turn, discard cards from your hand until you have only four remaining. Discarded cards go to your personal discard pile. Place one of your player discs from a disc space with white corners onto space 0 of the pathfinder track. Place your runholder onto any neutral building tile and continue directly with Phase B.

Turn Structure
Phase A: Move Runholder Movement Rules

Move your runholder from its current location along the trail to another location. Movement is measured in steps, with each location taking one step. Empty spaces are not locations. You must always move forward along the trail arrows. You cannot pass over Wellington. Multiple runholders may occupy the same location. You must move at least one step and cannot exceed your current step limit. The location where movement ends is used in Phase B.

Fee Payment Trigger

If your runholder moves to or passes over a location with a green or black hand, you must immediately pay a fee. Black hands cost one pound to the bank. Green hands cost two pounds to the player who owns the private building tile. Fee payment rules are detailed in the Fees & Movement Hands section.

Phase B: Resolve Location Location Resolution Flow

Determine the type of location reached to resolve actions. If you land on a neutral building tile or your own private building tile, you may perform the local actions of that tile or perform one single auxiliary action. If you land on an opponent's private building tile or a hazard tile, you may only perform one single auxiliary action. If you land on Wellington, you must carry out all four Wellington subphases in succession.

Local Action Rules

Most building tiles show two different local actions separated by dividers. You may perform each local action once in any order. You are not required to perform all available actions. If an action shows a cost or requirement, you must pay or meet it completely to gain the reward. Some tiles show two actions separated by a white slash; you may only perform one of those. Risk actions attached to trail spaces become part of the tile's local actions.

Auxiliary Action Rules

If you do not perform any local actions on a neutral or own building tile, you may perform one single auxiliary action instead. Auxiliary actions are found on the left side of your player board. At the start of the game, only two are available. Additional actions unlock when you clear their associated disc spaces.

Phase C: Draw to Hand Limit Hand Management

Each player starts with a hand limit of four cards, which can increase up to six. If you have fewer cards in hand than your current limit, draw cards from your draw stack to reach the limit. You may not play any deck-building or bonus cards during this phase. Hand limit increases are linked to clearing specific disc spaces.

Deck Reshuffling

When you must draw a card but have no cards left in your draw stack, pick up your discard pile, shuffle it thoroughly, and place it as your new face-down draw stack. Then continue drawing as usual.

Wellington Process
Subphase 1: Income Reveal & Calculate

Reveal as many sheep of different types as you like from your hand. Calculate their total breeding value by summing the breeding values of each revealed type. You may reveal each type only once. Ignore victory points or wool values. You cannot reveal deck-building or bonus cards that are not sheep.

Certificates & Payment

Increase the total breeding value by adding certificates. Temporary certificates are available based on your certificate marker position; move the marker backward to use them. Permanent certificates are cleared disc spaces and harbourmaster tile bonuses. Take pounds from the bank equal to the final total breeding value. Discard all revealed cards to your discard pile. Keep unrevealed cards in hand.

Subphase 2: Delivery Trading Post Selection

Deliver sheep to a trading post whose trading post value is equal to or lower than your total breeding value. You cannot choose a trading post that already has one of your discs, except for local trading post 0 and terminal local trading post 21. Place one of your discs on the chosen trading post space.

Disc Placement & Clearing

Take the disc from any disc space on your player board. Discs from spaces with white corners can be placed on any trading post space. Discs from spaces with dark corners can only be placed on trading post spaces that also have dark corners. Clearing a disc space unlocks its ability, which may include step limit increases, hand limit increases, auxiliary action unlocks, certificate limit increases, permanent wool unlocks, or permanent certificate unlocks. Some clearing actions grant immediate pounds or require immediate payment.

Delivery Actions & Costs

Placing a disc may trigger a delivery action, such as gaining pounds or adding a specific card to your discard pile. For each disc placed on trading post 0, immediately gain six pounds. Pay any transport costs for the delivery, which generally apply to dark corner disc spaces. If you cannot pay combined costs, choose a different disc or trading post.

Subphases 3 & 4: Foresight & Market Refill Foresight Tile Placement

Choose one tile from the foresight spaces next to spot 3 and one from spot 4. Move each tile to its corresponding section. Worker tiles go to the most expensive free space in their column in the job market. Hazard tiles go to the empty space with the lowest number in their section. Bonus tiles go to the next free space in the bonus tiles market row where the token is located, filling left to right.

Market Refill Triggers

When placing a bonus tile on the space with the bonus tiles market token, move the token along the arrow to the next row. If the token moves along a yellow or turquoise arrow, trigger a refill. A yellow arrow triggers a sheep market refill. A turquoise arrow triggers flipping the four neutral building tiles with the 11 icon to their back.

Refill Procedures

For a sheep market refill, draw from the market stack to restore the initial number of cards for your player count. Sort by colour and place face-up. If the stack is empty, add no cards. For the neutral building flip, runholders on flipped tiles remain and continue movement. The front side actions become inaccessible, and modified options apply for the rest of the game.

Post-Wellington Steps Turn Completion

Move your runholder to the start of the trail by placing it on the horseman space in the lower left corner. Refill each empty foresight space with tiles drawn from the Wellington supply, matching the letter on the tile back to the space letter. End your turn.

Actions Lookup
Core Local Actions Hire One Worker

Choose one worker tile from the job market, pay the hiring cost modified by the action icon, and place it in the leftmost free space of its type row on your player board. If the space depicts an immediate action, perform it or forfeit it. Full rows cannot accept more workers. Workers on the fifth space are worth four victory points at game end.

Buy Sheep From Sheep Market

Acquire sheep cards from the sheep market based on your shepherd count and pounds spent. With one shepherd, buy one Dorset Horn for 3 pounds, one Corriedale or Lincoln for 5 pounds, or one Hampshire or Ryeland for 9 pounds. With two shepherds, buy one Dorset Horn for 1 pound, one Corriedale or Lincoln for 2 pounds, or one Suffolk and one exchange token for 10 pounds. With three shepherds, pay 3 pounds for two sheep with 2 VP each. With four shepherds, pay 4 pounds for one Suffolk and one exchange token. Unused shepherds may draw two cards from the market stack to add to the sheep market. Acquired cards go to your discard pile. You may buy fewer than allowed.

Place Private Building

Choose a private building tile from above your player board. Place it on an empty building space or replace an existing one. You must have enough craftsmen and pay two pounds per craftsman needed. Replacing requires only the difference in craftsmen and cost. Building spaces require specific pathfinder track advancement. The neutral building tile B is the only one that allows placing your own building tiles on the trail.

Move Ship & Upgrade Harbour

Move your ship up to spaces equal to your sailor count in any direction. Movement ends when you choose to stop or upgrade a harbour. Upgrading requires paying the cost and placing a storehouse or disc. Small harbours grant a card and may unlock bonuses. Medium harbours grant a card and allow harbourmaster acquisition. Large harbours require no cost, grant a card, and unlock foreign trading posts. Reset ship to start if upgrading the final small harbour on a route.

Shear Sheep

Reveal up to as many different sheep cards as you have shearers. Sum wool values and add permanent wool. Take pounds equal to the total. Discard revealed cards. Unused shearers may draw one card and immediately discard one. You may place a disc on a wool trading post if your total wool value meets the requirement. Wool trading post 13 allows repeated delivery.

Gain Objective Card

Choose one objective card from the general objective display or draw the top card from the objective stack. Place it face-up on your discard pile. Refill the display if needed. Objective cards can be played before Phase A or before/after any single action in Phase B. Playing triggers an immediate action. Tasks are scored at game end.

Advance Pathfinder

Advance your disc on the pathfinder track. Spaces 3 and 8 grant gold. Spaces 4 or 6 grant step tiles. Spaces 2, 4, and 7 unlock building spaces. Space 11 allows flipping a step tile to +2 or flipping the fee tile to ignore hands. Space 16 grants pounds for further advancement. Victory points are scored at the highest reached value.

Remove Hazard

Remove one hazard tile from any section, place it face-up, and take the associated deck-building card. Pay the cost if shown. Rockfall hazards grant a Sheepdog card. Flood hazards grant a Ferry card. Gain victory points at game end.

Move Runholder Forward

Move your runholder forward up to the white number in the icon. Perform Phase B again on the new location without replenishing hand cards.

Auxiliary Actions Single vs Double Actions

Auxiliary actions unlock when at least one of their two disc spaces is cleared. If only one space is cleared, perform a single action. If both are cleared, you may perform a double action, doubling requirements and rewards. Single actions: Gain 1 pound. Draw 1 card then discard 1. Pay 2 pounds to move certificate marker 1 space. Pay 1 pound to move ship 1 space or upgrade harbour. Pay 1 pound to advance pathfinder disc 1 space. Double actions: Gain 2 pounds. Draw 2 cards then discard 2. Pay 4 pounds to move certificate marker up to 2 spaces. Pay 2 pounds to move ship up to 2 spaces or upgrade harbour. Pay 2 pounds to advance pathfinder disc 2 spaces.

Special & Free Actions Gain Exchange Token

Gain one exchange token from the bank. Tokens can be used at any time, even mid-action or on other turns. Return a token to the bank to draw up to two cards and immediately discard the same number. Combine two tokens to remove a Shropshire sheep from your deck and move your gold marker forward one space.

Collect Gold & Spend

Use available gold during Phase A or B to buy bonus cards from the bonus cards supply. Pay 3 gold for steering wheel/barrel cards, 4 gold for bell cards, and 5 gold for compass cards. Place purchased cards face-up on your discard pile.

Immediate & Triggered Actions

Immediate actions on worker rows, harbourmaster tiles, or objective cards must be performed or forfeited when triggered. Deck-building or bonus cards that are not sheep or objectives require drawing a card after performing or forfeiting their effect.

Core Mechanics
Disc Spaces & Clearing Placement Rules

Disc spaces have either white corners or dark corners. Discs from white corner spaces can be placed on any trading post or harbour space. Discs from dark corner spaces can only be placed on spaces that also have dark corners. If you must place a disc on a white corner space but only have dark corner discs left, you may place a dark corner disc. If you cannot place a disc due to lack of discs or payment, remove one from a medium harbour and place it instead.

Clearing Effects

Clearing a disc space unlocks its ability. Effects include increasing step limit, increasing hand limit, unlocking auxiliary actions, increasing temporary certificate limit to five, unlocking permanent wool, or unlocking permanent certificates. Some spaces grant immediate pounds or require immediate payment. Failure to pay clearing costs requires choosing a different space.

Fees & Movement Hands Payment Rules

Fees are triggered when moving to or passing over locations with hands. Black hands cost one pound to the bank. Green hands cost two pounds to the building owner. Pay fees immediately during movement. If you cannot pay some or all fees, pay as much as possible and continue movement. You never pay fees retroactively. All other costs must be paid in full. Reaching space 11 on the pathfinder track allows flipping the fee tile to ignore all hands.

Markers (Gold & Certificates) Certificate Track

The certificate marker starts at zero. The number indicates available temporary certificates. Move the marker backward to use them. The limit starts at three and increases to five when clearing the corresponding disc space. Moving the marker from 0 or 1 to 2 or higher also moves the gold marker forward one space.

Gold Track

The gold marker starts at one. The limit is five. Excess gold is lost. Move the marker backward to spend gold. Gold is gained through immediate actions, bonus tiles, and pathfinder spaces.

Flock Deck & Hand Management Deck Composition

The flock deck consists of the draw stack, hand, and discard pile. Start with a hand limit of four. Draw to limit in Phase C. Reshuffle discard pile into draw stack when empty. You may look at your discard pile at any time. You may not look at your draw stack.

Card Restrictions

You may not play deck-building or bonus cards during Phase C. Objective cards can be played before Phase A or before/after single actions in Phase B. Revealing cards for income or shearing is a snapshot; you cannot use exchange tokens or draw cards during the reveal.

Trading Posts & Harbours Trading Post Rules

Local trading posts are available from the start. Foreign trading posts unlock by placing storehouses on the sea routes board. Wool trading posts are available locally or unlock via large harbours. You cannot deliver to a post that already has your disc, except for local post 0, local post 21, and wool post 13. Delivery actions add cards to your discard pile.

Harbour Upgrades

Small harbours require paying a cost and placing a storehouse. They grant a card and may unlock player board bonuses. Medium harbours require paying a cost and placing a disc. They grant a card and allow harbourmaster acquisition by moving a worker from the rightmost space of its row. Large harbours require no cost, place a storehouse, grant a card, and unlock foreign trading posts. Upgrading the final small harbour on a route resets your ship to the start.

End Game & Scoring
Game End Trigger Trigger Condition

The game ends when the bonus tiles market token moves along the red arrow out of the bonus tiles market. Immediately take the token and place it in front of you. Refill any empty foresight spaces at the end of your turn. This is your last turn.

Final Turns Modified Turn Rules

Each remaining player takes one final turn. Move the runholder and resolve the location. Players reaching Wellington carry out all four subphases. If a player chooses a bonus tile from foresight space B during subphase 4, remove it from the game. Do not carry out Phase C on final turns. Refill empty foresight spaces after Phase B. The game ends after the last player finishes Phase B.

Harbourmaster Tasks Task Checklist

Gain 1 VP for every certificate you have, permanent or temporary. Gain 1 VP for each objective card in your objective area, fulfilled or not. Gain 1 VP for each hazard tile collected. Gain 2 VP per 5 spaces advanced on the pathfinder track. Gain 1 VP for every 2 storehouses placed on the sea routes board. Gain 1 VP for each private building tile on the game board. Gain 1 VP for each card in your deck showing a ferry. Gain 1 VP for every 2 worker tiles in your worker section, including printed ones.

Reference Tables
Action Icons & Modifiers Icon Reference

Requirements are indicated by red arrows or numbers. Rewards are indicated by green arrows or white numbers. Discarding cards means placing them face-up on your discard pile. Gaining pounds means taking them from the bank. Paying pounds means returning them to the bank. Cost modifiers on hire/invest icons: -0 means no change. -3 means cost increases by 3. -2 means cost decreases by 2. Certificate marker movement icons indicate spaces to move forward. You cannot exceed your temporary certificate limit. Gold marker movement follows the same forward movement rules, with a hard limit of 5.

Marker Movement Trigger

Moving the certificate marker from 0 or 1 to 2 or higher automatically moves the gold marker forward one space.

Component Glossary Key Terms

Runholder: Your movement piece that travels the trail to perform actions and reach Wellington. Flock Deck: Your personal deck comprising sheep, deck-building, bonus, and objective cards, split into draw stack, hand, and discard pile. Trading Post: Spaces on the board or sea routes where you deliver sheep or wool to gain victory points and trigger actions. Harbourmaster: A tile acquired by upgrading a medium harbour, granting immediate/permanent bonuses and end-game scoring tasks. Foresight: Spaces in Wellington holding tiles that refill the job market, hazard sections, or bonus market during your turn. Exchange Token: A special token used to draw and discard cards or remove specific sheep from your deck. Wellington Supply: Bags containing worker, hazard, and bonus tiles sorted by letter, used to refill board sections. Pathfinder Track: A progress track that grants step tiles, gold, building unlocks, and victory points. Disc Spaces: Spaces on your player board holding discs; clearing them unlocks abilities, limits, or actions. Bank: The general supply of pounds and exchange tokens available to all players. Money and exchange tokens are considered unlimited. If the objective stack runs out, vacant spaces in the general objective display are not refilled. If the display is empty, no new objective cards can be gained. The same applies to deck-building card stacks and the bonus cards supply. If you replace a building while your runholder is on it, you cannot immediately use the new building's local actions if you already used an action from the replaced building. If you remove a hazard tile with runholders on it, leave those runholders on the empty space.
