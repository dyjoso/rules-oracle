Game Overview & Setup

The principle of the game is simple. A player’s turn consists of two mandatory parts: first, distribute newly supplied goods onto the offer spaces; then take an action. As an action, players may choose either to take all goods of one type from an offer space or to use one of the available buildings. Building actions allow players to upgrade goods, sell them, or use them to build their own buildings and ships. Buildings are both an investment opportunity and a revenue stream, as players must pay an entry fee to use buildings that they do not own. Ships, on the other hand, are primarily used to provide the food that is needed to feed the workers.

After every seven turns, the round ends: a harvest increases players’ grain and cattle reserves, and players must feed their workers. After a fixed number of rounds, each player may carry out one final action, and then the game ends. Players add the value of their buildings and ships to their cash reserves. The player who has amassed the largest fortune is the winner.

The following table shows how many rounds are played, depending on the number of players:

Number of players 1 2 3 4 5
Number of rounds (full version) 7 14 18 20 20
Duration in minutes (full version) 60 120 180 200 210

The starting player is the player who lives closest to water. This player takes the Starting Player Tile. After the starting player’s turn, players take turns in clockwise order around the table.

Setup Checklist:

  1. Place the Game Board in the center of the table. Shuffle the 7 Supply Tiles and place them face down on the round spaces. Place players' Ship Markers beside the board near the first Supply tile.
  2. Place Goods and Food Tokens on their corresponding Supply spaces. Leave the majority in the box to add as needed. Note that only standard goods enter the game through the offer spaces; these can be upgraded using building actions.
  3. Place starting offers on the appropriate offer spaces: 2 Francs, 2 fish, 2 wood, and 1 clay. Give each player 5 Francs and 1 coal as starting resources.
  4. Shuffle the Special Building Cards and place 6 face-down on the Special Buildings space. Remove the green Construction Firm and the two Building Firm cards from the Standard Building pile and place them beside the board as town buildings. Shuffle the remaining Standard Building cards, divide them into 3 equally-sized piles, sort each pile by sort order number (lowest on top), and fan them out from the Building Proposals spaces.
  5. Sort the Round Cards by round number for the appropriate player count and place them face-up on the Round card space with round 1 on top. Place the Loan Cards beside the board. Place the Food Production Tokens beside the board.
Turn Sequence

A player’s turn consists of two mandatory actions as well as optional additional actions. Players must first carry out a Supply Action and then a Main Action. Additional actions may be carried out at any time during the player’s turn.

Supply Action Ship Placement & Tile Reveal

The player taking the turn places their Ship Marker on the next free Supply Tile in the direction of the arrow. In the first round, the Supply tiles are face down until they are occupied. Once occupied, they are turned over and remain face up until the end of the game without changing their position.

Goods Distribution

Two different tokens are shown on each Supply tile. The player takes 1 token from each of these Supply spaces and places them with the standard side up on the appropriate offer spaces. The border on the goods token shows which is the standard side.

Round-End & Interest Triggers

The turn in which a ship is placed on the seventh Supply tile is the last in a round. At the end of this turn, the current Round Card is resolved and turned over. After this, the first turn of the following round begins with the next player’s Supply action on the first Supply tile.

Interest Payment Trigger

The word “Interest” is written on one of the seven Supply tiles. Whenever any player places a ship on this tile, all players who have taken out a loan must immediately pay exactly 1 Franc in interest, irrespective of the number of loans they have. A player who cannot pay this Franc must either sell a building or ship or take another Loan Card and must then pay the 1 Franc.

Main Action Taking Goods

The player takes all tokens (goods or Francs) from one of the seven offer spaces. There is no limit to the size of a personal supply. All goods and Francs are placed face up and may not be concealed from the other players.

Entering Buildings

Most Building Cards allow visitors to take an action or to convert standard goods to upgraded goods. To use a building’s action, players must enter the building by moving their person disc to an unoccupied building. A player may enter buildings that are owned by the town or by any player. A player entering a building must carry out the building’s action.

Entry Fees & Ownership

There is often a fee to use another player’s building, shown at the top right of the Building card. It must be paid to the owner of the building, either in Food or in Francs, before the building can be entered and used. If food and money costs are separated with a “/”, a player only pays one of the two costs. Players may never enter a building if they are unable to pay the entry fee. Players do not pay entry fees to enter their own buildings. If the building belongs to the town, the fee is paid to the Treasury.

Optional Actions Purchasing Assets

At any time during their own turn, a player can buy one or more Building and/or Ship Cards. All buildings owned by the town and the buildings on the top of the three Building Proposals piles are available for purchase. In most cases, the purchase price is the building’s value; if not, the purchase price is indicated separately as Cost beneath the value. A player may buy more than one building from the same building pile in sequence. Only the topmost face-up card on each ship pile may be purchased. The cost of each ship is higher than its value and is indicated below the value. Ships can be purchased even if no-one has built a Wharf yet.

Selling Assets

Buildings and ships may be sold to the town for half of their value. When a player sells a building, it is placed with the other buildings that belong to the town. When a player sells a ship, it is placed on top of the pile of Ship cards of that type. Person discs on buildings are always returned to the player when the building is bought or sold. Buildings may not be sold and then bought again in the same game turn. Buildings and ships may not be sold to other players.

Timing & Permissions

Buying and selling actions may be carried out at any point during the player’s own turn, including before the Supply action or Main action. Selling actions may even be taken during another player’s turn, but not while the other player is taking an action.

Building & Card Mechanics
Reading Cards

Each Building Card has two or three lines of symbols indicating: sort order number, building cost, entry fee, value, and action. Standard Building cards and Special Building cards (shown by an anchor) are read identically. Ship cards are read in the same way: the first line gives build costs, the second shows value with the cost to buy shown below, the type of ship, and the ship symbol.

Construction vs. Purchasing

All buildings and ships may be either built or bought. Building is always a Main Action and requires building resources. It is taken in a Construction building such as the Construction Firm, one of the Building Firms, a Sawmill, or a Wharf. Only buildings in the Building Proposals can be built. Buildings that belong to the town cannot be built but can only be bought. Buying is always an Optional Action and requires money.

Resource Substitution & Change

In this game, Food can always be replaced 1:1 by Francs. Francs can, however, never be replaced by food. Players can exchange five 1 Franc coins for a 5 Franc coin (or vice versa) at any time, and can receive change if they pay more money than is required. Players who pay an entry fee with food or pay food during the Feeding phase do not receive change if they overpay. The same applies when a player pays too much Energy for a building action; the excess energy is not returned.

Rounding Conventions

On many Building cards, players may earn half goods or half Francs, or be required to pay half Energy. Always round to the player’s disadvantage. If a player should receive half of something, round down. If a player should pay half of something, round up.

End of Round

After every seventh game turn, the top Round Card from the pile is resolved. Refer to the line on the card that corresponds to the current number of players. The second number in the row gives the current round number. The third number, in the cooking pot symbol, dictates the amount of food that each player must pay during the feeding phase. The fourth item may show a building symbol. The far right column shows a starting player number relevant only at the start of a round.

Harvest Phase

In the harvest, players receive grain and cattle. A player with at least 1 grain receives 1 additional grain. A player with at least 2 cattle receives 1 additional cattle. In rounds where “No harvest” is shown, neither grain nor cattle is distributed. A player may never receive more than 1 grain and 1 cattle during a harvest.

Feeding Phase

Each player must pay the amount of food shown on the cooking pot symbol on the Round card. Each ship that a player owns reduces the amount of food that they must pay by the amount shown in the table on the Ship card. The formula is: Required Food − Ship Food Reduction = Payment Due. A player who does not have enough food must choose either to sell buildings or to take a Loan Card (or cards). Each Franc counts as 1 food. Players can pay all or part of their food requirement in Francs. Even if a player’s ships provide more food than must be paid, or if a player cannot pay the right amount of food, the player may not take the excess food.

Town Construction

The hammer and anchor symbols on some Round cards indicate that a Standard or Special Building card must be added to the town’s buildings. If a normal building is required, take the card with the lowest sort order number from the Building Proposals. If a Special Building card is required, the top card from the face-down Special Buildings pile is turned face up.

New Ship Reveal

Finally, the Round card is turned over to show the ship on its reverse. Place it on the pile of Ship cards of that type.

Next Round

The next player in turn begins the next round.

Final Stage & Scoring
Final Action Rules

The round stage ends when the last Round card is resolved. The final stage begins. Each player has exactly one more turn and carries out a final action, which is a Main Action. Supply Actions and buying actions may not be taken, and interest is no longer paid. Players may still repay loans and sell buildings and ships. The starting player begins, then each other player takes a turn.

In the final stage only, person discs may be moved to buildings which are already occupied by one or more other person discs. Each player has the chance to visit one building of their choice during the final action. The only building that may not be entered is the one that the person disc is already on.

Wealth Calculation

The game ends immediately after the final actions. The richest player is the winner. A player’s wealth is calculated by adding:

  1. The indicated values of their buildings and ships (the number to the left of the card name).
  2. The additional value of buildings with a plus symbol, which depend on the other buildings that are owned.
  3. Cash in hand.
  4. Deduct 7 Francs for each loan that has not been repaid.

Goods in a player’s supply have no value, with the exception of the Storehouse. Ties result in shared victory.

Quick Reference & Clarifications
Loans & Interest

A player may only take out one or more loans if they are unable to pay the interest on existing loans or cannot pay the required food during the feeding phase. The player must use all of their food and money first, and only if they still cannot pay the required amount can a new loan be taken out. A player taking a loan must take a Loan Card and place it face-up on the table in front of them. A player receives 4 Francs for each loan taken. A loan may be repaid at any time during the game (even immediately before interest is paid) for 5 Francs. At the end of the game, players deduct 7 Francs for each outstanding loan in their possession.

Forced Payments & Selling

A player may only sell buildings and ships outside of their turn if they cannot meet a forced payment like interest or food. The player may sell as many buildings and ships as is necessary to meet that payment. On their own turn, a player may sell as many buildings and ships as they like. Players are never obliged to sell buildings.

Token Limits & Proposal Piles

The supply of game tokens (goods and Francs) is unlimited. If the supply of one type of good runs out, there are 5x multipliers on the back of the 2 food production tokens. If a token is placed on one of these, it counts as 5 tokens. If any of the three Building Proposals is emptied of cards, it remains empty for the rest of the game.

Expert Variants

In a 2-, 3-, 4-, or 5-player game, each player with at least 2, 3, 4, or 5 loans respectively must pay 1 additional Franc of interest every round.
