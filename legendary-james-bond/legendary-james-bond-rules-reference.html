Setup & Game Start
Player Decks & Hands

Each player begins with a personal deck composed of eight 007 Basic Starters, four 00 Agent Basic Starters, and one Special Starter card. There are twenty different Special Starter cards available, with five corresponding to each movie. Shuffle each player's personal deck thoroughly and draw a starting hand of six cards.

Game Mat Stacks

Place the following card stacks face-up on their designated spaces on the game mat. Use all cards of each type for these stacks. Place twelve Miss Moneypenny cards in the Allies stack. Place thirty Wound cards in the Wound stack. Place thirty Gadget cards in the Gadget stack. Position a counter next to the Danger Level tracker. You may use a card from out of play, a coin, a bead, or any similar object for this counter.

Mastermind & Scheme Placement

Select one Mastermind card and place it face-up on the Mastermind space on the game mat. Take the four Mastermind Tactic cards that match the selected Mastermind and place them face-down underneath the Mastermind card in random order. Select one Scheme card and place it face-up on the Scheme space. Follow the setup instructions printed in the Setup section of that Scheme card. The Setup section always specifies how many Scheme Twist cards to add to the Villain Deck. Set aside that exact number of Scheme Twists for later assembly. Many Scheme cards also contain unique special rules that apply throughout the game.

Villain Deck Assembly

Determine the required Villain Groups, Henchmen Groups, Mission Groups, and Gadgets based on the player count using the table below. Each Mastermind card specifies a Villain Group it Always Leads; ensure that group is included. Some Scheme cards specify a Mission Group they Always Use; ensure that group is included. If no group is specified, choose one that fits the theme. Set aside all eight Villain cards from each selected Villain Group, all nine Mission cards from the selected Mission Group, and all ten cards from each selected Henchmen Group. Set aside five Master Strike cards for the Villain Deck.

Assemble the Villain Deck using the following three-step layering process. Add one Master Strike and one Scheme Twist to the Deck A pile. Add two Master Strikes and two Scheme Twists to the Deck B pile. Add the remaining two Master Strikes and two Scheme Twists to the Deck C pile. Shuffle the Deck C pile and place it face-down on top of the Inevitable card. Shuffle the Deck B pile and place it face-down on top of the Deck C pile. Shuffle the Deck A pile and place it face-down on top of the Deck B pile. The finished Villain Deck will have the Deck A pile on top and the Inevitable card on the bottom. Place the assembled Villain Deck face-down on the Villain Deck space.

Hero Deck & Q Branch

Select five Hero Groups for the Hero Deck. Exactly one of these groups must be a James Bond Hero. Each Hero Group contains fourteen cards. Add all cards from the selected groups to the Hero Deck, resulting in seventy cards total. If playing with four or five players, add a sixth Hero Group to the Hero Deck. Shuffle the Hero Deck thoroughly and place it face-down on the Hero Deck space. Flip five cards from the top of the Hero Deck face-up into the five Hero spaces in Q Branch.

First Player Selection

Choose one player to take the first turn. Players will take turns in clockwise order for the remainder of the game.

Turn Structure
Villain Phase

Play the top card of the Villain Deck face-up. If the card is a Villain or Mission, place it in the rightmost On Assignment space. If that space is occupied, push the existing card one space to the left to make room. Continue pushing cards leftward as necessary to accommodate the new entry. If a card is pushed off the leftmost On Assignment space, it escapes. Resolve all escape effects before resolving any ambush effects on the newly played card. If the played card is a Gadget, place it under the Villain or Mission closest to the Villain Deck. If no cards are On Assignment, the Mastermind acquires the Gadget. If the played card is a Scheme Twist or Master Strike, resolve its effect immediately and place it in the KO Pile unless instructed otherwise. Villain and Mission cards On Assignment do not move forward when a Gadget, Scheme Twist, or Master Strike is played.

Action Phase

Play cards from your hand one at a time in any order. Each played card generates Attack, Recruit Points, and/or special abilities as printed on the card. Keep all played cards in your play area until the end of your turn. Between playing cards, or after playing all cards, you may spend accumulated Attack and Recruit Points to fight Villains, complete Missions, or recruit Heroes. You may perform any number of these actions in any order during this phase.

Clean Up Phase

Discard all cards you played this turn into your discard pile. Discard any remaining cards in your hand. Draw six new cards from your deck. If your deck is empty and you still need to draw cards, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck and draw the remaining cards. Resolve any effects that trigger at the end of the turn.

Resolution Priority: Escape effects → Ambush effects → Player actions → End-of-turn effects.

Card Types & Board Zones
Board Zones On Assignment

This zone holds up to five Villain or Mission cards face-up. New entries always occupy the rightmost available space. Cards are pushed leftward when new cards enter. Cards pushed off the leftmost space escape to the Escaped Pile.

Q Branch

This zone displays five face-up Hero cards drawn from the Hero Deck. Players spend Recruit Points to recruit these Heroes. Empty spaces are immediately refilled by flipping the top card of the Hero Deck.

Victory Pile

Each player maintains a personal face-up Victory Pile. Defeated Villains, completed Missions, and recovered Gadgets are placed here. These cards track progress and determine final scoring.

KO Pile

This shared pile holds cards that are removed from the game. Scheme Twists, Master Strikes, and cards targeted by KO effects are placed here face-up.

Escaped Pile

This zone holds Villains and Missions that were pushed off the On Assignment zone. Escaped cards trigger failure effects and may carry Gadgets.

Card Types Villains & Missions

Function: Obstacles that enter On Assignment and must be defeated or completed. Entry Rule: Enter the rightmost On Assignment space, pushing existing cards left. Defeat/Complete Condition: Spend Attack equal to the card's Attack value. Escape/Failure Effect: KO a Hero costing six or less from Q Branch. If the card carries Gadgets, each player discards one card from their hand. Increase the Danger Level by the Mission's Danger Value if a Mission escapes. Victory Point Value: Printed on the card.

Heroes

Function: Cards played from hand to generate resources and abilities. Entry Rule: Played from hand during the Action Phase. Defeat/Complete Condition: Not applicable. Escape/Failure Effect: Not applicable. Victory Point Value: Not applicable.

Gadgets

Function: Items acquired by enemies that grant Victory Points when recovered. Entry Rule: Placed under the Villain or Mission closest to the Villain Deck, or under the Mastermind if no cards are On Assignment. Defeat/Complete Condition: Recovered when the holding card is defeated or completed. Escape/Failure Effect: Carried to the Escaped Pile if the holder escapes. Victory Point Value: Printed on the card.

Masterminds & Mastermind Tactics

Function: Primary antagonists that must be defeated four times to win. Entry Rule: Placed face-up on the Mastermind space with four Tactic cards face-down underneath. Defeat/Complete Condition: Spend Attack equal to the Mastermind's Attack value. Choose a random Tactic card, place it in your Victory Pile, and resolve its Fight effect. Escape/Failure Effect: Not applicable. Victory Point Value: Printed on the Mastermind and Tactic cards.

Scheme Twists & Master Strikes

Function: Special event cards that advance the Mastermind's plans or hinder players. Entry Rule: Played from the Villain Deck during the Villain Phase. Defeat/Complete Condition: Not applicable. Escape/Failure Effect: Not applicable. Victory Point Value: Not applicable.

Wounds & Allies

Function: Wounds weaken player hands. Allies provide additional recruitment options. Entry Rule: Wounds are placed in a player's discard pile when gained. Allies are placed in the Allies stack. Defeat/Complete Condition: Not applicable. Escape/Failure Effect: Not applicable. Victory Point Value: Not applicable.

Core Actions: Recruiting & Combat
Recruiting Heroes

Spend Recruit Points equal to a Hero's cost to recruit it from Q Branch or the Allies stack. Place the recruited Hero into your discard pile. Refill the empty space in Q Branch immediately by flipping the top card of the Hero Deck. You may chain recruitments in a single turn if you have sufficient Recruit Points.

Fighting Villains

Spend Attack equal to a Villain's Attack value to defeat it. You may target any Villain On Assignment regardless of its position. Resolve any Fight effect printed on the Villain before comparing Attack totals. Place the defeated Villain and any attached Gadgets into your Victory Pile. Remaining Attack may be used to fight additional targets.

Completing Missions

Complete Missions using the same process as fighting Villains. Some Missions require Recruit Points instead of Attack, indicated by a star icon. Spend the required resource equal to the Mission's value. Resolve any Success effect printed on the Mission upon completion. Place the completed Mission and any attached Gadgets into your Victory Pile. Remaining resources may be used for other actions.

Class Abilities

Class Special Abilities trigger when you play cards of a matching Hero Class earlier in the same turn. A single class icon requires one previously played card of that class. A double class icon requires two previously played cards of that class. If a card has both single and double class abilities, playing one matching card triggers the single ability, while playing two matching cards triggers both abilities. You may only use a card's Class Special Ability once per turn, even if multiple qualifying cards were played.

Victory Pile Tracking

Maintain your Victory Pile face-up and separate from your discard pile. Many players keep it horizontal to avoid confusion. Villains, Missions, and Gadgets in your Victory Pile do not return to your deck. At the end of the game, tally the Victory Points printed on each card in your Victory Pile to determine your final score.

Keywords & Special Rules
Keywords Liaison

You may leave a Liaison card in your play area after your turn. During a Friendly Player's next turn, they may discard it to gain the Liaison effect. If unused by the start of your next turn, it is discarded automatically. In solo play, you gain the effect on your next turn or it is discarded at the end of that turn.

Chase

After playing the Villain Deck card but before playing Heroes from your hand, a Chase card moves one space left, pushing other cards normally. This occurs even if the card was already pushed this turn or just entered On Assignment. Resolve multiple Chase moves from left to right.

High-Speed Chase

Functions identically to Chase, but the card moves two spaces left instead of one.

Flying

Enters On Assignment in the first open space from the right, rather than the rightmost space.

Persist

If pushed off On Assignment, it remains in place and the card pushing it escapes instead.

Squeeze

When it enters On Assignment, KO the leftmost and rightmost spaces in Q Branch. Those spaces do not refill until the Squeeze card leaves play. Multiple Squeeze cards block additional spaces symmetrically. Three or more Squeeze cards empty Q Branch entirely.

00 Status

At the end of your turn, before discarding your hand, if you defeated at least one Villain or the Mastermind that turn, resolve each of your cards' 00 Status effects.

Loyalty to the Mission

The "X" value on these cards equals the highest Danger Value among Missions in your Victory Pile.

Inevitable

The final card of the Villain Deck. Moves one space automatically every turn. Escaping triggers an instant loss. See Inevitable Card Rules for full details.

Poker Hands

Choose up to two cards with cost one or higher from your hand and/or play area. Combine them with cards in Q Branch to form a five-card hand. Rankings from weakest to strongest: Pair, Two Pair, Three of a Kind, Straight, Flush, Full House, Four of a Kind, Straight Flush. Five cards of the same cost do not count as a Full House. Tied hands remain tied.

General Ability Rules

Special Abilities override standard rules. If an ability affects multiple players, the Active Player acts first, followed by others clockwise. If you cannot fully resolve an effect, perform as much as possible. If a choice is required and the chooser is unspecified, the Active Player decides.

Wounds are placed in your discard pile when gained. They provide no Attack or Recruit Points. You may KO Wounds from your hand using their healing ability, provided you have not recruited Heroes, fought Villains or the Mastermind, or completed Missions during that turn. Wounds are not Heroes, but they are cards and can be targeted by effects that say "KO one of your cards."

Generated Extra Cards are placed near the game mat during setup. Follow the specific instructions on the card when an effect brings them into play.

Common Cards include Basic Starters, Wounds, Gadgets, and other shared components used across multiple movie scenarios and future expansions.

Reducing ⚔ or ⭐ can never lower a value below zero.

Printed ⭐ or ⚔ refers strictly to the numeral printed on the card, ignoring any modifications from special abilities.

Cards with No Cost count as having zero cost for effects that reference cost.

Masterminds do not count as Villains. Effects that specifically target Villains do not affect Masterminds.

Villain Cards That Go In Your Deck are added to your discard pile when fought. Once drawn, use the secondary text and resource icons printed on the left side of the card.

Once This Turn abilities may be resolved at any point during the turn, not necessarily immediately when the card is played.

Moves refers to a card starting on an On Assignment space and ending on another space or in the Escaped Pile. Initial entry into On Assignment does not count as moving.

007, 00 Agents, and Miss Moneypenny Allies all count as Heroes for ability purposes.

Winning, Losing & Scoring
Win Conditions

The game ends immediately when players have defeated the Mastermind four times. All players achieve a cooperative victory.

Loss Conditions

The game ends immediately if the Danger Level reaches the threshold printed on the Scheme card. The game also ends immediately if any effect states "Evil Wins." All players suffer a cooperative loss.

Scoring & Tiebreakers

Tally the Victory Points from all cards in each player's Victory Pile. The player with the highest total is declared the Top Agent and wins the individual competitive portion of the game. Groups may choose to focus primarily on cooperative survival, competitive point accumulation, or a balanced mix of both.

Edge Cases & Deck Management
Deck Exhaustion

If the Hero Deck runs out before the game ends, finish the current turn. The game concludes as a draw between good and evil. The player with the most Victory Points wins the individual victory.

If the Gadget stack runs out, the game continues. Effects that would acquire a Gadget simply fail to acquire one. Do not retrieve extra copies from the KO Pile.

If the Wound stack runs out, the game continues. If a player would gain a Wound but none remain, gather all KO'd Wounds to create a new stack.

If the Allies stack runs out, the game continues. Players can no longer recruit from that stack.

Card Resolution Terms

KO means place the targeted card into the KO Pile. Cards in the KO Pile are out of the game. Effects specify whether the KO'd card comes from your hand, your play area, or another location. KO'd cards retain any resources or abilities they generated before being removed.

Defeat means add the Villain to your Victory Pile. Resolve its Fight effect before moving it.

Complete means add the Mission to your Victory Pile. Resolve its Success effect before moving it.

Acquire a Gadget means take the top Gadget from the Gadget stack and place it into your Victory Pile.

Gaining a card means place that card into your discard pile.

Player & Target Definitions

The Active Player is the player whose turn it currently is.

Friendly Players includes all other players during cooperative play. During competitive play, only you are considered Friendly. Team play treats teammates as Friendly.

Inevitable Card Rules

The Inevitable card moves one space left whenever a card is played from the Villain Deck, typically once per turn. It is never successfully completed. Instead, it provides instructions for what to do when its defeat condition is met. No Villain or Mission may be placed to the right of an Inevitable card. Ignore any effects that would attempt to do so. The Inevitable card is only moved by its own printed rules or automatic turn progression.

Variants & Solo Play
Solo Setup & Scoring

Use only four Hero Groups for the Hero Deck, totaling fifty-six cards. Assemble the Villain Deck with one Villain Group, one Mission Group, three Henchmen cards from the same group (one in Deck A, one in Deck B, one in Deck C), zero Gadgets, five Master Strikes (one in Deck A, two in Deck B, two in Deck C), and five Scheme Twists (one in Deck A, two in Deck B, two in Deck C).

After playing a Scheme Twist, place a Hero of your choice costing six or less from Q Branch on the bottom of the Hero Deck. After playing a Master Strike, immediately play the next card from the Villain Deck.

If you win, calculate your final score by subtracting penalties from your Victory Points. Subtract four points for each Gadget carried away. Subtract three points for each Scheme Twist that occurred. Subtract one point for each Villain that escaped. Subtract points equal to the final Danger Level. Record your score, used Heroes, Scheme, and Mastermind to track progress across different scenarios.

Difficulty Modifiers

To ease the challenge, grant each player one or more prep turns. During a prep turn, take a normal turn but skip playing the top card of the Villain Deck.

To increase the challenge, grant each player one or more surprised turns. During a surprised turn, take a normal turn but skip the step where you play cards from your hand.

To balance skill levels, replace some 007 Basic Starters in newer players' decks with Miss Moneypenny Allies.

Perfect Mission Mode

Enable this mode to require the completion of every Mission. Failing even a single Mission results in an immediate loss.
