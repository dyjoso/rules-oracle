Game Flow & Structure

March of the Ants is played over multiple rounds. Each round follows a strict four-phase sequence: Worker Phase, Soldier Phase, Queen Phase, and Slumber. The player with the most Colony Points at the end of the final round wins the game.

The Active Player Marker determines turn order. It begins with a randomly selected player and passes clockwise after each turn. The player holding the marker at the end of the Worker Phase retains it and takes the first turn in the next round.

Setup
  1. Place the Great Tunnel in the center of the table.
  2. Place the Score/Round Track on one side of the play area on the 4-round side. Place the Strength Track beneath it. Place the Round Marker on Round 1 and the Centipede Size Marker on the space corresponding to the player count.
  3. Shuffle the starting hexes (#1-10) together and place 2 per player to form a facedown stack. Return the rest to the game box.
  4. Randomly select 2 Beyond the Meadow hexes (#24-28) and shuffle them with the standard hexes (#11-23). Place the starting hex stack on top of these, and place the combined stack near the Great Tunnel. With 4-5 players, add 1 extra Beyond the Meadow hex.
  5. Place the food, centipede, and wormhole tokens next to the play area.
  6. Place the Great Defender Rest Tile beneath the Round Track. Shuffle the other Rest Tiles and place them in a facedown stack nearby. Draw and place tiles faceup under the Score Track until there is 1 Rest Tile per player.
  7. Randomly select 2 Reward Tiles from each level. Place the two "I - II" tiles on top of the two "III+" tiles to form a facedown stack. Return the rest to the box.
  8. Reveal the top Reward Tile and place it faceup in the center of the Great Tunnel.
  9. Randomly select a starting player and give them the Active Player Marker.
  10. Shuffle the Ant Card deck. Place it near the play area.
  11. Each player chooses a color and takes 36 ant tokens of that color and 1 Nest Mat with the star side up.
  12. Each player places 4 ant tokens into their Larvae Chamber as larvae and 2 in the Great Tunnel as ants. Place the rest on the side of the Nest Mat as eggs.
  13. Each player places their Score and Strength Markers on the spaces of their respective tracks, with the printed sides down.
  14. Each player places 2 food tokens into their Food Stores.
  15. Each player draws 3 cards.

See Variants & Player Counts section for 2-player, Epic, Ancient Species, and Solo/Co-op rules.

The Worker Phase
Turn Structure

Players take turns in clockwise order, starting with the player holding the Active Player Marker. Play continues until all faceup Rest Tiles have been taken using the Rest action.

On your turn, you first pay the cost, then perform the chosen Action. After your action resolves, the two neighboring players may take the associated Reaction in clockwise order, starting with the player to your left. Reactions have no cost. Once your action and all Reactions are resolved, pass the Active Player Marker clockwise to begin the next turn.

Actions & Reactions

Explore Cost: 1 food Action: Draw a hex and place it connected by a tunnel to an adjacent location where you have at least 1 ant. Move any number of your ants from any adjacent connected locations into the explored hex. You must move at least 1 ant. Ants immediately move into collection sites, or to the Outskirts if all sites are full. Reaction: Each neighbor may move 1 larva from their Larvae Chamber to a hex they inhabit or to the Great Tunnel.

Forage Cost: 1 food Action: Draw 3 cards, then discard any 1 card from your hand. There is no limit to hand size. If you would draw a card and the deck is empty, shuffle the discard pile to make a new deck. Reaction: Each neighbor may gain 1 larva by moving an egg from their supply to their Larvae Chamber. If you have no eggs, you may not gain a larva.

March Cost: 1 food Action: Take up to 6 steps with your larvae and/or ants. Taking steps is always optional. Reaction: Each neighbor may take 1 ant step, or move 1 larva from their Larvae Chamber to the Great Tunnel.

Play a Card Cost: Shown on Card Action: Play one card from your hand. Playing a card does not cost food. To pay, remove larvae and/or ants equal to the card's cost from your Larvae Chamber or the Meadow and return them to your egg supply. Pay with any combination of larvae and ants, or only with ants, or only with larvae, as specified on the card. Reaction: Each neighbor may discard a card from their hand to draw a card.

Rest Cost: None Action: Take 1 faceup Rest Tile of your choice and gain its rewards. You may not Rest on your first turn in a round unless you have no food and no cards in hand. Once you have Rested, you must Rest on each subsequent turn. When a player takes the last faceup Rest Tile, they finish their turn, pass the Active Player Marker clockwise, and the Worker Phase ends. Reaction: None.

End of Phase Reactions

At the end of the Worker Phase, in player order starting with the Active Player, take Reactions equal to the number of thorax Evolutions you have in play. You may not choose the same Reaction twice. Each player takes all of their Reactions before the next player takes any. Choose Reactions from the four standard actions: Explore, Forage, March, or Play a Card. If you have more than 4 evolved thoraxes, you only take 4 Reactions—one of each.

The Soldier Phase
Battle Trigger & Setup

Battles occur in the Great Tunnel and in Contested hexes. A hex is Contested when two or more players have ants in it and there are ants in the Outskirts, or if a centipede is present in a hex containing any ants. If a Contested hex becomes uncontested before a battle starts, the battle will not occur. No hex battles occur in Round 1.

The Great Tunnel does not count as a hex. A battle takes place there if ants from two or more different colonies are present. If only one player's ants are present, they gain the 1st-place award and no battle occurs. Hexes adjacent to the Great Tunnel grant their controller points during Slumber. Players may always move larvae directly to the Great Tunnel.

Resolution Steps
  1. Determine Defender: In hexes, the player who inhabits the Control Site is the defender. In the Great Tunnel, the player with the Great Defender Rest Tile is the defender. The defender wins ties and acts last.
  2. Declare Strength: Each player counts their ants in the location and adds the number of head Evolutions they have. Set your marker to that number on the Strength Track.
  3. Ferocity: Starting with the player to the left of the defender and proceeding clockwise, each player in the battle may play 1 card from their hand facedown. All players reveal their cards and add the number of pips shown in the symbol to their Strength, adjusting their position on the Strength Track.
  4. Outcome: The player with the greatest Strength wins. In the Great Tunnel, also determine 2nd and 3rd place based on Strength. Ties are broken in clockwise order starting with the defender. Only one player can win. All other players lose.
  5. Casualties: Losing players assign casualties to the winner. For every 2 losing ants combined, assign 1 casualty to the winner. The winning player assigns 1 casualty per ant they have, distributed as evenly as possible to losing players in clockwise order. The winner cannot lose their last ant in the location. Each player may only be assigned enough casualties to remove all of their ants. Once assigned, each player loses 1 ant of their choice from the battle location for each casualty assigned to them.
  6. Spoils: In hexes, the winner gains control and may move 1 ant in the hex into the Control Site, pushing any occupant to the Outskirts. In the Great Tunnel, 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place players gain the spoils shown on the Reward Tile. Players gain spoils even if all their ants were removed during Casualties.
  7. Cleanup: Players take turns moving all of their ants in the Outskirts of that hex to vacant collection sites in clockwise order starting with the winner. Reset the markers on the Strength Track to zero. Discard all cards played during battle.
The Queen Phase & Slumber
Harvest & Feeding

Gain resources from collection sites that your ants inhabit. Add 1 food to your stores, add 1 or 2 larvae to your Larvae Chamber as shown on the site, draw a card, or advance your Score Marker 1 space.

All ants in the Meadow must be fed to survive. Larvae do not need to be fed. Each food token feeds up to 4 ants, plus 1 additional ant for each abdomen Evolution you have. You may choose not to feed any number of your ants, but you will lose any unfed ants, returning them to your egg supply.

Queen’s Choice & Scoring

After all ants have been fed, each player chooses twice to gain either 1 food or 3 larvae. There is no Queen's Choice in the final round. If needed, resolve the Queen's Choice in clockwise order starting with the Active Player.

Gain 1 Colony Point for each Inner Meadow hex you control. All hexes adjacent to the Great Tunnel are part of the Inner Meadow. They do not need to be connected to the Great Tunnel to score.

Gain 1, 2, or 3 Colony Points for meeting the conditions of the Colony Goal cards you have in play. The number of points gained is based on how well you meet the condition, as shown on the card.

Round Cleanup

If it is the final round, the game ends. Otherwise, advance the Round Marker one space and complete the following steps:

  1. Return the Great Defender Rest Tile to the area beneath the Round Track.
  2. Discard all other faceup Rest Tiles in a discard stack. Reveal new Rest Tiles until there is 1 tile per player beneath the Round Track. If all Rest Tiles have been used, shuffle all discarded Rest Tiles to form a new stack.
  3. Discard the current Reward Tile. Reveal the topmost Reward Tile and place it faceup in the Great Tunnel.
  4. The next Worker Phase begins. The player who was passed the Active Player Marker at the end of the last Worker Phase takes the first turn.
Core Mechanics & Concepts
Board & Hex Rules Hex Placement

Anytime you place a hex in the Meadow, whether through Explore, a card effect, or another ability, you must connect at least one of its tunnel openings to an existing tunnel opening. You may block its tunnels or the tunnels of other hexes. If the hex has a feature, place the corresponding token on the hex and resolve any special abilities.

Inhabiting & Control

When you have at least one ant in a collection site in a hex, you inhabit that hex. Inhabiting a hex allows you to move larvae directly to that hex and is required for many card effects and abilities. A Control Site is a type of collection site that also grants control of the hex. Control is important for scoring and winning ties in battle.

Outskirts & Sharing

When you move an ant into a hex, it immediately inhabits an empty collection site of your choice. If none are empty, place the ant in the Outskirts (anywhere else on that hex). When an ant leaves a collection site, any ant in the Outskirts of that hex will immediately inhabit it at the end of the current action or Reaction. If multiple players have ants in the Outskirts, players take turns inhabiting empty sites in player order.

Ants from multiple colonies may peacefully share a hex as long as there are enough collection sites for all the ants. A hex is Shared when two or more players' ants inhabit that hex. If a hex is Shared but there are not enough sites for all the ants, that hex is also Contested.

Meadow Features

Hexes have different features, collection sites, and sometimes special rules. Hex numbers are used in the Soldier Phase and are color-coded according to their number. Hex names do not have any gameplay effect. Ants may move between adjacent locations if those locations have a connected tunnel. Beyond the Meadow hexes have unique effects written on them. The player who places the hex resolves any "when placed" effects before any players take Reactions. Beyond the Meadow hexes are part of the Meadow.

Centipedes are threats to the ants. Place a centipede token on the hex and advance the Centipede Size by 1. Ants may move into hexes with centipedes and inhabit collection sites as normal, but will have to battle the centipedes during the Soldier Phase.

Wormholes allow ants to move between two hexes that have wormholes with a single step. Wormholes do not connect hexes for the purposes of card effects and Explore actions.

Movement & Steps

Steps let you move ants around the Meadow and move larvae into the Meadow, where they become ants. Taking steps is always optional. One step allows you to move:

  1. 1 ant from any location to any adjacent connected location.
  2. 1 ant from a location with a wormhole to any other location with a wormhole.
  3. 1 ant from a collection site to another open collection site in the same hex.
  4. 1 larva from your Larvae Chamber to any hex that you inhabit or the Great Tunnel.

Abilities that instruct you to take ant steps only allow you to move ants, not larvae. You may use multiple steps to move 1 ant several times, or distribute them among several ants or larvae.

Ant Lifecycle & Resources

The wooden ant tokens represent different stages of the life cycle depending on where they are located. When tokens are in the Meadow (the Great Tunnel or hexes), they are ants. When they are in your Larvae Chamber, they are larvae. All other tokens are kept to the side of the Nest Mat as eggs.

To gain a larva, take an egg from your supply and place it in your Larvae Chamber. To move a larva, take a larva from your Larvae Chamber and place it into the Meadow, where it immediately becomes an ant.

Each player has a limit of 36 wooden ant tokens available each game. If they are all in use, the player cannot gain more larvae or ants. A few extras are included in the game to cover manufacturing issues. Set aside any extras to use as backup.

Cards & Rest Tiles Card Payment & Types

Playing a card does not cost food. To pay for a card, remove larvae and/or ants equal to the cost of the card from your Larvae Chamber or the Meadow and return them to your supply of eggs. Cards may require payment with any combination of larvae and ants, only with ants from the Meadow, or only with larvae from the Larvae Chamber. Card types, their uses, and their effects are detailed on the cards themselves.

Rest Tile Effects

Each time you Rest, choose an available Rest Tile and place it next to your Nest Mat. Some Rest Tiles grant immediate rewards, while others grant bonuses or effects later in the round. Immediate effects resolve when the tile is taken. Ongoing effects apply until the end of the round or as specified on the tile.

Game End & Victory

After the final round, determine the winner of each endgame award. Each award grants Colony Points to the player who wins it. If players tie for an award, each tied player gains the points. Players who failed to feed any of their ants in Autumn are ineligible for endgame awards. If an ineligible player would win an award, it goes to the player with the next most qualifying resources.

The awards go to the player who has: Largest Brood: The most larvae. Greatest Stores: The most food. Untold Potential: The most cards in hand.

The player with the highest score wins. In case of a tie, the player with the most ants in the Meadow wins. If still tied, all tied players share the victory.

Clarifications & FAQ
Card Interactions

Adaptive Radiation: If this card causes you to lose and then complete an ant body, you do not lose points or score points as a result. Into the Unknown: There may only be one wormhole per hex. There may only be 1 centipede per hex. Mating Flight: You may choose to exchange the location of any player's ant, including your own, with that of any other player's ant. Sneak Attack: This card will not cause a hex battle to take place in round 1. Battle in the hex resolves as it normally would in the Soldier Phase, at which point the Worker Phase continues. If there is a centipede in the hex, it will battle until it is defeated, then, if the hex is still Contested, resolve one ant battle. Amazon Head: You gain a larva even if no opponents have larvae to lose. Armored Head: This does not prevent your last ant in a hex from being lost, as long as enough casualties are assigned to you. This does not prevent you from losing all your ants when you lose to a centipede. Army Head: You may move 1 larva from your Larvae Chamber or 1 ant from an adjacent location into the current battle. Betrayer Abdomen/Traitorous Head: Instead of battling a centipede in a hex you are in, you may choose to lose 1 ant from that hex. If you do, other players in that hex still battle the centipede. Blasting Head: When ants are removed, the player whose ants are removed decides which ants are lost. If this card's effect causes a hex to become uncontested, no battle will take place there. You cannot choose to remove less than 3 ants, even if it means removing your own. This may be used in Round 1. Flying Thorax: This does not allow you to move larvae to hexes you do not inhabit or place an explored hex with no connecting tunnels. Garden Thorax: The cost of cards cannot be reduced to zero. Golden Fire Head: You must choose to use this ability immediately after you play your standard Ferocity card. Marching Thorax: This does not double March Reactions when taken as a thorax bonus Reaction. Parasitic Thorax: If 2 or more opponents have an equal amount of larvae, steal 1 larva from each of them. Steal means the opponent loses 1 larva and you gain 1. Renegade Thorax: Centipedes may not end their movement in the Great Tunnel or a hex with another centipede. Scouting Thorax: You do not need to show the unexplored hex to other players. Tunneling Thorax: You cannot place an explored hex with no connecting tunnels. Because you always inhabit hexes with wormholes, you may move larvae directly to those hexes with Explore Reactions or with 1 step. Weaver Thorax: Your opponents cannot use any actions or card effects to move ants out of hexes you control, including effects that would move your ants. This includes effects that exchange ants. They may use ants in those hexes to pay for the cost of cards. Extensive Tunnel: "Hexes connected by a single tunnel" refers to a group of hexes that an ant could traverse without retracing its steps. Eyeless Overlords: There may only be 1 wormhole per hex. Abandoned Spider's Lair: The player who places this hex may choose not to play any of the revealed cards. Ant Lion Den: Only discard cards for inhabited sites, not for the total number of ants in the hex. Nest of Centipedes: There can only be 1 centipede in each hex at any time. If all connected hexes already contain a centipede token, no centipede token is placed.

Hex & Board Rules

Contested Hex: If multiple players have ants in the same hex and there are ants in the Outskirts, it is Contested. If a centipede and ants are in the same hex, it is Contested. If a hex is Contested during Soldier Phase, a battle takes place there. Control Site: A site in a hex that gives the occupying ant's player control over that hex, allowing them to win ties in battle and gain 1 point in Slumber if the hex is adjacent to the Great Tunnel. Excess Casualties: Once all casualties have been assigned, any extra have no effect. Great Defender Rest Tile: This Rest Tile is available each round. The player with this tile counts as the defender in the Great Tunnel Battle, even if they are not present in the battle. Ties are broken in clockwise order, starting with them, and Ferocity card play starts with the player to their left. The Great Tunnel: The central location of the Meadow. You may always move larvae into the Great Tunnel. A battle will take place there during Soldier Phase if ants from 2 or more different colonies are present. The Great Tunnel is not a hex. Hand: A player's cards. There is no hand size limit. The number of cards each player has is public knowledge. Hex Battle: A battle that takes place in a hex. (As opposed to the Great Tunnel Battle, which is not a hex battle.) Inhabit: A hex is inhabited by each player who has at least one ant in a collection site in that hex. The Meadow: Consists of the Great Tunnel and all placed hexes. Moving Larvae: When a larva moves to the Meadow, it immediately becomes an ant. When taking steps, you may move larva directly to any hex you inhabit or the Great Tunnel. Location: Each hex and the Great Tunnel. Lose Ants: Remove your own ants from the Meadow. When you are instructed to lose ants, as a result of a card effect or battle casualties, you choose which ants to lose. Outer Meadow Hex: A hex that is not adjacent to the Great Tunnel. Outskirts: Any place in a hex that is not a collection site. If all collection sites in a hex are inhabited, any ant that enters that hex is placed in the Outskirts. Ants in the Outskirts of a hex automatically move to inhabit collection sites that become empty as soon as the current action or Reaction is resolved. Player Order: The order in which turns are taken. Player order is clockwise and starts with the Active Player unless specified otherwise. Play Priority: Unless specified, resolve questions of priority in player order. Reactions: Players only take Reactions to their neighbors' main action that they take on their turn. Players do not take Reactions to action-like effects that result from cards or other abilities. Shared Hex: A hex that 2 or more players inhabit. Wormhole Hex: A hex that contains a wormhole token is sometimes referred to as a wormhole hex. Wormholes: A location with a wormhole token has a wormhole. Ants may move from any hex with a wormhole to any other hex with a wormhole with 1 step. Ants may not Explore through wormholes.

General Terminology

Active Player: The player with the Active Player Marker. Centipedes: A hex can only have 1 centipede token on it at a time. Centipedes may not be placed in the Great Tunnel.

Variants & Player Counts
2-Player Rules

After completing normal setup, take these extra steps:

  1. Beginning with the Active Player, each player takes 2 of the unused starting hexes at random. Each player chooses 1 hex and places it connected to the Great Tunnel. Return the unchosen hex to the game box.
  2. Each player places 3 ants into their placed hex.
  3. Set aside the ants and Strength Marker of an unused color for the Old Guard. At the start of each Worker Phase, reveal 1 additional Rest Tile. You will use 3 Rest Tiles each round: the Great Defender Tile, and two additional ones.
Epic Game

The epic game is played over 5 rounds rather than 4. It follows all standard rules with these adjustments: During setup, flip the Score Track to the 5-round side and add 1 additional "II+" Reward Tile to the bottom of the stack. In Rounds 4 and 5, each food feeds up to 3 ants per food instead of 4 (before adding abdomen bonuses).

Ancient Species

Randomly assign a Nest Mat to each player. Each player plays as the Ancient Species found on the backside of the Nest Mat. The game follows standard rules and structure. Each Ancient Species shows the new rules that apply to it.

Each Ancient Species has a unique action you can use once per Worker Phase. These actions give a Global Reaction, meaning that all other players, not just the neighboring players, get to take the Reaction. At the start of each Worker Phase, place your Strength Marker in the circular space above your unique action to track whether you have used it. Return it to the Strength Track when you use your unique action, or at the start of the Soldier Phase.

Features are other abilities or penalties that apply to the species. Each Ancient Species has a drawback, and some have a special way to score points during Slumber.

Egg Gardeners: Reaction: Choose one of the played Evolution cards and gain larvae equal to its cost. If no evolutions are played for Reactions, you do not gain any larvae. If an ability lets you play more than one Ferocity card, you will lose a point for each. You do not lose points for playing cards for Ferocity in the Great Tunnel Battle. Horizon Seekers: Expand counts as a single Explore action for purposes of cards or other effects. Abilities that trigger off of Explore actions only apply to one of the explored hexes. If you place both hexes, they must be connected to the same hex and you must move at least 1 ant into each of them. Visionary Queen: You may have any number of Colony Goals in play but once you have 2 or more, you may only add additional Colony Goals by using your unique action. If you play a Colony Goal in any other way, you must replace one of your current ones. The additional cost to Evolve multiple times is always 1 ant or larva, regardless of the cost requirement of the card you are playing. Chaos Dancers: You must take the steps before resolving the card effect.

Solo/Co-op

The rules for the solo and co-op modes are explained on the standalone Rise of the Centipedes sheet.
