March of the Ants is a civilization game where each player grows an ant colony. You will explore the meadow, gather resources, evolve your ants, meeting colony goals, and march to battle. There are many different paths to victory. Throughout the game you will gain Colony Points by expanding your territory, evolving your ants, meeting colony goals, and battling. At the end of the game, the player with the most points will rule the Meadow!

March of the Ants is played over several rounds, each of which has 4 distinct phases:

  • Worker Phase, where you take turns taking actions.
  • Soldier Phase, where battles are fought in contested locations.
  • Queen Phase, where you harvest resources and feed your ants.
  • Slumber, where you score points for your achievements during the round.
GAME SETUP
3-5 PLAYER SETUP Game Setup
  1. Place the Great Tunnel in the center of the table.
  2. Place the Score/Round Track on one side of the play area on the 4-round side and place the Strength Track beneath it. Place the Round Marker on Round 1 and the Centipede Size Marker on the space corresponding to the player count.
  3. Shuffle the starting hexes (#1-10) together and place 2 per player to form a facedown stack. Return the rest to the game box.
  4. Randomly select 2" Beyond the Meadow hexes (#24-28) and shuffle them with the standard hexes (#11-23). Place the starting hex stack from step 3 on top of these, and place the combined stack near the Great Tunnel. *With 4-5 players, add 1 extra Beyond the Meadow hex.
  5. Place the food, centipede, and wormhole tokens next to the play area.
  6. Place the Great Defender Rest Tile beneath the Round Track. Shuffle the other Rest Tiles and place them in a facedown stack nearby. Draw and place tiles faceup under the Score Track until there is 1 Rest Tile per player.
  7. Randomly select 2 Reward Tiles from each level. Place the two "I - II" tiles on top of the two "III+" tiles to form a facedown stack. Return the rest to the box.
  8. Reveal the top Reward Tile and place it faceup in the center of the Great Tunnel.
  9. Randomly select a starting player and give them the Active Player Marker.
  10. Shuffle the Ant Card deck. Place it near the play area.

Player Setup (each player)
  1. Choose a color. Take 36 ant tokens of that color and 1 Nest Mat with the star side up.
  2. Place 4 of your ant tokens into your Larvae Chamber as larvae and 2 in the Great Tunnel as ants. Place the rest on the side of your Nest Mat as eggs.
  3. Place your Score and Strength Markers on the spaces of their respective tracks, with the printed sides down.
  4. Place 2 food tokens into your Food Stores.
  5. Draw 3 cards. Advanced Variant: Each player draws 2 cards, plus their number in the player order (e.g. the 3rd player draws 5 cards). Each player selects 3 cards to keep. Shuffle and place the rest on bottom of the deck.
Ant, Larva, or Egg?

The wooden ant tokens represent different stages of the life cycle of ants, depending on where they are located. When tokens are in the Meadow (the Great Tunnel or hexes), they are ants. When they are in your Larvae Chamber, they are larvae. All other tokens are kept to the side of the Nest Mat as eggs.

GAIN LARVA

Take an egg from your supply and place it in your Larvae Chamber.

MOVE LARVA

Take a larva from your Larvae Chamber and place it into the Meadow, where it immediately becomes an ant.

LIMITED ANTS

Each player has a limit of 36 wooden ant tokens available to them each game. If they are all in use, the player cannot gain more larva or ants. We've included a few extras in the game to cover any manufacturing issues. Please set aside any extras to use as backup.

THE WORKER PHASE
Overview

During the Worker Phase, players take turns in clockwise order, starting with the player with the Active Player Marker. Play continues until all faceup Rest Tiles have been taken using the Rest action.

At the end of the Worker Phase, each player with an evolved thorax will get to take one or more bonus reactions.

Taking a Turn

On your turn, choose an action to perform.

ACTION – Explore, Forage, March, Play a Card, or Rest. You first pay the cost, then perform the action.

REACTIONS – After you take an action, the 2 neighboring players may take the associated reaction. Reactions have no cost and are taken in clockwise order from the Active Player. The player to your left goes first, then the player to the right.

After your action and the reactions are resolved, pass the Active Player Marker clockwise, and the next turn begins.

Worker Phase Actions

There are 5 Worker Phase actions that you may choose from on your turn. On the following pages, you'll find each of these explained in detail.

EXPLORE – Add a new hex to the Meadow, and move ants from adjacent connected locations to it. Reaction: Move a larva into the Meadow.

FORAGE – Draw 3 cards, then discard a card from your hand. Reaction: Gain a larva.

MARCH – Move your larvae to the Meadow and your ants around the Meadow using steps. Reaction: Take 1 ant step or move a larva to the Great Tunnel.

PLAY A CARD – Pay the cost for a card in your hand to play it. Reaction: Discard a card from your hand to draw a new card.

REST – Conclude your other actions for the round and begin taking Rest Tiles. You may not Rest on your first turn in a round. Reaction: None.

CONCEPTS: HEX RULES Placing a Hex

Anytime you place a hex in the Meadow, whether through Exploration, a card effect, or another ability:

  • You must connect at least one of its tunnel openings to an existing tunnel opening.
  • You may block its tunnels or the tunnels of other hexes. If the hex has a feature (wormhole or centipede) place the corresponding token on the hex. Resolve any special abilities.
Inhabiting a Hex

When you have at least one ant in a collection site in a hex, you inhabit that hex. Inhabiting a hex allows you to move larvae directly to that hex when moving larvae, and is required for many card effects and abilities.

Movement and Hexes

When you move an ant into a hex, it immediately inhabits an empty collection site of your choice. If none are empty, place the ant in the outskirts (anywhere else on that hex). When an ant leaves a collection site, any ant in the outskirts of that hex will immediately inhabit it at the end of the current action or reaction. If multiple players have ants in the outskirts, players take turns inhabiting as many of the empty sites as they can in player order.

Sharing & Contesting Hexes

Ants from multiple colonies may peacefully share a hex as long as there are enough collection sites for all the ants. A hex is shared when 2 or more players' ants inhabit that hex. If a hex is shared, but there are not enough sites for all the ants, that hex is also contested. During the Soldier Phase, there will be a battle in each contested hex.

CONCEPTS: GUIDE TO THE MEADOW

The Meadow is the game board where most of the action takes place in March of the Ants. It is made up of distinct locations, which include the Great Tunnel and all placed hexes. It will expand during the game as you Explore and place new hexes.

The Great Tunnel

The Great Tunnel is the center of the Meadow and the origin of all of the ant colonies. A battle will take place here during the Soldier Phase if multiple players' ants are present.

GREAT TUNNEL RULES
  • The Great Tunnel does not count as a hex.
  • Hexes adjacent to the Great Tunnel grant their controller during Slumber.
  • When moving larvae, players may always move them to the Great Tunnel.
Hexes

Hexes have different features, collection sites, and sometimes special rules. Ants in a hex will first inhabit collection sites but if all sites are full may reside in the outskirts.

  • COLLECTION SITE – Offers valuable resources that ants may collect during the Queen Phase. Each site may be inhabited by at most one ant.
  • CONTROL SITE – A type of collection site that also grants control of the hex. Control of a hex is important for scoring, and during battle.
  • OUTSKIRTS – Anywhere in the hex that is not a site. Ants may occupy the outskirts of a hex only if there are no empty collection sites in that hex. They do not inhabit the hex or harvest resources, but do participate in battle.
  • NAME AND NUMBER – Hex numbers are used in the Soldier Phase, and are color-coded according to their number. Hex names do not have any gameplay effect.
  • TUNNELS – Ants may move between adjacent locations if those locations have a connected tunnel.

  • BEYOND THE MEADOW EFFECTS – Beyond the Meadow hexes have unique effects. Each has its special effect written on it. The player who places the hex resolves any "when placed" effects before any players take reactions. Beyond the Meadow hexes are part of the Meadow, despite their name.

  • CENTIPEDES – Place a centipede token on the hex. Advance the Centipede Size by 1. To advance the Centipede Size, move the Centipede Size Marker down one space. Centipedes are threats to the ants. Ants may move into hexes with centipedes and inhabit collection sites as normal, but will have to battle the centipedes during the Soldier Phase.

  • WORMHOLES – Place a wormhole token on the hex. Ants may move between 2 hexes that have wormholes with a single step. Wormholes do not connect hexes for the purposes of card effects and exploration.

ACTIONS & REACTIONS

Actions are detailed here, and are shown in icon form on each player's nest mat.

Action Diagram
  1. The cost that must be paid before taking the action.
  2. The action you take.
  3. The reaction each neighboring player may take.
Explore

Cost: 1 food ACTION: Draw a hex and place it connected by a tunnel to an adjacent location where you have at least 1 ant. Draw the hex first, then choose where to place it. For full hex placement rules see page 6. Move any number of your ants from any adjacent connected locations into the explored hex. You must move at least 1 ant. Ants will immediately move into collection sites, or to the outskirts if all sites are full. REACTIONS: Each neighbor may move 1 larva from their Larvae Chamber to a hex they inhabit or to the Great Tunnel. You inhabit any hex where you have an ant in a collection site. When a larva enters the Meadow it becomes an ant.

Forage

Cost: 1 food ACTION: Draw 3 cards, then discard any 1 card from your hand. There is no limit to hand size. REACTION: Each neighbor may gain 1 larva. To gain a larva, move an egg from your supply to your Larvae Chamber. If you have no eggs in your supply, you may not gain a larva. If at any time you would draw a card and there are none left in the deck, shuffle the discard pile to make a new deck. Then draw from the new deck.

March

Cost: 1 food ACTION: Take 6 steps with your larvae and/or ants. REACTION: Each neighbor may take 1 ant step, or move 1 larva from their Larvae Chamber to the Great Tunnel. Taking steps is always optional. When you March you may take any number of steps up to 6.

CONCEPTS: STEPS

Steps let you move ants around the Meadow and move larvae into the Meadow, where they become ants. Taking steps is always optional. One step allows you to move:

  • 1 ant from any location to any adjacent connected location.
  • 1 ant from a location with a wormhole to any other location with a wormhole.
  • 1 ant from a collection site to another open collection site in the same hex.
  • 1 larva from your Larvae Chamber to any hex that you inhabit or the Great Tunnel.
ANT STEP

Abilities that instruct you to take ant steps only allow you to move ants, not larvae. You may use multiple steps to move 1 ant several times, or distribute them among several ants or larvae.

Play a Card

Cost: Shown on Card ACTION: Play one card from your hand. REACTION: Each neighbor may discard a card from their hand to draw a card. Playing a card does not cost food. To pay for a card, remove larvae and/or ants equal to the cost of the card from your Larvae Chamber or the Meadow and return them to your supply of eggs.

  • Pay with any combination of larvae from your Larvae Chamber and ants from the Meadow.
  • Pay only with ants from the Meadow.
  • Pay only with larvae from your Larvae Chamber. The card types, their different uses, and their effects are explained on pages 12 and 13.
Rest

Cost: None You may not Rest as your first action of a round, unless you have no food and no cards in hand. ACTION: Take 1 Rest Tile of your choice from the faceup Rest Tiles for the round and gain its rewards. You may only take the Rest action on subsequent turns. REACTION: None. Rest is a special action, taken when you are finished taking other actions for the round. Once you have Rested, you must Rest on each subsequent turn. Each time you Rest, choose an available Rest Tile and place it next to your Nest Mat. Some Rest Tiles will grant immediate rewards, while some grant bonuses or effects later in the round. A reference to each can be found on the back cover of these rules. When a player takes the last faceup Rest tile, they finish their turn, pass the Active Player Marker clockwise, and the Worker Phase ends. (See the following page for end of Worker Phase effects.) The player with the Active Player Marker keeps it for the rest of the round, and will go first in the next Worker Phase.

END OF THE WORKER PHASE

At the end of the Worker Phase, in player order starting with the Active Player, take reactions equal to the number of thorax Evolutions you have in play.

  • You may not choose the same reaction twice.
  • Each player takes all of their reactions before the next player takes any of theirs.
  • Choose reactions from the four standard actions: Explore, Forage, March, or Play a Card.

If you have more than 4 evolved thoraxes, you only take 4 reactions—one of each.

After all thorax reactions have been resolved, the Soldier Phase begins. (The Soldier Phase rules start on page 14).

CONCEPTS: GREAT TUNNEL DEFENDER

The Great Defender Rest Tile is available each round. It grants the player who has it the status of defender in the Great Tunnel. Being the defender provides benefits in battle: the defender wins ties, and has the opportunity to act last. (See page 14 for more details.)

Occasionally a player will take the Great Defender tile but will not be involved in the Great Tunnel Battle. In this sad case, they are still the defender for the purposes of determining the order of playing cards and breaking ties in the Great Tunnel, even though they do not participate in the battle.

THE SOLDIER PHASE
SOLDIER PHASE STEPS
  1. In the Great Tunnel, resolve battle if 2 or more players' ants are present. If only 1 player's ants are present, they gain the 1st-place award and no battle occurs.
  2. Resolve battles in contested hexes in ascending order of hex number. No hex battles occur in Round 1. Skip this step.
  3. After all battles are resolved, the Soldier Phase ends and the Queen Phase begins.
Contested Hexes

A hex is contested when there are 2 or more players' ants in the hex and there are ants in the outskirts, or if there is a centipede in a hex containing any ants. If a contested hex becomes uncontested before a battle starts there, the battle will not occur.

ANT BATTLE

Ant battle is fought between all players with ants in a contested location. Only one ant battle occurs in each location during the Soldier Phase, even if the location is still contested after battle.

THE CONTEST: STRENGTH, FEROCITY AND OUTCOME

  1. Determine Defender In hexes, the player who inhabits the control site is the defender. In the Great Tunnel, it is the player with the Great Defender Rest Tile.

  2. Declare Strength Each player counts their ants in the hex, and adds the number of head Evolutions they have. They set their marker to that number on the Strength Track.

  3. Ferocity Starting with the player to the left of the defender and proceeding clockwise, each player in the battle may play 1 card from their hand facedown. REVEAL FEROCITY – All players reveal their cards and add the number of pips shown in the symbol to their Strength, adjusting their position on the Strength Track.

  4. Outcome The player with the greatest Strength is the winner. In the Great Tunnel, also determine 2nd and 3rd place based on Strength. Ties: Starting with the defender, ties are won in clockwise order. Only one player can win. The other players, including the 2nd and 3rd place players, lose.

THE RESULTS: SPOILS AND CASUALTIES

  1. Casualties Players assign casualties to each other, and then lose ants. Losing Players: For every 2 ants, assign 1 casualty to the winner. Count all losing players' ants together. Winning Player: For each ant, assign 1 casualty, distributed as evenly as possible to losing players in clockwise order from the winner. CASUALTY RULES – • Lone Survivor: The winner cannot lose their last ant in the location. • Each player may only be assigned enough casualties to remove all of their ants. For instance, if a player has 3 ants, plus the Armored Head (which prevents one casualty) they may be assigned 4 casualties maximum. Once all casualties have been assigned, each player loses 1 ant of their choice from the battle location for each casualty they were assigned.

  2. Spoils Players collect their spoils: Hexes: The winner gains and may move 1 ant in the hex into the control site, pushing any occupant to the outskirts. The Great Tunnel: 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place players gain the spoils shown on the reward tile. Players gain spoils even if all their ants were removed during Casualties.

AFTER BATTLE
  1. Cleanup Players take turns moving all of their ants in the outskirts of that hex to vacant collection sites in clockwise order starting with the winner. Reset the markers on the Strength Track to zero. Discard all cards played during battle.
THE QUEEN PHASE

During the Queen Phase, you Harvest, Feed Ants, and then make your Queen's Choice.

Harvest

Gain resources from collection sites that your ants inhabit.

  • Add 1 food to your stores.
  • Add 1 or 2 larvae to your Larvae Chamber (as shown on the collection site).
  • Draw a card.
  • Advance your Score Marker 1 space. To help correctly collect your harvest, we recommend counting each type of resource separately.
Feed Ants

All ants in the Meadow need to be fed to survive. Larvae do not need to be fed. Each food can feed up to 4 ants, plus 1 ant for each abdomen Evolution you have. If you have one abdomen Evolution, you would be able to feed 5 ants with 1 food, up to 10 ants with 2 food, and so on. You may choose not to feed any number of your ants, but you will lose any ants you do not feed, returning them to your egg supply. Starvation: If you fail to feed one or more ants, you lose (total, not per ant). Additionally, if you fail to feed any ants in Autumn you will not be eligible for endgame awards.

Queen's Choice

After all ants have been fed, you decide if your queen will produce food, larvae, or a mix of both to prepare the colony for the next round. Each player chooses twice to gain 1 food or 3 larvae. There is no Queen's Choice in the final round. If needed, resolve the Queen's Choice in clockwise order starting with the active player.

SLUMBER

During Slumber, you score points and refresh the board for the next round.

Score Inner Meadow Hexes

Gain 1 for each Inner Meadow hex you control. All hexes that are adjacent to the Great Tunnel are part of the Inner Meadow. They do not need to be connected to the Great Tunnel to score.

Score Colony Goals

You can gain 1, 2, or 3 points for meeting the conditions of the Colony Goal cards you have in play. The number of points you gain is based on how well you meet the condition, as shown on the card. (See page 12 for more details.)

Prepare For The Next Round

If it is the final round, the game ends. Otherwise, advance the Round Marker one space and complete the following steps to prepare for the next round:

  1. Return the Great Defender Rest Tile to the area beneath the Round Track.
  2. Discard all other Rest Tiles in a faceup discard stack. Reveal new Rest Tiles until there is 1 tile per player beneath the Round Track.
    • If all Rest Tiles have been used, shuffle all discarded Rest Tiles to form a new stack.
  3. Discard the current Reward Tile. Reveal the topmost Reward Tile and place it faceup in the Great Tunnel.
  4. The next Worker Phase begins. The player who was passed the Active Player Marker at the end of the last Worker Phase takes the first turn.
GAME END AND VICTORY

After the final round, determine the winner of each endgame award and declare a winner!

ENDGAME AWARDS

Compare resources for endgame awards. Each award grants [icon] to the player who wins it. If players tie for an award, each tied player gains [icon]. Players who failed to feed any of their ants in Autumn are ineligible for endgame awards. If they would win an award, it goes to the player with the next most.

The awards go to the player who has:

LARGEST BROOD

The most larvae.

GREATEST STORES

The most food.

UNTOLD POTENTIAL

The most cards in hand.

The player with the highest score wins! In case of a tie, the player with the most ants in the Meadow wins. If still tied, all tied players share the victory!

2-PLAYER RULES

For a 2-player game of March of the Ants, follow these additional rules.

Additional Setup

After completing the normal setup, take these extra steps:

  1. Beginning with the Active Player, each player takes 2 of the unused starting hexes at random. Each player chooses 1 hex and places it connected to the Great Tunnel. Return the unchosen hex to the game box.
  2. Each player places 3 ants into their placed hex.
  3. Set aside the ants and Strength Marker of an unused color for the Old Guard.
Worker Phase

At the start of each Worker Phase, reveal 1 additional Rest Tiles. You’ll use 3 Rest Tiles each round–the Great Defender Tile, and two additional ones.

ADVANCED VARIANTS

We highly recommend playing without the advanced modes for your first few games.

EPIC GAME

The epic game of March of the Ants is played over 5 rounds rather than 4. It follows all the standard rules of the game with a few adjustments. The Epic game adds about 10 minutes per player to the game length. In addition, the extra round gives colonies a chance to develop into larger, more complex engines. Players should have experience with the base game, a good understanding of the economy and how to feed their ants, and be familiar with the different ways to score points. • During setup, flip the Score Track to the 5-round side and add 1 additional "II+" Reward Tile to the bottom of the stack. • In Rounds 4 and 5, each food feeds up to 3 ants per food instead of 4 (before adding abdomen bonuses).

ANCIENT SPECIES

Ancient Species is an advanced play variant that allows you to begin the game with unique abilities, drawbacks, and scoring conditions. Each Ancient Species encourages certain styles of play and rewards you for utilizing its powers. This variant adds about 10-15 minutes per player to the game length. It also adds considerable complexity, extra unpredictability, and big power swings to the game. Players will need an excellent understanding of the base game rules, round structure, and resource management to succeed using Ancient Species.

Setup

Randomly assign a Nest Mat to each player. Each player plays as the Ancient Species found on the backside of the Nest Mat.

Gameplay

The game follows the standard rules and structure. Each Ancient Species shows the new rules that apply to it.

UNIQUE ACTIONS

Each Ancient Species has a unique action you can use once per Worker Phase. These actions give a Global Reaction, meaning that all other players, not just the neighboring players, get to take the reaction. At the start of each Worker Phase, place your Strength Marker in the circular space above your unique action to track whether you have used it. Return it to the Strength Track when you use your unique action, or at the start of the Soldier Phase.

FEATURES

Features are other abilities or penalties that apply to the species. Each Ancient Species has a drawback, and some have a special way to score points during Slumber.

Unique Action Clarifications
Egg Gardeners

Reaction: Choose one of the played Evolution cards and gain larvae equal to its cost. If no evolutions are played for reactions, you do not gain any larvae. If an ability lets you play more than one Ferocity card, you will lose a point for each. You do not lose [icon] for playing cards for Ferocity in the Great Tunnel Battle.

Horizon Seekers

Expand counts as a single Explore action for purposes of cards or other effects. Abilities that trigger off of Explore actions (Harvesting or Pollinator Thorax) only apply to one of the explored hexes. If you place both hexes, they must be connected to the same hex and you must move at least 1 ant into each of them.

Visionary Queen

You may have any number of Colony Goals in play but once you have 2 or more, you may only add additional Colony Goals by using your unique action. If you play a Colony Goal in any other way, you must replace one of your current ones. The additional cost to Evolve multiple times is always 1 ant or larvae, regardless of the cost requirement of the card you are playing.

Chaos Dancers

You must take the steps before resolving the card effect.

CARD, HEX, AND RULES CLARIFICATIONS
CARD & HEX CLARIFICATIONS Event Cards

ADAPTIVE RADIATION – If this card causes you to lose and then complete an ant body you do not lose points or score points as a result. INTO THE UNKNOWN – There may only be one wormhole per hex. There may only be 1 centipede per hex. MATING FLIGHT – You may choose to exchange the location of any player's ant, including your own, with that of any other player's ant. SNEAK ATTACK – This card will not cause a hex battle to take place in round 1. Battle in the hex resolves as it normally would in the Soldier Phase, at which point the Worker Phase continues. If there is a centipede in the hex, it will battle until it is defeated, then, if the hex is still contested, resolve one ant battle.

Evolution Cards

AMAZON HEAD – You gain a larva even if no opponents have larva to lose. ARMORED HEAD – This does not prevent your last ant in a hex from being lost, as long as enough casualties are assigned to you. This does not prevent you from losing all your ants when you lose to a centipede. ARMY HEAD – You may move 1 larva from your Larvae Chamber or 1 ant from an adjacent location into the current battle. BETRAYER ABDOMEN/TRAITOROUS HEAD – Instead of battling a centipede in a hex you are in, you may choose to lose 1 ant from that hex. If you do, other players in that hex still battle the centipede. BLASTING HEAD – When ants are removed, the player whose ants are removed decides which ants are lost. If this card's effect causes a hex to become uncontested, no battle will take place there. You cannot choose to remove less than 3 ants, even if it means removing your own. This may be used in Round 1. FLYING THORAX – This does not allow you to move larvae to hexes you do not inhabit or place an explored hex with no connecting tunnels. GARDEN THORAX – The cost of cards cannot be reduced to zero. GOLDEN FIRE HEAD – You must choose to use this ability immediately after you play your standard Ferocity card. MARCHING THORAX – This does not double March reactions when taken as a Thorax bonus reaction. PARASITIC THORAX – If 2 or more opponents have an equal amount of larvae, steal 1 larva from each of them. Steal means the opponent loses 1 larva and you gain 1. RENEGADE THORAX – Centipedes may not end their movement in the Great Tunnel or a hex with another centipede. SCOUTING THORAX – You do not need to show the unexplored hex to other players. TUNNELING THORAX – You cannot place an explored hex with no connecting tunnels. Because you always inhabit hexes with wormholes, you may move larvae directly to those hexes with Explore reactions or with 1 step. WEAVER THORAX – Your opponents cannot use any actions or card effects to move ants out of hexes you control, including effects that would move your ants. This includes effects that exchange ants. They may use ants in those hexes to pay for the cost of cards.

Colony Goal Cards

EXTENSIVE TUNNEL – "Hexes connected by a single tunnel" refers to a group of hexes that an ant could traverse without retracing its steps. EYELESS OVERLORDS – There may only be 1 wormhole per hex.

Beyond the Meadow Hexes

ABANDONED SPIDER'S LAIR – The player who places this hex may choose not to play any of the revealed cards. ANT LION DEN – Only discard cards for inhabited sites, not for the total number of ants in the hex. NEST OF CENTIPEDES – There can only be 1 centipede in each hex at any time. If all connected hexes already contain a centipede token, no centipede token is placed.

GENERAL RULES CLARIFICATIONS

ACTIVE PLAYER – The player with the Active Player Marker. CENTIPEDES – A hex can only have 1 centipede token on it at a time. Centipedes may not be placed in the Great Tunnel. CONTESTED HEX – If multiple players have ants in the same hex and there are ants in the outskirts, it is contested. If a centipede and ants are in the same hex, it is contested. If a hex is contested during Soldier Phase, a battle takes place there. CONTROL SITE – A site in a hex that gives the occupying ant's player control over that hex, allowing them to win ties in battle and gain 1 point in Slumber if the hex is adjacent to the Great Tunnel. EXCESS CASUALTIES – Once all casualties have been assigned, any extra have no effect. GREAT DEFENDER REST TILE – This Rest Tile is available each round. The player with this tile counts as the defender in the Great Tunnel Battle, even if they are not present in the battle. Ties are broken in clockwise order, starting with them, and Ferocity card play starts with the player to their left. THE GREAT TUNNEL – The central location of the Meadow. You may always move larvae into the Great Tunnel. A battle will take place there during Soldier Phase if ants from 2 or more different colonies are present. The Great Tunnel is not a hex. HAND – A player's cards. There is no hand size limit. The number of cards each player has is public knowledge. HEX BATTLE – A battle that takes place in a hex. (As opposed to the Great Tunnel Battle, which is not a hex battle.) INHABIT – A hex is inhabited by each player who has at least one ant in a collection site in that hex. THE MEADOW – Consists of the Great Tunnel and all placed hexes. MOVING LARVAE – When a larva moves to the Meadow, it immediately becomes an ant. When taking steps, you may move larva directly to any hex you inhabit or the Great Tunnel. LOCATION – Each hex and the Great Tunnel. LOSE ANTS – Remove your own ants from the Meadow. When you are instructed to lose ants, as a result of a card effect or battle casualties, you choose which ants to lose. OUTER MEADOW HEX – A hex that is not adjacent to the Great Tunnel. OUTSKIRTS – Any place in a hex that is not a collection site. If all collection sites in a hex are inhabited, any ant that enters that hex is placed in the outskirts. Ants in the outskirts of a hex automatically move to inhabit collection sites that become empty as soon as the current action or reaction is resolved. PLAYER ORDER – The order in which turns are taken. Player order is clockwise and starts with the Active Player unless specified otherwise. PLAY PRIORITY – Unless specified, resolve questions of priority in player order. REACTIONS – Players only take reactions to their neighbors' main action that they take on their turn. Players do not take reactions to action-like effects that result from cards or other abilities. SHARED HEX – A hex that 2 or more players inhabit. WORMHOLE HEX – A hex that contains a wormhole token is sometimes referred to as a wormhole hex. WORMHOLES – A location with a wormhole token has a wormhole. Ants may move from any hex with a wormhole to any other hex with a wormhole with 1 step. Ants may not Explore through wormholes.

Worker Phase Actions

EXPLORE – Add a new hex to the Meadow. You may move ants from adjacent connected locations into it (you must move at least 1 ant). Reaction: Move a larva into the Meadow. FORAGE – Draw 3 cards, then discard a card from your hand. Reaction: Gain a larva. MARCH – Move your larvae to the Meadow and your ants around the Meadow using steps. Reaction: Take 1 ant step or move a larva to the Great Tunnel. PLAY A CARD – Pay the cost for a card in your hand to play it. Reaction: Discard a card from your hand to draw a new card. REST – Conclude your other actions for the round and begin taking Rest Tiles. You may not Rest on your first turn in a round. Reaction: None.

Segment Bonuses

For each head, gain +1 Strength in battle. At the end of the Worker Phase, choose 1 different reaction per thorax. Each food feeds 1 additional ant per abdomen.

Rest Tiles Immediate Effects

Place 1 egg in the Great Tunnel. Take it from your supply, not from your Larvae Chamber. Take the indicated number of ant steps. You may lose 1 ant to gain the indicated reward. Gain the indicated resource(s).

Other Effects

Great Defender Tile: You are the defender in the Great Tunnel. Available each round. You have +1 Ferocity in all battles this round. 2 of your ants do not need to be fed this round. They are considered fed.

Card Icons

The Ferocity number. Used when played in battle, and when resolving some card effects. Immediate effect when played. Effect that takes place during Slumber. Head Thorax Abdomen

Great Tunnel Rules

• The Great Tunnel does not count as a hex. • Hexes adjacent to the Great Tunnel grant to their controller during Slumber. • When moving larvae, players may always move them to the Great Tunnel.
