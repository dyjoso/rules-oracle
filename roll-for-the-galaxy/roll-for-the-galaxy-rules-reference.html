Core Objective & Components

The player who best manages their workers to create the greatest space empire of worlds, developments, and victory point chips wins.

Die Type Phase Specialization
Home (White) Explore
Military (Red) Develop, Settle
Consumption (Purple) Ship (x3)
Novelty (Blue) Produce, Ship
Rare Elements (Brown) Develop
Genes (Green) Settle, Wild
Alien (Yellow) Wild (x3)

Core components include game tiles (worlds and developments), dice (workers), Galactic Credits, VP chips, player screens, phase strips, player mats, dice cups, and phase tiles.

Setup
Component Placement

Place the 55 game tiles in the bag, all dice, the VP chips (common pool), and the phase tiles (X side up, in numeric order) in the center of the play area.

Victory Point Pool Configuration

Use 12 VP chips per player, distributed as follows: 2 players require 24 VPs, 3 players require 36 VPs, 4 players require 48 VPs, and 5 players require 60 VPs. Set all 10VP chips aside for use only in the final round. Put the remaining VP chips away.

Player Initialization

Give each player a dice cup, a credit marker, a player mat (place the marker on the 1 [$] space), a screen, and a phase strip. Give each player one faction tile and one home world tile, chosen at random. Return the rest to the box. Each player places these tiles face-up in front of their mat to form their initial tableau.

Starting Tile Allocation

Each player draws two tiles from the bag and places them in their construction zone. Put one tile development-side-up on the left and one tile world-side-up on the right. You may choose which side of each double-sided tile faces up. You cannot place both starting tiles in the same construction zone stack.

Starting Dice Allocation

Put 3 Home dice in your dice cup. Put 2 Home dice in your Citizenry. Take all dice granted by worlds in your empire and place them as indicated on the tile (in the Citizenry, dice cup, or as a good on that world). Do not take dice for the world in your construction zone, as it grants dice only when settled and moved into your tableau. Dice from your faction and home world are taken only during setup. The home world Doomed World grants no dice; instead, its player starts with 8 Galactic Credits. The faction Destroyed Colony has two worlds, both of which grant dice.

Round Flow
Roll & Assign Rolling Workers

Players simultaneously roll all workers (dice in their cups) behind their screens. Play is simultaneous; there are no separate player turns.

Assigning to Phases

Initially, assign workers to phases by placing them below the phase strip in columns, matching each die face rolled with the phase symbol. All wild faces are wild and can be assigned to any phase. Multiple wild dice can go under the same or different columns. A die's column, not its faces, determines which worker it is. You may assign workers that show wild faces, though it rarely makes sense to do so. You may also assign a die without a given phase symbol to that phase.

Phase Selection & Dictate

Each player selects a phase by taking one worker and placing it on any space on the phase strip. Only selected phases occur. This assigns the worker to the selected phase; the player now ignores the die's face and treats it as if its face was the selected phase. Each player may select only one phase. The die placed on the phase strip remains a worker.

All players have one built-in Reassign power called Dictate, shown on the phase strip. To Dictate, place any worker to the right of the phase strip in the Dictate area and then Reassign another worker to a different column. The worker in the Dictate area will be returned to the cup during the Reveal step. You must select a phase before using Reassign powers.

Reassign Mechanics

Some developments have powers that allow a player to Reassign workers to other phases, placing those dice in other columns below the phase strip. Each Reassign power can be used at most once per round. Many powers are optional, as indicated by "may" in their descriptions. A Reassign power that assigns multiple workers can assign them to different columns. You cannot Reassign a die in the Dictate area. Move a Reassigned worker to its new column without rotating it to show a new face. All workers must be assigned to phases before screens are lifted.

Reveal & Phase Resolution Revealing Selections

Each player lifts their screen to reveal their assigned workers, announcing the phase they have selected. Flip the matching phase tile from its X side to its black side. In a 2-player game, roll a spare Home die. If the rolled face is a phase that was not selected by either player, flip that phase tile over as well. This phase will also occur this round. Return to players' cups any workers assigned to X phases and any workers next to the Dictate area.

Phase Execution Order

Simultaneously, players do all selected phases in numeric order. Do not perform any phase not selected this round. To do a phase, use all workers assigned to it, including any worker that selected it. All workers perform their tasks sequentially, one at a time. Players do not have to declare in advance what tasks their workers will do. Complete one worker's task before deciding which task the next worker will do. Workers that complete tasks go to the Citizenry. Unused workers return to the dice cup.

Timing Exceptions

Most play can and should be done simultaneously. However, strict clockwise player order applies only when a player might build to 12 or more tile squares (triggering game end) or when the VP chip pool might be exhausted (triggering game end). When strict timing is required, do the phase in clockwise order starting with the player with the lowest numbered faction tile.

Manage Empire Recruiting Workers

Recruit workers by selecting dice from the Citizenry, putting them in your dice cup, and decreasing your Galactic Credits by 1 for each die recruited. You must recruit as many dice as you can afford from your Citizenry. Do this until either your Citizenry is empty or your credits run out. If you have fewer credits than dice in your Citizenry, you freely choose which dice to recruit. After recruiting, if your credits are at 0, shift the token to 1. Track credits on the mat by shifting the Galactic Credit marker. A player may never have more than $10 at a time; any excess credits are lost.

Recalling Dice

You may recall any developers, settlers (from their construction zone stacks), and goods (from their worlds). Put them in your dice cup. Recalling dice does not cost Galactic Credits. If you have no dice in your cup after recruiting, you must recall at least one die.

Resetting & Game End Check

Flip all phase tiles to their X sides. Check game end conditions. If either the VP chip pool was exhausted or any player has 12 or more tile squares in their tableau, the game is over.

Phase Actions
Phase I: Explore Scout Tasks

Use each explorer to either Scout or Stock. Put the die in the Citizenry after using it. To Scout, after possibly abandoning some tiles from your construction zone, draw 1 or more tiles from the bag and examine them. Draw 1 more tile than the number of tiles abandoned. You may simply draw 1 tile; you need not abandon tiles. Then, in any order, choose whether each tile will be a development or world and put it under its construction zone stack. Tiles can be abandoned from any position in your construction zone stacks. Put abandoned tiles under the Explore phase tile. You may abandon tiles every time you Scout, including tiles drawn in earlier Scout tasks. At the end of the Explore phase, return all abandoned tiles to the bag.

Stock Tasks

To Stock, add 2 Galactic Credits to your total.

Construction Zone Management

During Explore, developers and settlers on construction zone stacks stay on top of their stacks, even if a stack is emptied due to abandoning tiles. The rule that both construction zone stacks must have one tile applies only during setup, not during play.

Phase II: Develop Completing Developments

Put each developer, one at a time, on the top-most tile in the development construction zone stack. Once the number of developers equals the top-most development's cost, the development is complete. Move its tile to the tableau and put the developers who completed it in the Citizenry. Use any remaining developers to build the next tile in the stack. A player may complete more than one development in a Develop phase. If any developers are left after completing all developments in the construction zone, return them to the dice cup. You must use all applicable discounts you have. The minimum number of workers to place a tile is 1, not 0, even if discounts would reduce it below 1.

Handling Pre-Completed Tiles

Occasionally, due to Explore powers or Scouting, the topmost development might be complete during an Explore phase. Do not move its tile to the tableau or return its developers until a Develop phase occurs. At the start of the next Develop phase, resolve completed developments, choosing which developers to return to the Citizenry.

Discount & Power Rules

Most developments grant a power once built, usable either during the Assign step or in the phase(s) shown. Powers are mandatory unless their text includes "may". Powers are used, if applicable, in a phase that occurs even if you have no workers assigned to it. Three developments have immediate effects instead, similar to worlds.

Phase III: Settle Completing Worlds

Put each settler, one at a time, on the top-most tile in the world construction zone stack. Once the number of settlers equals the top-most world's cost, the world is complete. Move its tile to the tableau and put the settlers who completed it in the Citizenry. Use any remaining settlers to build the next tile in the stack. A player may complete more than one world in a Settle phase. If any settlers are left after completing all worlds in the construction zone, return them to the dice cup. The minimum number of workers to place a tile is 1, not 0, even if discounts would reduce it below 1.

Immediate World Effects

Each world has an immediate effect, shown on its tile. It occurs once upon placing the tile. All worlds grant dice. Some worlds also give Galactic Credits. For immediate effects that involve removing one of your dice, return it from any location to the center. Removing a die from your cup, phase strip, construction zone, or any of your worlds has no effects beyond its removal. If you remove the only die used to select a phase, that phase still occurs.

Handling Pre-Completed Worlds

Occasionally, due to Scouting or development powers, the topmost world might be complete during an Explore phase. Do not move its tile to the tableau or return its settlers until a Settle phase occurs. At the start of the next Settle phase, resolve completed worlds, choosing which settlers to return to the Citizenry.

Phase IV: Produce Placing Producers

Each producer creates a good on a non-gray world in the player's tableau. The producer die becomes the good; put it on the world. Any producer die of any color can become a good on any non-gray world. Matching a good's color with the world's color matters only if the good will be Consumed, not if it will be Traded.

World Capacity & Excess Dice

Each world can have at most 1 good. Return any excess producers to the dice cup.

Phase V: Ship Trade Tasks

Each shipper takes a good from a world in the player's tableau and either Traded or Consumes it. Put both dice (the shipper and the good) in the Citizenry. Each Trade earns a player Galactic Credits according to the color of the good's world (not the color of the dice): Novelty worlds trade for 3, Rare Elements worlds trade for 4, Genes worlds trade for 5, and Alien Technology worlds trade for 6. Some powers further increase these amounts.

Consume Tasks

Each Consume earns the shipping player 1 to 3 VP chips: 1 VP chip always, +1 VP chip if the good's color matches the world it is on, and +1 VP chip if the shipper's color matches the color of the good's world. Some powers further increase the number of VP chips earned or even earn the player Galactic Credits.

Consumption Dice & Color Matching

A Consumption (purple) die, used as either the good or the shipper, matches all colors and always earns +1 VP. Using 2 Consumption dice for both the good and the shipper will always earn both VP bonuses. Wild faces do not provide extra VP chips when used in Consume; only the colors of the dice involved matter for Consume. You may deliberately mismatch goods and shippers to earn fewer VP chips when you Consume.

Scoring & Game End
Game End Triggers

The game ends after completing the entire round if either of the following conditions is met: the initial pool of VP chips is exhausted (add the set-aside 10 VP chips to the pool if this occurs during play to continue earning VPs), or at least one player has 12 or more tile squares in their tableau. Each player's faction and home world tiles together count as 3 tile squares towards the 12 needed to trigger game end. A player can end with more than 12 tile squares by building several tiles during the final round.

Final Scoring Calculation

Total each player's score by adding: VP chips, VPs equal to the costs of all developments and worlds in the tableau (include both numbers from a faction tile, do not include any tiles in the construction zone), and bonus VPs for 6-cost developments beyond the 6 VPs for the development itself. When figuring these bonuses, round any fraction up to the next whole number. Some 6-cost developments count towards their own bonuses.

Tiebreaker Procedure

If two or more players tie, each tied player adds the number of dice in their cup and their Galactic Credits. The player with the highest total among the tied players wins. If there is still a tie, all tied players win. Recall dice on the final round to increase the number of dice in your cup for tiebreaker purposes, as workers in the construction zone or goods do not count.

Power & Tile Reference
Reassign Powers

Reassign powers can be used only during the Assign step. Each Reassign power can be used at most once per round. A Reassign power that assigns multiple workers can assign them to different columns. You cannot Reassign a die in the Dictate area. You may use Reassign powers to affect workers that show wild faces. You may use a Reassign power to assign a die without a given phase symbol to that phase. Only a die's column, not its faces, determines which of the five possible workers it is. Powers can affect dice assigned to a phase whose faces don't match the symbols shown on the dice in the power's icon.

Immediate Effects

Immediate effects occur once upon placing the tile. When removing a die as part of an immediate effect, you can remove any of your dice. Removing a die from your cup, phase strip, construction zone, or any of your worlds has no effects beyond its removal. If you remove the only die used to select a phase, that phase still occurs.

Continuing Effects

Continuing effects persist after a development is built. Once built, you may ignore the restriction that each non-gray world can have at most one good. Instead, each of your non-gray worlds can have up to two goods of either the same or different colors. Each good still requires 1 shipper to Ship.

General Power Activation Rules

Powers are mandatory unless their text includes "may". You do not need workers assigned to a phase to trigger powers that occur in it. Powers are used, if applicable, in a phase that occurs even if you have no workers assigned to it. You can use a development's power in the phase you build it. A power can be used as many times per phase as it is triggered. When a power instructs you to round up, treat any partial amount as one full unit. Consumption (purple) dice do not count as other die colors when evaluating powers or during Game End scoring. They match colors only for earning VP chips during Consume.

Quick FAQ

What happens if a player doing a Scout task needs to draw more tiles than are in the bag? After drawing all the tiles in the bag, the player finishes this Scout task by taking all the abandoned tiles under the Explore phase tile, putting them in the bag, and drawing the rest of the needed tiles. If there are still not enough tiles, the Scouting player places all drawn tiles in their construction zone. Then, each player with at least 3 tiles in their construction zone must abandon 1 tile into the bag. The Scouting player then draws tiles to finish the task.

When must strict clockwise timing be used instead of simultaneous play? Strict timing is required only when a player might build to 12 or more tile squares or when the VP chip pool might be exhausted. In these cases, resolve the phase in clockwise order starting with the player with the lowest numbered faction tile.

Are recruitment and recall mandatory under all circumstances? You must recruit as many dice as you can afford from your Citizenry. You must recall at least one die if you have no dice in your cup after recruiting. You may only recall dice during the Manage Empire step.

Can players deliberately mismatch colors to earn fewer VPs during Consume? Yes, deliberately mismatching goods and shippers to earn fewer VP chips can occasionally make strategic sense.

What is the difference between workers, shippers, and goods? Workers are dice in a player's cup, below a player's phase strip, or being used to select a phase. Dice that are unowned, in a player's Citizenry, or in Dictate areas are not workers. Based on the phase they are assigned to, workers become explorers, developers, settlers, producers, or shippers. Producers put onto worlds become goods.
