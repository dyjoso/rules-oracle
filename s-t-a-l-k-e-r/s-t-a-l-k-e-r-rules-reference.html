Starting the Campaign: Step-by-Step

To start a new Campaign, perform the following tasks in order:

  1. Learn about the game: Review the rulebook chapters: Zone Exploration, Mission Setup, Mission Map, and Playing the Mission.
  2. Check the Gameplay Example: Play through the short scenario to verify understanding of core mechanics.
  3. Prepare Core Components: Take out the Campaign binder, Stash (plastic envelope), and Zone Map from the box. These are used during Zone Exploration.
  4. Slot Starting Cards: Find Contact cards C01 (Sidorovich), C08 (Guide), and C99 (Patron) in the box. Place them in any order into the slots of the Campaign binder’s cardholder.
  5. Choose Stalkers: Each player chooses 1 Stalker card and 1 Stalker model. Models are universal; choose whichever you prefer. Recommended first-playthrough Stalkers: Borsuk, Mamon, Milady, Velel. These Stalkers remain with you for the whole Campaign. Each player then chooses a color and takes the corresponding components: PDA, Attention token, Plastic ring. Place the plastic ring on the model. Place the Stalker card in the middle of the PDA. Place the Attention token in the slot near Pockets.
  6. Gain Starting Equipment: Gain 5000 [icon] regardless of team size and place them in your Stash to be split between your team. Additionally, each Stalker starts with a Leather Jacket Armor card (take 1 per Stalker from the deck and place it in your Stash).
  7. Ready to Start: Open the Campaign binder on the first page (Zone Navigation) and follow its instructions to begin.

Replaying the Campaign
Before starting a second or subsequent Campaign, reset all components. Use a new Zone Map and reset the contents of all Mission boxes using the full component lists found in the Mission Scriptbook pages 4-5.

Mission Guide Structure & Setup Workflow

During each Mission, you must set up the board according to the Mission guide. The guide is split into three sections:

Prologue
Read this when you choose a Mission on the Zone Map. It contains story introduction and hints for preparation. Choosing a Mission does not start it immediately; you will return to the Campaign binder to explore further, visit Traders, or meet Contacts before starting.

Setup
This section contains all instructions to prepare the Mission Map. Mission-specific cards and tokens are in the corresponding Mission box. Follow the two-page setup instructions step-by-step. On these pages, you only see the connection between setup instructions and the prepared map; all detailed rules are in the Rulebook.

Example Setup Workflow (Mission A1):

  1. Map Setup: Place specified Map Tiles (1-5) as shown. Place starting overlay cards (A1-02a, A1-03a, etc.) on the map. Keep remaining Environment cards next to the map as the Environment deck.
  2. Environment Setup: Take out all Environment cards from the Mission box. Find and read the Mission Objective card (A1-0a), place it next to the map. Randomly select one of each duplicate card (A1-05a, A1-07a) and return the other copies to the box. Draw 1 random Gravitational Anomaly card and 1 Electro Anomaly card face up next to the map. Draw 1 random Gravitational overlay (3, 5, or 8) and place it on the map pointing top. Draw 1 random Electric overlay (6, 7, or 11) and place it so the triangle tip points to each Anomaly center. Place matching Anomaly standees on those overlays.
  3. Other Setups: Place Stalker models on "Starting Point" spaces. For Enemies: Find specific Enemy cards (e.g., Bandit Rifle, Bandit Shotgun), mark them with [icon] tokens, and place them next to the map. Place corresponding standees on marked spaces. For the Event Deck: Take all Mission Event cards from the box. Randomly return one duplicate (II), stack the rest in numerical order face down (I on top, VIII on bottom). Leave the Random Event card in the box. The Mission begins; start the first Round.

Note: Not all components in a Mission box are used during setup. Only use what is instructed. Full contents of each Mission box are listed in the Scriptbook pages 4-5 for reference or reset purposes.

Epilogue
Read this after finishing a Mission. It continues the story and details rewards (Equipment, Coupons, Contacts, stickers adding points of interest to the Zone Map, unlocking new Missions).

Optional Modes & Expansion Setup

Single Stalker Mode
Recommended for solo play to preserve atmosphere. Play with only 1 Stalker.

  • Turn Overlays: Place special Turn overlays on your PDA. This allows you to perform 3 Standard Actions instead of 2 per Turn, for a total of 6 per Round. Each Enemy with LoS to your Stalker Attacks your Stalker twice during the Enemies & Zone Phase.
  • Lead Stalker: You are always the Lead Stalker and never lose the token. When you perform the [icon] Action from the Lead Stalker token, flip it; it is unflipped by the Event card's End of Round effect.
  • Critical Injury: Place Critical Injuries in the same spot as usual. On the Turn overlay, a Critical Injury covers 2 slots, causing you to lose 2 Standard Actions during that Turn.

Lost Territories
An optional expansion adding new areas for Campaigns or Zone Survival Scenarios. Use after gaining experience with the game.

  • Components: 8 Setup cards, 13 Lost Territories Environment cards, 3 Lost Territory tiles, 2 Entrance tokens.
  • Adding to Game: After finishing Map setup for a Mission/Scenario, draw 1 Lost Territories Setup card and resolve it. It specifies where to place the Entrance token, which tile to use, and any additional Environment cards. Environment cards start with "L".
  • Reaching the Territory: Each tile has an Entrance. Place the Entrance token on the Map as instructed. Spaces with a Lost Territories Entrance are adjacent for Movement and Range measurement, but you cannot draw Line of Sight through the Entrance token. You can throw Grenades/Bolts across, Enemies can follow, but Weapons requiring LoS cannot be used.
  • Setup Instructions: Draw 1 random Setup card before Round 1. It shows where to place the Entrance token (players decide if multiple spaces available), which tile to place next to the Map, which L-01a Environment card to use, and reminder instructions for traversal.
Campaign & Mission Overview
Game Identity & Golden Rules

S.T.A.L.K.E.R. Universe
The game takes place in an alternate version of the Chornobyl Nuclear Power Plant Exclusion Zone, formed after the 1986 accident and a second incident in the 2000s. The mutated irradiated area hosts anomalies, mutants, and warring factions (Monolith, Duty, Freedom, traders, bandits). Players take on the role of Stalkers exploring for artifacts, personal motivations, or survival. The Zone is a hostile, living microcosmos.

Golden Rules
In unclear situations, follow these rules:

  1. Cards before Rulebook: If a card’s text directly contradicts the Rulebook, the card takes precedence.
  2. Limited Components: All components are limited. If an effect requires a component that isn’t available and doesn’t specify otherwise, that part of the effect is ignored (e.g., if an Anomaly causes a Stalker to lose 7HP and gain [icon] but all [icon] tokens are used, the Stalker only loses 7HP).
  3. Uncaring Zone: The Zone reacts almost as if it has a mind of its own. Whenever you have an unclear situation, rule in the player’s disfavor.
Campaign Flow & Story Tracking

Campaign Structure
One Campaign playthrough consists of playing a series of consecutive Missions with one group of Stalkers. Each Campaign requires a fresh Zone Map to mark progress and a separate Stash (Stalker groups don’t share belongings).

Stories
Each Mission is part of a Story providing overarching narrative. Outcomes impact the Campaign. Stories have letters (e.g., A1, A2) shown in the Mission guide and on the Zone Map. You do not need to finish one Story to begin another. Save the Zone Map and Stash contents after finishing all Corebox Stories; upcoming expansions will use your Campaign state.

Stalker Count [icon]
Some game effects change based on the number of Stalkers in a Mission. Each player controls 1 Stalker. To add a new Stalker to the Campaign group, choose a Stalker card for them and equip them using Stash contents.

Gear Management & PDA Setup

PDA & Equipment Overview
The PDA (Player Board) holds your Equipment and Stalker info. It contains:

  1. Stalker slot: Place your Stalker card here.
  2. Turn slots: Place Turn tokens here. Slide them down to mark performed Standard Actions. Contains Critical Injury tokens that block part of the slot, limiting available Actions per Turn.
  3. Weapon slot: Holds equipped Weapon card, up to 2 matching Weapon Attachments, and the Magazine track for Ammo markers.
  4. Armor slot: Holds equipped Armor card and up to 1 Armor Attachment.
  5. Artifact slots: Top slot holds the Artifact with highest Base Radiation value. Below it is an Artifact Container token (if applicable) and up to 2 additional Artifacts whose radiation values are covered by the PDA.
  6. Backpack: Holds Items and unused Equipment cards. Base capacity is 6 Equipment cards. Exceeding capacity forces you to discard excess immediately after equipping actions finish. Only Item cards can be used while in the Backpack; others must be equipped via "Prepare" Action.
  7. Pockets: Hold [icon] Magazines and Mission-specific tokens. Use [icon] here for "Bolts & Nuts" and "Decoy" Actions.
  8. Attention slot: Place your Attention token here when not on the Map.
  9. HP track: Mark current HP value here. Drop to 0 HP gains a Critical Injury. Third Critical Injury = Death.

Losing and Healing HP
Lose HP by moving marker right (to 0). Heal by moving left (up to Max HP).

Lead Stalker
A moving title tied to one Stalker at a time. The Lead Stalker breaks ties. Represented by a token. Flip and pass to next Stalker to choose: Heal 2HP or Gain [icon]. While holding the token, you may perform an Action to flip it and pass it left; they cannot use its effect until unflipped during End of Round. You may perform this even if gaining nothing.

Stash & Zone Exploration
Before each Mission, perform Zone Exploration using the Campaign binder and Zone Map. Visit locations on the Zone Map by adding stickers. Mark completed locations by crossing them out. Choose 1 Mission location and up to 1 New Contact or Stash location per Navigation phase. Read specified scripts/back of Mission guide for outcomes.

Contacts
Represent Characters/Animals met during Campaign. Resolve up to 3 slotted Contact cards per Zone Management phase. Types: Loner (small tasks/payment), Traders (buy/sell Equipment), Companions (take 1 on a Mission), Contracts (take 1 additional objective for [icon]). Double-faced: "a" side for Zone Exploration, "b" side for Mission rules. Buy/Sell: Gain/Discard Coupons equal to good’s value (round down to next 50). Trading modifies values. Buy: place in Stash or immediately equip. Sell: discard and return to correct deck/token compartment.

Going on a Mission
Prepare Stalkers before departing. Each Stalker must have at least 1 Armor and 1 Weapon at all times. Before each Mission: Set HP to Max value. Pack few Bolts in Pockets and Focus on the Mission. Fully load each Weapon. Set Radiation dosage on Geiger counter. Artifacts require at least 1 Detector Attachment card in the team to retrieve, though gathering them is recommended.

Post-Mission Cleanup
After each Mission, choose to save Campaign or continue. Clear table per instructions.

Stalker Selection & Starting Conditions

Each player chooses 1 Stalker card and model. Models are universal. Recommended starters: Borsuk (Zone explorer), Mamon (lone fighter), Milady (sneaky sniper), Velel (versatile tank). These remain fixed for the Campaign. Each player picks a color and takes corresponding PDA, Attention token, and plastic ring. Place ring on model, Stalker card in center of PDA, Attention token near Pockets. Gain 5000 [icon] total for the team into Stash. Each Stalker starts with 1 Leather Jacket Armor card in Stash.

The Mission Map & Core Mechanics
Map Layout & Space Rules

Tiles & Spaces
Each Mission Map consists of several Map tiles oriented as shown in setup diagrams. Numbers indicate required tiles. Each tile has a number in one corner and is divided into spaces of different sizes/properties.

  • Water Spaces [icon]: Marked with [icon]. No Entity may enter or move through them.
  • Entities: Living beings traversing the Zone: Stalkers (player characters), Companions (Stalker helpers), Enemies (hostile Humans/Mutants).
  • Thickness of Space Edge: Visual thickness has no gameplay effect.
  • Space Size: Determines Entity capacity. Smallest is 1x1 grid square (holds 1 Entity). Larger spaces combine squares; capacity increases proportionally. Fragments not a full 1x1 square do not add capacity.

Fully Occupied Space
A space with a number of Entities equal to its size is fully occupied.

  • Enemies cannot end Movement in a fully occupied space.
  • Stalkers ending Movement in a fully occupied space Push one Entity from that space 1 space Away (Stalkers first if present). If no Entity can be Pushed to create a slot, you cannot end Movement there.

Buildings and Rooms
Rooms are areas fully enclosed by Walls, Doors, and Windows. Multiple neighboring Rooms create a Building. Effects may reference Indoor (any space in any Room) or Outdoor (spaces not in any Room).

Pathfinding & Proximity

Space Edges
Two spaces sharing an edge in at least one of four cardinal directions are adjacent. Five connection types change interaction:

  1. Open edge: Entities can Move, draw LoS, measure Range through it. Most common.
  2. Impassable Terrain: Entities cannot Move but can draw LoS and measure Range through it. Forms lone structures or separates Map parts (e.g., water).
  3. Wall: Entities cannot Move, draw LoS, nor measure Range through it. Spaces separated by a Wall aren't adjacent. Forms lone structures or buildings.
  4. Door: Part of Walls treated as Open edges. Entities can Move, draw LoS, measure Range through it. Represent entrances.
  5. Window: Treated differently: Stalkers and Human Enemies treat as Open edges; Psionic Enemies treat as Impassable Terrain; all other Enemies treat as Walls.

If two different edges of two tiles connect, treat as one edge of the higher restriction: Wall > Impassable Terrain > Open edge.

Range
Measured between two spaces. Equal to the lowest number of edges crossed. Not measured in a straight line.

  • Range 0 effects apply only to your space.
  • "Within Range X" affects spaces at Range X and lower.
  • "Within Range X+" affects spaces at Range X and higher.

Closest Object and Shortest Route
To find the closest object:

  1. Visualize shortest routes (least number of spaces between). Human Enemies ignore routes containing uncovered Anomaly symbols (unless no other routes exist).
  2. Choose the object with the shortest route.
  3. Tiebreaker: Choose the route with the least number of corners.
  4. If still tied, Lead Stalker decides.
Line of Sight & Visibility

Line of Sight (LoS)
Determines if two Entities can see each other. Always measured between spaces.

  • Check all cardinal directions individually for Stalkers; check directions depending on Facing for Enemies.
  • Check spaces horizontally/vertically connected to Entity's space in the checked direction. Repeat until reaching Map edge, end of Sight Range, or a LoS blocking effect (Wall or No Visibility token). Compare LoS to all spaces reachable with Movement without changing direction.
  • An Entity always has LoS to its own space.
  • An Entity has LoS to all those spaces and can see each Entity present there.
  • Entities, Anomalies, and most tokens do not block LoS unless clearly stated.

[icon] NO VISIBILITY
No Entity can draw LoS to, from, or through a space with this token (treated as having a Wall in each edge for LoS purposes). Entities on the same space with [icon] can still Target each other. Removed during Enemies & Zone Phase.

Environment Cards & Map Tokens

Environment Cards
Each Mission has a unique set influencing the Map, providing hints, or changing rules. Two main types:

  • Map overlays: Placed directly on tiles, adding Actions/Triggers or changing spaces/connections. Text oriented consistently for correct placement.
  • Notes: Kept next to Map. Textful cards with single-use instructions, hints, or additional rules.

Action Choices and Costs: Note cards may offer multiple Actions. Some marked with [icon] require sliding Turn token down once (may increase Radiation dose).

Double Faced Cards: Most are double-faced ("a" front, "b" back). Follow game instructions to keep correct face showing. Stack Environment deck with front face up by default.

Flipping an Environment Card:

  • Overlay: Place back on Map in direct neighborhood or using visual cue.
  • Note: Resolve effects, then usually discard. If not discarded, place next to Map for ongoing rules/info.

Discarding and Removing: Discard goes to Mission box. Remove goes separately (won't be used again in Campaign).

Tokens on Mission Map

  • [icon] BOLT: Kept in Pockets. Placed on Map via "Bolts & Nuts" Action to cover Anomaly symbols by Range 3. Covered symbols ignored for Anomaly Activation rolls. Removed during Enemies & Zone Phase.
  • [icon] ARTIFACT: Usually hidden under Anomaly standee, placed on Map with Detector Armor Attachment. Covered symbols ignored for Activation rolls. Gained via "Pick Up" Action by drawing an Artifact card matching the Anomaly type (or random if unavailable/non-Anomaly space).
  • [icon] LOOT: Equipment scattered throughout Zone (Bolts, Items, Weapons, Artifacts). Placed in stacks on same space. Resolve top token from stack when picking up. If unflipped, flip and gain visible reward. Discard the flipped ? to the side; it cannot be used again this Mission.
  • [icon] UNKNOWN ENEMY: Represents reinforcements. Placed in stacks on one space. Resolve top token: If ×, discard. If [color]/[color]/[color]/[color], place 1 or 2 corresponding standees facing arrow direction. If placed at least 1 standee, discard ?. If not enough standees in pool, draw an Enemy Activation card (Red if at least 1 Red token on Map, else Yellow). Activate only 1 Enemy matching the color that should be placed but couldn't, closest to resolved ?. Discard ?. Resolve multiple/no ? one by one. No ? on Map = nothing happens.
  • Placing Enemies and fully occupied spaces: If new Enemy standee should be placed on fully occupied space, place it on closest available space facing direction of space it couldn't be placed on.
Radiation Hotspots & Cover

Map Radiation [icon]
Radiation is a dangerous part of the Zone. Not all areas are contaminated. Visual intensity changes colors: 1-2 [icon] yellow, 3-4 orange, 5+ red.
Each time a Stalker slides their Turn token (after resolving effect or Standard Action causing slide), if on a space with [icon], increase Radiation dosage on Geiger counter equal to the space's [icon] value. Reduced by Armor’s Map Radiation modifier.
Free Actions: Don't cause you to slide Turn token down, so don't increase Radiation dose from spaces on your current space.
Radiation During Movement: If Movement caused sliding a Turn token and you moved to/from/through spaces with [icon], increase dosage only once equal to the highest value found. Reduced by Armor modifier.

Cover [icon]
Spaces with this icon contain Cover, making it harder to hit Targets unless close. Represents obstacles/hiding spots.
Whenever an Entity on a space with [icon] is Attacked and Attacker isn't on same space (or in same Room for Buildings), reduce ★ successes from Stalkers' Attacks and [icon] damage from Enemies' Attacks equal to the [icon] value.

Round Structure & Player Actions
Phase Overview & Turn Economy

Game Round Structure
Each Mission is played through several Rounds until objective met, Stalker dies, or time runs out. Each Round consists of three Phases:

  1. Event Phase: Draw/read new Event card, apply Instant rules.
  2. Players Phase: Each player carries out two Turns performing Actions.
  3. Enemies & Zone Phase: Enemies Activate, Stalkers suffer Radiation effects, End of Round effects apply.

Event Information
Part of Event cards resolved during Enemies & Zone Phase, but you may read it when Active to plan Actions.

Event Phase
Mark passage of time and narrate. Draw top Event card, read, resolve Instant effects, place next to deck. Proceed to Players Phase.
Decks consist of:

  • Mission Event: Story progression/Mission specific. Unique per Mission. Multiple variants with same number picked randomly when building deck.
  • Random Event: Small story twists (loot, radiation spikes). Added during Mission.

Card structure: Name, Number, Story, Instant effects (resolve immediately on draw, may grant extra Actions but don't slide Turn token unless specified), End of Round effects (resolved at end of Enemies & Zone Phase). Marked effects vary (add Random Events, spawn Enemies). Unmarked effects are always same (final instructions between Rounds).

Players Phase & Turns
Stalkers carry out Turns clockwise. Lead Stalker starts first Round.
Each Stalker carries out two Turns per Round (each represented by one Turn token). During each Turn, perform 2 Standard Actions [icon]. When all finish both Turns, proceed to Enemies & Zone Phase.

Turns
One Turn must finish before next begins. During each Turn, Stalker must perform 2 Standard Actions and may perform unlimited Free Actions. Each Standard Action slides Turn token down once. When Token reaches bottom slot and no additional Free Actions desired, Turn ends.
Markers on Turn tokens: Effects may place [icon] on Turn token. Increase number of Standard Actions per Turn. When performing Standard Action while having at least 1 [icon], discard 1 [icon] instead of sliding Turn token down. During End of Round, discard remaining [icon].

End of Stalker's Turn
After each Turn, Enemies with LoS to that Stalker may React by Attacking (reminded on PDA when Token reaches bottom).

Passing the Turn
Player may pass before Turn begins. Proceed to next Stalker. Passing doesn't lose Turn; you can postpone until better situation arises but must carry out eventually.

Core Basic Actions

Basic Actions are known to every Stalker (listed on Help card). One Action must finish before next begins. Other Actions come from Environment cards and Equipment.
Actions have two icons:

  • [icon] Standard Action: Slide Turn token once. Limited per short span.
  • [icon] Free Action: No slide required. Unlimited per Turn, before/after/between Standard Actions.

[icon] Movement: Move up to 3 spaces. Most common. Results in moving/placing Attention token.
Rules: Move X spaces = move to adjacent space X times. Impassable Terrain/Wall without Window aren't adjacent. Enter space with Enemy = interrupt and immediately end. End on fully occupied space = Push one Entity from that space (Stalkers first) to adjacent space (Move from if possible). If no slot created, can't end there. Move through fully occupied spaces without Enemies allowed. Move from space with Enemy = rotate Enemy standee to face adjacent space you Move to.
Attention: Move through space in Enemy LoS = place [icon] on first space moved through that is no longer in Enemy LoS. End Movement in Enemy LoS = place [icon] on your space. End Movement and [icon] not placed on Map = move/place [icon] on your space.
Radiation: If Movement caused sliding Turn token and you moved to/from/through spaces with [icon], increase dosage equal to highest value found (reduced by Armor).
Triggers: During Movement, check if entered space with [icon] or Anomaly symbols ([icon], [icon], [icon]). Resolve triggered effects. If applies immediately and doesn't end Movement, continue after resolving.

[icon] Careful Movement: Move 1 space. Cannot move from/to Enemy LoS. Don't move/place Attention token.

[icon] Prepare: Gain 1 [icon] (Focus). Gain 1 [icon] (Bolt). Then, any number of times in any order, Stalkers on your space may: Exchange Artifact/Attachment/Armor/Item/Weapon/Magazine/[icon]/Personal Important Finding with another Stalker on same space. Move Weapon between Weapon slot and Backpack (Ammo stays on Weapon). Move Armor between Armor slot and Backpack. Install/Uninstall Attachment from Armor/Weapon. Move Artifact between Artifact slot and Backpack.

[icon] Reload: If have [icon], treat as [icon] (don't discard that [icon]). Discard 1 Magazine from Pockets matching Weapon type (Pistol/Rifle/Shotgun) to add ★ to Weapon to reach its Ammo capacity.

[icon] Pick up: Choose 1 [icon] (Loot) or [icon] (Artifact) on your space. If unflipped [icon], flip it, gain rewards, discard it. When picking up [icon], gain Artifact card matching Anomaly type.

[icon] Decoy: Cannot perform if you have [icon] on Map. Discard 1 [icon] from Pockets. Choose space within Range 3 of Stalker. Place [icon] there. If any Enemies on chosen/adjacent spaces, draw Red Enemy Activation card and resolve it, but only Activate Enemies on chosen/adjacent spaces. If any of those Enemies have LoS to any Attention token, move that Attention token by 1 toward its owner.

Bolts & Nuts: Place 1 [icon] from Pockets onto chosen Anomaly symbol within Range 3. Covered symbols ignored for Activation rolls. Removed during Enemies & Zone Phase.

Knife: Attack Action. Must be on same space as Enemy to Attack. Pick Body Part, roll 3 [icon]. If kill target, reduce your Attention.

Other Actions & Where to Find Them:

  • Weapons: Each has at least one Attack Action requiring Ammo. Examples: SAW-OFF (Accurate Range 0-1, Max Range 2, Blast hits all Enemies on Target's space), FRAG GRENADE (Throw Range 2, Place [icon] on chosen space, Roll 4 ★, Attack all Enemies within that space. Stalkers: Target Torsos. Enemies: Target Head. Discard card.), EYE FIRE ([icon] to move Attention token to space within Range 2 of Stalker).
  • Environment Cards: Interact via Actions written on them.
  • Items: Each has its own Action (single-use, discarded after use). Kept in Backpack. Can be used while stored.
  • Artifacts: Some provide unique Actions.

Instant Triggers and Additional Rules
Triggers appear on Environment/Anomaly cards. Specify if applied immediately during Action or after finishing. Immediate application won't end Action unless instructed. Marked with [icon]/[icon]/[icon] color depending on card. Examples: Grenade Launcher (After Attack, may use [icon] from Grenade Item as [icon]), Improved Artifact Container (May use IMPROVED regardless of Armor), SLIME CHEMICAL (Whenever Enemy within Range 1 Attacks, may increase Radiation dose by 3 to deal [icon] to that Enemy).

Combat Sequence: Attacks & Defense

Attacks Overview
Actions engaging Enemies. Found on Weapons and some cards. Basic Attack: Knife Action. Require Range and/or LoS to target's space. Usually only target Enemy models unless stated otherwise.

Attack Sequence (Stalkers)

  1. Choose Attack Action and valid Target. Weapon Attacks: Target within Max Range and in LoS. Some state minimum Range. Non-Weapon specify Range and LoS requirement.
  2. Slide Turn token if [icon] and spend Ammo if needed.
  3. Place required Attention token. If places [icon] and doesn't specify where, place on your space. If places [icon] and you have [icon] anywhere on Map, do not place [icon]. If in Enemy LoS, always have [icon] on your space.
  4. Target Body Part. If Attack allows picking, do so. Otherwise target Torso. Body Parts have different To Hit tables.
  5. Roll dice. Weapon: roll [icon] from Stalker card + additional [icon] specified by Attack. Non-Weapon specify number of [icon]/[icon]. Roll any additional [icon]/[icon] from other components (commonly Weapon Attachments).
  6. If have [icon] or effect allowing reroll, do so now.
  7. Resolve roll results: Spend rolled [icon] to apply additional effects (same effect multiple times if enough [icon]). Count [icon] and compare to To Hit value of targeted Body Part. Resolve highest reached threshold effect. Weapon Attack within Accurate Range: treat [icon] as 4 [icon]. If Light/Heavy Wound, draw Enemy Wound card and apply outcome. If no threshold reached, miss. Unspent [icon] can be spent for additional effects.
  8. If fail to kill Enemy, rotate Enemy to face your Stalker.

Masks [icon]: On [icon] dice, sides contain [icon] symbols. Spent to apply additional Weapon effects (may be applied multiple times, choose different if more available as long as cost paid). Subtract required [icon] from roll result. May be used before/after Hit check.

Attacking Multiple Enemies:

  • Burst/Full Auto Attacks: Spread [icon] rolled and additional effects between main Target and any Enemy adjacent to it and in your LoS (don't need same cardinal direction).
  • Blast effects: Shotgun trait/Blast Items hit all Enemies on one or more spaces. Roll dice once, apply same [icon] result to all hit.

Enemy Movement & Positioning

Enemy Movement Rules
Two types:

  • Move: Toward target, choosing shortest route. Follow route rotating towards space they will Move to before moving. Repeat as many times as Move Action indicates, stop at target or max distance. If didn't reach destination and aren't facing direction to continue, rotate towards that direction.
  • Go forward: Patrolling without paying mind to surroundings. Goes directly ahead, rotating 90° only if can't proceed farther. Rotates least number of times (clockwise tie) until can Go forward again.

Accompanied by numeric value representing spaces. Sometimes indicated with [icon] referring to Movement value on Enemy card. Example: Zombie "Move up to 2" allows Move 1 space, but "Go forward up to 2" allows Move 2 spaces.

Movement Interruption
Enemies aware of surroundings. If spot Target, won't blindly follow first attention. At any point during Movement, if gains LoS to possible Target or already had LoS to Target, immediately end Movement (e.g., if should Move but already have Target in LoS, won't even rotate). Some Movement specify can't be interrupted. Interruption doesn't cause immediate Attack/Action. If interruption occurred during Enemy Activation card, continue resolving normally.

Enemy Movement and Fully Occupied Spaces
Can't end Movement in fully occupied space. If would happen, ends in adjacent space along route. If also fully occupied, chooses next shortest route instead. Can Move through fully occupied spaces without possible Targets, but can't be interrupted on that space. Example: Snork Moves toward closest [icon]. Path to it doesn't contain corners.
