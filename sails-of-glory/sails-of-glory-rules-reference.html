Component Overview
Component Description & Key Features
Ship Bases Double-sided plastic bases holding ship models. Indentations align with maneuver card movement lines. Displays Burden (hull size) and Veer (maneuverability) ratings.
Ship Cards Double-sided cards listing Ship's Name, Nationality, Burden, Maneuver Deck letter, Veer, Colored Borders (attitude zones), Firing Arcs, Number of Guns, Number of Crewmen, and Class/Type. Launch/capture years are noted.
Base Cards Double-sided cardboard inserts matching ship names. Placed inside ship bases before securing the transparent lid and model.
Ship Mats Grid boards tracking Broadside (left/right), Ammunition Storage (left/right), Sail Settings (Full, Battle, Backing, Struck), Planned Actions, and Unused Actions.
Ship Logs Paper trackers showing Ship's Name, Year, Burden, Veer, Artillery Firepower (Stem, Full Broadside, Stern), Number of Crew Actions, Musketry Firepower, and damage tracks.
Maneuver Decks Card sets matching ship maneuverability letters. Blue border cards are normal maneuvers. Red border cards are for taken-aback ships. Dark gray border cards are for broken masts (✠✠✠). Cards show movement lines for sail settings and Veer values.
Damage Counters Lettered pools (A–E) containing numeric values, Crew Loss symbols, and special damage icons (fire, leak, mast, rudder, sail). Used to track hull and crew damage.
Wind Gauge Circular dial with directional arrow and strength track. Indicates wind direction and intensity (low, medium, high).
Combat Ruler Measuring tool with colored zones (red/purple, orange, yellow) for range determination. Features gun symbols and firing arc alignment markers.
Action Counters Tokens for planning crew actions: Reload Broadside, Fire Broadside, Musketry Fire, Increase/Decrease Sails, Pump Water, Extinguish Fire, Repair Damage. Includes Boarding, Blank, and Grog counters for optional rules.
Special Damage Markers Tokens for tracking persistent effects: Fire on Board, Leak, Broken Mast, Sail Damage, Rudder Damage, Crew Loss, Entanglement, Grappling.
Setup Checklist
  1. Prepare a flat playing surface of at least 90 x 70 cm with clearly defined boundaries.
  2. Divide players evenly into two teams. Each player controls one or more ships independently.
  3. For each ship, insert the matching Base Card into the Ship Base, secure the transparent lid, and place the ship model on top.
  4. Place the corresponding Ship Log and Ship Card on the designated area of the Ship Mat for quick reference.
  5. Separate the Maneuver Deck into blue border cards and dark gray border cards. Dark gray cards are only used with Advanced Rules (✠✠✠).
  6. Divide damage counters by letter. For Basic Rules (✠), prepare pools A, B, and E. For Standard Rules (✠✠), add pools C and D. For Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), use all pools. Place them facedown in separate containers.
  7. Separate Crew Loss special damage markers from other special damage markers.
  8. Load ammunition: Place two Ball ammunition counters facedown in the left and right Broadside boxes of each Ship Mat (✠). For Standard Rules (✠✠), place one counter of each type (Ball, Chain, Grapeshot) facedown in both Ammunition Storage boxes, then secretly move one counter of your choice to each Broadside box.
  9. Orient the Wind Gauge on the table. In Basic Rules (✠), wind comes from the right of the British side. Align the arrow parallel to the starting lines.
  10. For Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), place Action Counters in the Unused Actions box of each Ship Mat. Place a Sails Status Counter on the chosen sail setting (Full, Battle, or Backing).
  11. For the first turn, place one blue border Maneuver Card facedown in the first space of each Ship Mat.
Turn Sequence Overview

Each turn follows a strict phase order. All players perform each phase simultaneously. Conclude each phase completely before proceeding to the next.

  1. Planning Phase: Check wind attitude, select maneuver cards, apply veer limits, plan crew actions, note restrictions.
  2. Action Phase (✠✠✠ Only): Resolve planned crew actions immediately or delay them to later phases.
  3. Movement Phase: Reveal maneuver cards, apply sail/attitude modifiers, execute movement, resolve collisions/overlaps, handle field exits.
  4. Combat Phase: Declare attacks, resolve artillery and musketry fire simultaneously, apply damage.
  5. Reloading Phase: Transfer used ammunition to storage, load fresh ammunition into empty broadsides.

After the Reloading Phase, begin a new turn with the Planning Phase.

Phase 1: Planning
Wind & Attitude Reading

Before planning movement, determine each ship's Attitude to Wind. Place an Attitude Indicator beside the ship, aligned with the Wind Gauge arrow, pointing toward the main mast. Note the color where the indicator crosses the ship base edge. Red indicates Taken Aback. Orange indicates Beating or Running. Green indicates Reaching. If the indicator crosses exactly between two colors, choose the most favorable (fastest) color: orange over red, green over orange. It is forbidden to take range measurements before planning. Only attitude checking is permitted.

Maneuver Selection & Veer Limits

Secretly choose a Maneuver Card from the ship's deck. If the ship is beating, running, or reaching (orange or green), select any normal blue border card. If the ship is taken aback (red), select only a red border card. Place the card facedown in the first space of the Ship Mat. When playing with Standard Rules (✠✠), players must plan maneuvers one turn in advance (Time to React rule). Place the selected card in the second space of the Ship Mat. The card in the first space executes this turn; the card in the second space executes next turn. Players may not check wind attitude before choosing the card. Each ship has a Veer rating. During planning, a selected card's veer value may not differ from the previous card's veer value by more than the ship's Veer rating. If an illegal maneuver is revealed, replace it with a straight maneuver (veer 5, straight line).

Crew Action Planning

When playing with Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), each Ship Log shows the number of Crew Actions available. In addition to maneuver selection, place facedown Action Counters in the Planned Actions boxes of the Ship Mat, up to the ship's crew action limit. Use the leftmost uncovered symbol in both damage tracks to determine current action capacity. If a ship has Struck Sails, it may not plan a new maneuver card.

Struck Sail Restrictions

Ships with Struck Sails cannot plan maneuvers. Any previously planned maneuver executes using the backing sails movement line, then the ship stops. The ship cannot use the Increase Sails action until it comes to a full stop. Once Increase Sails is used, sails return to backing, and maneuver planning resumes. If both maneuver spaces are filled, normal movement resumes. If a ship transitions from backing to struck sails with only one programmed maneuver, that maneuver is not executed and returns to the deck.

Phase 2: Action (Advanced Rules Only)
Standard Actions

Standard actions resolve immediately upon revelation and return to the Unused Actions box. Increase Sails modifies sail setting upward: struck becomes backing, backing becomes battle, battle becomes full. No effect if already at full sails. Move the Sails Status Counter left. Decrease Sails modifies sail setting downward: full becomes battle, battle becomes backing, backing becomes struck. No effect if already struck. Move the Sails Status Counter right. Pump Water removes one water damage token if present. If the occupied box is not the rightmost, shift all counters left to close gaps.

Combat Actions

Combat actions resolve during the Combat Phase. Fire the Left/Right Broadside may only be planned if the corresponding broadside is loaded. Firing is not mandatory. After firing, turn the ammunition counter faceup. Return the action counter to the Unused Actions box at phase end, regardless of whether fire occurred. Musketry Fire allows one musketry attack. Firing is not mandatory. Return the counter to the Unused Actions box at phase end.

Reloading Actions

Reloading actions resolve during the Reloading Phase. Reload the Left/Right Broadside may only be planned if the broadside box is empty. Move one facedown ammunition counter from storage to the broadside box. Return the action counter to the Unused Actions box at phase end.

Delayed Actions

Delayed actions remain faceup in the Planned Actions box until the next turn's Action Phase. They count toward the action limit for the following turn. Extinguish Fire requires a fire on board. Move the counter to the Special Damage Box to indicate progress. At the next Action Phase, resolve the action. At turn end, remove the counter and one fire marker. Fire damage on the track remains. Multiple fires require multiple actions. Repair Damage allows choosing to repair normal damage, a leak, a damaged rudder, or a broken mast. Move the counter to the Special Damage Box. Resolve at the next Action Phase.

Repairing Normal Damage

Move all counters in one damage box to the 0 Damage Box. Shift remaining groups left to close gaps. Water and fire damage counters cannot be removed this way. Special damage effects from removed counters persist.

Repairing a Leak

Move the counter to the Special Damage Box. At turn end, remove the counter and one leak marker. Water damage on the track remains.

Repairing a Damaged Rudder

Move the counter to the Special Damage Box. At turn end, remove the counter and one rudder damage marker.

Repairing a Broken Mast

Move the counter to the Special Damage Box. At turn end, remove the counter and one broken mast marker.

Limitation to Repairs

A ship may repair leaks and rudder damage unlimited times. Normal damage and broken masts may only be repaired once per game. Leave removed counters or markers in the 0 Damage Box as a reminder.

Phase 3: Movement
Maneuver Execution & Sail Modifiers

After planning, reveal all first-space maneuver cards simultaneously. Execute movement by placing the Maneuver Card on the table. Align the ship base indents with the card's movement line. For blue border cards, align the front indent with the card's base line. Movement lines correspond to sail settings: full sails, battle sails, or backing sails. In Basic Rules (✠), only the blue battle sails line is used. Each line has orange (beating/running) and green (reaching) arrows. Slide the ship base onto the card so the rear indent matches the chosen arrow tip. After movement, remove the card and return it to the deck. For Standard Rules (✠✠), move the second-space card to the first space after movement completes.

Taken-Aback Movement

If a ship is taken aback (red attitude), replace the revealed blue card with a red border card matching the original card's veer value (4 or less, exactly 5, 6 or more). On the first taken-aback turn, use the one-sandglass line, placing the card in front of the ship. On consecutive taken-aback turns, use the two-sandglass line, placing the card behind the ship to show backing wind. Leave red cards faceup on the deck as a reminder. Dark gray border cards are used only when masts are broken (✠✠✠).

Collision & Overlap Resolution

If ships would overlap after movement, return involved ships to their starting positions. Move non-overlapping ships normally. Resolve overlapping ships sequentially. Determine order by highest Burden first. Tie-break by best attitude (green, then orange, then red). Randomize if still tied. Move ships one at a time. If a ship cannot complete movement due to obstruction, slide it along the movement line until it touches another base. Place the maneuver card in front, align the ship, and slide until contact. Friendly ship collisions inflict damage equal to the total Burden of overlapping ships.

Leaving the Game Field

If any part of a ship base exits the playing area after movement, the ship is eliminated and counts as surrendered.

Phase 4: Combat
Firing Mechanics

After movement, declare attacks. Attacks resolve simultaneously. Effects apply simultaneously regardless of type. A ship has two Broadsides, indicated by boxes on the Ship Mat. A ship may fire once per side. If multiple targets are in range, choose one per side. Firing is never mandatory. A ship may choose not to fire or fire only one side.

Firing Arcs & Line of Sight

Each broadside has three firing arcs: central (full broadside), forward (stem), and rear (stern). Central arcs provide maximum firepower. Forward/rear arcs provide reduced firepower. Use the Combat Ruler to check arcs. Place the gun symbol end on the arc's red dot. If the ruler reaches any part of the target base within the arc's white lines, the ship can fire. If multiple arcs can reach the target, choose one. Only the chosen arc's firepower applies. Line of Sight is blocked if the ruler crosses another ship's base. Choose a different arc or target if blocked.

Ammunition & Range Modifiers

A broadside may only fire if loaded. At game start, both sides are loaded. After firing, turn the ammunition counter faceup. Guns are unloaded until reloaded. Determine Firepower using the leftmost empty box on the Ship Log top row. The middle number is for full broadsides, left for forward, right for rear. Damage received this turn does not reduce current firepower. Determine Range using the Combat Ruler from the arc dot to the target base. Short range (red/purple or orange zones) and long range (yellow zone) both inflict damage equal to the firepower number. The attacker chooses which arc to use if ranges differ.

Raking Shots

A raking shot travels down the ship's length. Trace Line of Sight from the central arc dot to the target's main mast. If the line passes through a short side of the target base, the attack is a raking shot. If entering through the front, the target takes one additional damage counter for every three drawn (round up). If entering through the rear, the target takes one additional counter for every two drawn (round up).

Musketry Fire

If a ship base is within one Combat Ruler width of an enemy base, musketry fire is possible. Musketry is optional and in addition to artillery. A ship may attack only one target with musketry per turn. Musketry targets may differ from artillery targets. Musketry Firepower is shown on the Ship Log bottom row. The leftmost empty box indicates current firepower. Musketry does not require reloading.

Boarding

When enemy ship bases contact at the start of Planning, declare boarding. Each player secretly places a Boarding or Blank counter facedown. Reveal simultaneously. Both Blank: no boarding. Both Boarding: grappled, cannot move. Mixed: shuffle and draw one. Blank drawn: no boarding. Boarding drawn: grappled. Place a marker between ships. Grappled ships discard planned maneuvers (✠✠/✠✠✠) or cannot plan movement (✠). Resolve boarding after combat. Each ship inflicts damage counters equal to its Burden. Draw counters alternately, applying effects simultaneously. If burdens differ, draw remaining counters at the end. Boarding stops when one ship surrenders. If both would surrender simultaneously, randomly choose one. Grappled ships remain until the next Planning phase. Multiple boardings resolve independently per pair. Declare targets in order of lowest crew first. If a ship grapples multiple opponents, declare targets sequentially. Boarding attacks resolve together. In Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), boarding is planned as an action. Only ships planning Boarding may declare it. If a ship boards an opponent without a boarding action, the defender takes one damage counter first, and damage resolves sequentially (attacker first).

Phase 5: Reloading
Reloading Sequence

Guns may only reload the turn after firing, or later. During Reloading, move one facedown counter from Ammunition Storage to an empty Broadside box (fired in a previous turn). Keep it facedown. This ammunition may fire next turn. Then, move any faceup counter from a Broadside box (fired this turn) to Ammunition Storage, flipping it facedown. This ammunition may reload at the end of the next turn. A ship may reload both sides in one turn if both started unloaded.

Special Ammunition Loading

When playing with Standard Rules (✠✠), choose ammunition type during reloading. Ball has longer range and causes hull damage. Chain has shorter range (red/purple zone only) and damages masts/sails. Grapeshot has shorter range (red/purple zone only) and damages crew. Leave used counters faceup until the next reloading phase. Ammunition cannot be changed once loaded. Firing without a target is permitted to empty guns for reloading different ammunition later.

Optional Rule Interactions

Gunners Lacking Training (✠/✠✠/✠✠✠): Requires an extra turn to reload. After firing, return the counter to storage faceup. Next turn, flip it facedown. The turn after, reload normally. In Standard Rules (✠✠), no other counter from that storage may reload this turn. In Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), Reload Broadside actions become delayed actions. Reveal, leave faceup, move to storage next Action Phase, reload during Reloading Phase. Double Shot (✠✠/✠✠✠): Loading takes two turns. Flip counter faceup in storage during first reload turn. Move to broadside facedown on the second turn. Fires only within half-ruler range. Inflicts both short and long range damage tokens simultaneously.

Damage Tracking & Ship Status
Drawing & Placing Damage Counters

When attacked, draw damage counters randomly based on firepower and range. Show drawn counters to opponents. Place 0 counters in the 0 Damage Box. Place numbered counters on the Ship Damage Track (top row). Place Crew Loss counters on the Crew Damage Track (bottom row). Counters with both a number and Crew Loss symbol go on the Ship Track, and a Crew Loss marker goes on the Crew Track. Ignore other special symbols in Basic Rules (✠). Start placing counters in the leftmost non-full box.

Ship & Crew Damage Tracks

Numbered counters represent hull damage. Place them highest value to lowest. Each box holds counters until their total value meets or exceeds the ship's Burden. When full, turn counters facedown. Move to the next box. A single counter equal to or greater than Burden fills the box immediately. Crew damage boxes hold only one counter each.

Special Damage Effects

Fire on Board (✠✠✠): Place a fire marker in the Special Damage Box. At the start of each turn, before Planning, place one fire damage marker on an empty Ship Track box. The box is full and blocks other damage. Ends when extinguished. Leak (✠✠✠): Place a water marker in the Special Damage Box. At the start of each turn, before Planning, place one water damage marker on an empty Ship Track box. The box is full. Ends when repaired. Pump Water counters the effect but does not repair. Broken Mast (✠✠✠): Starting next turn, plan only dark gray border cards with matching broken mast symbols. Use appropriate movement arrows. If taken aback, replace with a random red card. Second broken mast restricts to two-symbol cards. Third broken mast causes surrender. Repairable once per game. Sail Damage (✠✠✠): Place a sail marker. First damage has no effect. Second damage prevents full sails (immediately change to battle if full). Fourth damage restricts to backing sails (immediately change if battle). Sixth damage counts as an unrepairable broken mast. Further damage has no effect. Rudder Damage (✠✠✠): Place a rudder marker. Decrease Veer rating by 1. Further rudder damage counts as broken masts.

Surrender & Elimination

If all boxes on either the Ship or Crew Damage Track are covered, the ship surrenders and is removed. Reshuffle all damage counters, including 0s, with unused counters of the same type. Ships that surrender may still fire in the phase they are eliminated.

Optional Status Effects

Flagship (✠/✠✠/✠✠✠): If eliminated, all friendly ships take two Crew Loss markers. All enemy ships remove one crew damage marker (or two if they fired at the flagship this turn). Ammunition Explosion (✠/✠✠/✠✠✠): If a ship has fire on board and receives a third or more fire damage marker, shuffle three fire markers and one explosion marker. Draw one. Fire marker: apply damage normally. Explosion marker: ship is eliminated. Ships in base contact draw a damage counter. Non-zero counters also start a fire on the contacting ship. Double fire damage places two fire markers. Sinking Ships (✠/✠✠/✠✠✠): If a ship has a leak and receives a third or more water damage marker, shuffle three water markers and one sinking marker. Draw one. Water marker: apply damage normally. Sinking marker: ship is eliminated. Spreading Fires (✠/✠✠/✠✠✠): If a ship fires a broadside at a contacting enemy and inflicts fire damage, the firing ship also catches fire. Planning an Extinguish Fire action targeting the enemy prevents fire on both ships. Surrendered Ships (✠/✠✠/✠✠✠): Surrendered ships remain as obstacles. Execute last planned maneuvers, then stop as wrecks. Wrecks inflict collision damage to all ships. Cannot be boarded. Remove from play if eliminated by explosion, sinking, or if they have open leaks/fires when surrendering.

Wind, Attitude & Sailing
Attitude Indicator & Wind Colors

The Attitude Indicator determines wind relationship. Red indicates Taken Aback. Orange indicates Beating or Running. Green indicates Reaching. Colors modify movement speed and maneuver card selection. Attitude changes immediately when wind direction or strength changes.

Variable Wind Direction

Wind direction may change during the game. Place the Wind Gauge aligned with the table edge. Rotate the arrow to the starting direction. Shuffle wind direction change counters. At the start of each Planning Phase, draw one counter. The first change counter drawn is placed on the gauge but does not change wind yet. If a blank counter is drawn, wind remains unchanged. Reshuffle. If a change counter is drawn while one is already on the gauge, compare them. Same direction: rotate the gauge arrow 45° clockwise or counter-clockwise. Opposite directions: wind does not change. Reshuffle both counters. Direction changes affect ship attitude immediately in the current Movement Phase.

Variable Wind Strength

When using Variable Wind Direction with Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), wind strength varies. Start at medium strength. Place the strength counter on the middle track square. Low Strength: All sail settings are considered one step lower. Full sails use battle lines, battle use backing lines, backing becomes struck. Medium Strength: Normal movement. High Strength: All sail settings are considered one step higher. Backing uses battle lines, battle uses full lines, struck remains struck. Ships at full sails must draw a damage counter each turn. Crew Loss symbol inflicts sail damage. 0 symbol has no effect. At the start of each Planning Phase, draw a strength change counter. First change counter is placed on the gauge but does not change strength yet. If a blank counter is drawn with no counter on the gauge, strength remains unchanged. If a blank counter is drawn with a change counter on the gauge, strength changes. Decrease moves the counter left. Increase moves it right. Strength caps at low or high. Reshuffle counters. Strength changes affect movement immediately in the current Movement Phase.

Terrain, Batteries & Solitaire Play
Terrain Elements

Reefs: Hard obstacles. If a ship would overlap a reef, return it to its start and resolve movement as an overlap. Movement ends when the base touches the reef. The ship takes damage tokens equal to its Burden. If damage exceeds Burden in one turn, the ship runs aground and cannot move again. Planned maneuvers are discarded (✠✠/✠✠✠). Leaks stop inflicting water damage. Special damage symbols (except leak/rudder) are ignored on reef collision counters (✠✠✠). Reefs do not affect combat. Shoals: Shallow water hazards. Overlapping a shoal causes the ship to run aground immediately. Cannot move again. Planned maneuvers discarded (✠✠/✠✠✠). Leaks stop inflicting water damage. Ships cannot sink on shoals if Sinking Ships or Surrendered Ships rules are active (✠✠✠). Shoals do not affect combat. Coasts & Islands: Block line of sight. Scenarios indicate dangerous distances (ruler width, red/purple zone, or none). Sandy coasts act as shoals. Rocky coasts/islands act as reefs. Overlapping terrain inflicts double Burden damage and always causes grounding. Line of sight is blocked from central arcs but not forward arcs. Harbors are safe zones but still cause damage/grounding if overlapped. Coasts block firing arcs completely if the line of sight crosses sea, land, then sea again.

Coastal Batteries

Batteries are stationary terrain features with firing arcs. They use a Battery Log with a Fortification Rating (similar to Burden), Artillery Firepower, Crew Actions, and damage tracks. Batteries do not move or plan maneuvers. They follow ship combat rules: shoot, reload, take damage. Firepower is determined by the leftmost empty top-row box. Batteries may have multiple arcs. Choose one arc per target. Range is measured from the red dot. Musketry is possible if the target is within the red/purple zone. Batteries inflict raking damage if any arc meets raking requirements (✠✠). Batteries ignore special damages except crew and fire (✠✠✠). If all Battery Damage or Crew Damage boxes are full, the battery is eliminated. Reshuffle counters. Eliminated batteries may still fire in the phase they fall. In Advanced Rules (✠✠✠), batteries plan actions: Fire Guns, Reload Guns, Musketry Fire, Extinguish Fire, Repair Damage. Repair only fixes normal damage.

Solitaire Play

Automatic enemy ships use a filtered maneuver deck (veer 3–7). During Movement, draw the top card. Used cards go to a discard pile. Shuffle sideslips (veer 5, non-straight) back into the deck when one left and one right are in the discard. Shuffle turns back when one left and one right sum to exactly 10. Random ships do not plan one turn ahead (✠✠/✠✠✠). Combat: Basic/Standard (✠/✠✠) ships fire when loaded with a target in range. Reload immediately. Use Forced Aim for target selection. If Continuous Fire is active, fire if a target exists, reload only if none. Standard/Advanced (✠✠/✠✠✠) ships use fixed ammunition damage based on range zones. Advanced (✠✠✠) action priorities: 1) Musketry if target in range. 2) Extinguish fire. 3) Repair leak. 4) Fire broadside if loaded/target exists. 5) Reload broadside. 6) Repair damages (points, then mast, then rudder). Battery priorities: 1) Extinguish fire. 2) Fire guns. 3) Musketry. 4) Reload guns. 5) Repair damage.

Glossary & Symbol Key

Burden: The numerical size rating of a ship, indicating hull damage capacity and collision impact.

Veer: The maneuverability rating of a ship, limiting how sharply a ship can turn between turns.

Taken Aback: A wind attitude where the wind hits the ship's sails from behind, forcing the use of red border maneuver cards and reduced movement.

Broadside: A ship's left or right side, equipped with guns that fire simultaneously.

Raking: A shot that travels along the length of a target ship, inflicting additional damage based on entry point.

Attitude to Wind: The angle between a ship's heading and the wind direction, categorized as Beating/Running (orange), Reaching (green), or Taken Aback (red).

Ship Log: A paper tracker recording firepower, crew actions, and damage accumulation for a specific ship.

Combat Ruler: A measuring tool used to determine firing arcs, line of sight, and damage range zones.

Maneuver Card: A card dictating a ship's movement path, veer value, and sail setting modifiers.

Special Damage: Persistent negative effects (fire, leak, broken mast, rudder damage, sail damage) that require specific actions to resolve.

Tier Markers: ✠ indicates Basic Rules. ✠✠ indicates Standard Rules. ✠✠✠ indicates Advanced Rules.

Damage Symbols: Numeric values represent hull damage. Crew Loss symbols represent crew casualties. Fire, Leak, Mast, Rudder, and Sail icons represent special damage types. Zero (0) counters represent non-damaging hits.

Action Counters: Tokens representing crew tasks such as reloading, firing, adjusting sails, pumping water, extinguishing fires, and repairing damage.

Wind Gauge: A dial indicating current wind direction and strength, used to determine ship attitudes and movement modifiers.

Base Card: A double-sided insert matching a ship's identity, placed inside the ship base for identification.

Ship Mat: A grid board tracking ammunition status, sail settings, planned actions, and damage markers for a ship.

Fortification Rating: The damage capacity of a coastal battery, functioning similarly to a ship's Burden.

Continuous Fire: An optional rule allowing unloaded broadsides to fire at reduced damage.

Forced Aim: An optional rule requiring ships to target the nearest enemy within their firing arc.

Double Shot: An optional ammunition type requiring extended loading time but inflicting combined damage at close range.

Aiming High: An optional rule allowing players to trade hull/crew damage for mast/sail damage.

Entanglement: An optional rule where contacting ships may become temporarily immobilized.

Grog: An optional morale action allowing a ship to remove one crew damage token once per game.

Surrendered Ships: An optional rule keeping eliminated ships on the table as stationary obstacles.

Variable Wind: Optional rules allowing wind direction and strength to change dynamically during gameplay.
