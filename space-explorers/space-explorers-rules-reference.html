Game Objective & End Conditions

You are the head of a Research & Development Hub in a Space Research Center. Your goal is to complete large-scale space projects by gathering the best and brightest minds humanity has to offer. You score Progress points by recruiting Specialists and completing Projects. The player who made the most Progress is the winner.

The game ends when either of the following triggers occurs:

  • One player has recruited 12 Specialists to their Hub.
  • All available Projects have been completed.

Play continues until it reaches the First Player, who does not take another turn. Note that the First Player marker never passes during the game; knowing who started is critical for this end-game trigger.

Setup

Prepare the table with the following components and starting positions:

  • Shuffle the Specialist cards and place them face-down in the center to form the Specialist Deck. Reveal the top 6 cards and place them face-up beside the deck to form the Space Research Center.
  • Take Project tiles equal to the number of players plus 2, choosing at random. Return unused tiles to the box. Place the chosen Projects in a line beside the Specialist Deck.
  • Give each player a Research & Development Hub (with the two pieces connected), 5 Research tokens (one of each color), and a Player Reference sheet. Return unused components to the box.
  • Deal one card from the Specialist Deck to each player to form their starting hand. Players must keep cards in their hand secret.
  • Choose a First Player and give them the First Player marker.
Turn Structure

Starting with the First Player and proceeding clockwise, players take turns to perform exactly one action. The turn follows a strict flow:

  1. Choose an action: Draft a Specialist or Recruit a Specialist.
  2. Resolve the chosen action.
  3. Optionally complete a Project.

You must perform one and only one of these actions on your turn.

Action 1: Draft a Specialist

On your turn, you may take one Specialist card into your hand by taking a Specialist from the Center or by drawing a card from the Specialist Deck. There is no cost to take a Specialist into your hand.

If you take a card and there are fewer than 6 Specialists remaining face-up in the Center, reveal the top card of the Specialist Deck to fill the vacant place.

Action 2: Recruit a Specialist

On your turn, you may recruit a Specialist to your Hub directly from the face-up Specialists in the Center or from your hand. Follow these steps to recruit the chosen Specialist:

Step 1: Assign to a Division

All Specialists possess a Skill in a particular field, marked with round icons on the upper left of the card. A Specialist may only be assigned to a Division that matches the Specialist’s Skills. For example, a Tester (a green Skill icon) must be assigned to the Testing Division (the green Hub section).

If the Specialist has Skills in two different areas, you may choose which of those two Divisions the Specialist joins.

Step 2: Calculate Recruitment Cost

Specialists have a basic Recruitment Cost, marked on the lower left of the card with square icons, showing the Research they require. When you recruit the first Specialist in a Division, you pay the full basic Recruitment Cost, providing Research icons matching all of the icons shown.

Each Specialist added to a Division reduces the cost to recruit the next Specialist into the same Division. Once a Specialist is added to your Hub, each Skill icon they have that matches their Division will reduce the cost for new recruits, allowing you to ignore one Research icon in the Recruitment Cost. The Recruitment Cost decreases from bottom to top, as indicated by the triangle arrow in the lower left corner of the card.

If a Specialist has two Skills, only the Skills that match the Division are counted toward Recruitment Cost. The Recruitment Cost can be reduced to zero if there are enough Specialists already in that Division with matching Skills.

Step 3: Pay Research & Place Card

Now that you have determined the Recruitment Cost, you must provide the required icons to recruit the Specialist. You can combine these methods any way you wish to produce the Research icons you need.

Payment Methods
  1. Provide the Research icons directly by giving matching tokens from your supply to the player on your left.
  2. Return one or more Specialists from your hand to the Center. Each Specialist returned counts as up to two Research icons of any type you need. These icons do not carry over to the next turn and must be used immediately. You cannot return a Specialist to the Center and recruit that same Specialist in the same turn.
  3. Use abilities of Specialists that are active whenever they are the top card in their Division. Some of these abilities provide Research icons that you can use each turn when recruiting a new Specialist.

After you have provided all the required Research icons, place the newly recruited Specialist on top of the other Specialists already in that Division. Any ability of the new Specialist cannot be used immediately; the new ability becomes available on your next turn. When a new Specialist is added to a Division, the abilities of the previous Specialists are covered up and no longer available. Only the abilities of Specialists on the top of each Division are active.

End-of-Turn: Complete a Project

At the end of your turn, you may complete any Project not already taken by another player. This does not require an action. You can only complete one Project per turn, even if you have enough Skills in your Hub to complete more.

Compare the combined Skills available from all Specialists in your Hub with the Skills required by each Project. If you have Skills that meet or exceed a Project’s requirements, you can complete that Project. Take the Project tile and place it beside your Hub for scoring at game end. Completing a Project does not use up your Specialists. They remain in your Hub and can use their Skills on the next Project.

Specialist Abilities & Card Anatomy

Specialists are distinguished Engineers, Testers, Scientists, Builders, and Astronauts, who contribute their Skills to help you complete Projects. Projects are the aim of the game: building and launching spacecraft from the historical US and Soviet space programs. Skills of different types are required to complete a Project. The type of Skills required, and how many of each, are marked on each Project with circular icons. The Space Research Center consists of the Specialist cards placed face-up beside the Specialist Deck. The Research & Development Hub has five Divisions: Engineering (blue), Testing (green), Science (yellow), Construction (red), and Space Flight (purple). Research is the main resource for recruiting Specialists.

Card Layout & Icon Legend

Specialist cards contain the following zones:

  • Picture: Reflects the primary Skill of the Specialist.
  • Progress points: Scored by the Specialist at game end. If marked with an asterisk, the score depends on some other condition.
  • Skills: Indicate what the Specialist contributes to completing a Project, which Divisions they may be assigned to, and how much they reduce the Recruitment Cost for new Specialists added to the same Division.
  • Recruitment Cost: Shows the Research icons required to recruit the Specialist.
  • Triangle arrow: A reminder that the Recruitment Cost is reduced from the bottom up.
  • Ability: Active while the Specialist card is on top of their Division, or during scoring at game end.
  • Card number: Used to look up the matching ability number in the reference.

Project cards contain the following zones:

  • Picture: Shows a real satellite, spaceship, or interplanetary station.
  • Progress points: Added to your score if you complete this Project by game end.
  • Skills: Required to complete the Project.

Icon Legend:

  • Any Research symbol: Represents any type of Research icon.
  • Any Specialist card: Represents any Specialist card.
  • Specialist card whose base Recruitment Cost is equal to the number of Research icons shown: Represents a Specialist with that specific cost.
  • Specialist card with two Skills: Represents a Specialist possessing two Skills.
  • Specialist recruited to a Division: Represents a Specialist placed in a Division.
  • This Specialist card: Represents the specific Specialist card in question.
  • A Division of your Hub: Represents a Division on your Hub.
  • A Specialist card in your hand: Represents a Specialist in your hand.
  • Draw a Specialist card from the deck: Represents drawing from the Specialist Deck.
  • Specialist cards in the Center: Represents Specialists in the Space Research Center.
  • Any Project: Represents any Project tile.
  • Pass one Research token to the next player clockwise: Represents passing a token.
  • Change the order of Specialists in one Division of your Hub as you choose: Represents reordering a Division.
  • This Specialist's ability can only be used at the beginning of your turn: Represents a start-of-turn trigger.
  • Any skill: Represents any Skill type.
  • Progress points you gain at game end: Represents end-game scoring.
Ability Timing & Activation Rules

Many Specialists have extra abilities. Specialists whose cards are marked with an asterisk in their Progress points have abilities that are used during scoring at game end. These apply whether the Specialist is on top of their Division or not.

Other abilities are available during the game for as long as the Specialist is the top card in their Division. Each turn, you may choose whether to use each ability or not. Each such ability can be used at most once per turn. Abilities are optional unless stated otherwise. You cannot return a Specialist to the Center and recruit that same Specialist in the same turn.

End Game & Scoring

The game ends when one player has recruited 12 Specialists to their Hub or all available Projects have been completed. Play continues until it reaches the First Player, who does not take another turn. Total each player's Progress points to determine the winner.

Calculate your score in the following order:

  1. Count the Progress points marked on your completed Projects.
  2. Count the Progress points marked on all Specialists in your Hub, whether they are on top of the Division or not. Do not count Specialist cards in your hand.
  3. Determine the score for Specialists marked with an asterisk in the upper left corner. These abilities score Progress points according to their ability. Scoring abilities are active even if their cards are not on top of their Division. You may find it easier to count the ordinary Progress points first and then add Progress points scored by Specialists with asterisks.

The player who made the most Progress wins. In case of a tie, the player with the most points from completed Projects wins. If there is still a tie, both players are proclaimed the winners.
