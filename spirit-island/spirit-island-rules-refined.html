1. Quick Turn Sequence
1.1 Phase Order & Step Sequencing

Each turn follows a strict sequence of phases. Players act simultaneously within each phase, conferring as they wish. Table talk is encouraged and often necessary for victory. The turn proceeds as follows:

  1. Spirit Phase
  2. Fast Power Phase
  3. Invader Phase
  4. Slow Power Phase
  5. Time Passes

The Spirit Phase begins with Growth, followed by Gain Energy, and concludes with Play and Pay for Power Cards. During the Invader Phase, the board resolves in this precise order: Blighted Island Effect, Event (except on the first turn), Fear Effects, Ravage, Build, Explore, and finally Advance Invader Cards. The Time Passes phase serves as the wrap-up step, clearing damage, elements, and single-turn effects, and managing blight card flipping.

1.2 Phase Details Overview

The following sections detail the precise timing, sequencing, and mechanical interactions for each phase. All powers, events, and invader actions resolve according to the step order outlined below. Players should announce phase transitions clearly to avoid mid-resolution interruptions.

2. Phase Details
2.1 Spirit Phase

Growth Options & Costs
Each Spirit performs three Growth actions in sequence, though the order of icons within a single Growth option may be chosen freely. The first option is Add a Presence, which places one Presence at Range 2 from existing Presence. The second is Gain Energy, granting 2 Energy in addition to normal income. The third is Gain Power Card, which draws from the appropriate deck. The fourth is Reclaim Cards, returning played cards to hand. Spirits with "Growth (Pick #)" may choose that many options, completing one fully before starting the next. Some Growth options cost Energy; if you lack the Energy, you cannot choose that option. You may use Energy gained from a prior Growth option to pay for a later one. Presence repositioning is allowed during Growth: when adding a Presence from your track, you may instead use an existing Presence from anywhere on the Island.

Energy Income & Track Bonuses
After Growth, you gain Energy equal to the highest uncovered number on your Energy Presence Track. Unspent Energy carries over to the next turn. Energy is individual and cannot be transferred. The bottom Presence Track determines your maximum Card Plays per turn. Static bonuses (like +1 Range) and Elements on tracks are constantly in effect. Optional actions like Reclaim One, Move Presence, or Pay Energy to Gain a Power Card may be taken once per turn during the Spirit Phase.

Playing & Paying for Cards
Select which Fast and Slow Power Cards to play. You may play up to the number shown on your Card Plays Track. You must immediately pay Energy for all played cards, even Slow ones. You immediately gain all Elements from played cards, even if their effects are Slow. Do not pick targets or resolve effects yet. Energy costs cannot drop below zero unless explicitly stated. Thresholds with Energy costs are optional; you may decline to pay and skip that specific threshold.

2.2 Fast & Slow Power Phases

Resolution Order & Timing
Players resolve Fast Powers before the Invader Phase begins, and Slow Powers after it concludes. Powers resolve in whatever order players choose, provided no Power interrupts another partway through. If a player does not want to use a Power's text effect, they may skip it entirely. You cannot delay a Fast Power until the Slow Phase.

Repeats & Triggered Actions
Repeating a Power activates its effect again. Repeats do not grant Elements again and cannot be chained. A Repeat is a new basic Action that may fire during any phase if conditions are met. Triggered Actions (e.g., "After X, do Y") fire when conditions are met, but cannot trigger themselves directly or indirectly. If a Power is Repeated, the Repeat is a separate Action. You may make different choices during a Repeat. Changes to a Power (like Range boosts or Fast/Slow status) carry over to the Repeat.

Elemental Thresholds & Usage Limits
Innate Powers have Elemental Thresholds, meaning they can only be used on turns you have all required Elements. Elements are never spent, only checked. You may always resolve a Power as if you had fewer Elements than you actually do. Thresholds are resolved top to bottom. If a threshold says "Instead", it replaces previous levels. A Power affects only one target land unless stated otherwise, affects only the current turn, and can only be used once per turn.

2.3 Invader Phase

Blighted Island Effect
If the Blight Card has flipped to "Blighted Island", follow its instructions immediately. This occurs after Setup and before any other Invader Phase steps.

Event Resolution
After the Blighted Island effect and before resolving Earned Fear Cards, draw and resolve one Event Card (except on the first turn, where it is drawn but discarded). Events resolve top to bottom, performing as much as possible. Events are not Powers; you must follow instructions exactly and cannot skip irrelevant parts by choosing a different target. Choice Events require joint consensus; if no consensus is reached, the first listed option is the default.

Fear Effects & Terror Levels
If any Fear Cards have been earned, flip the face-down stack over and resolve them one at a time in the order they were earned. Use only the effect listed next to the current Terror Level. Fear effects last only for the current turn unless stated otherwise. Destroying a Town generates 1 Fear; Destroying a City generates 2 Fear. Removing or Replacing does not generate Fear.

Ravage Resolution Order
Ravage follows a precise 5-step sequence:

  1. Determine participating Invaders and Dahan. Add together individual Damage from each Invader. Apply individual modifiers (like Disease) and remove one Disease per Invader that has one.
  2. If Invaders deal 1 or more Damage, increase it by per-land modifiers (like Badlands).
  3. Lower Damage with Defend.
  4. Simultaneously deal Damage to the land and to Towns/Cities. If 1 or more Damage is done to Towns/Cities, they take +1 Damage per City.
  5. Surviving Dahan fight back. Each Dahan deals 2 Damage to Invaders. Dahan fight back even if Damage is reduced to 0 by Defend, but not if the Ravage Action is skipped entirely.

Build & Explore
Invaders Build in lands matching the Build Card. If Invaders are present, they add a City if the land has more Towns than Cities, otherwise a Town. Invaders Explore in accessible lands matching the Explore Card, adding one Explorer from Towns/Cities or the Ocean. If a card has a flag icon and an Adversary is active, perform the Escalation effect first. Wilds prevent the next Explore and are then Removed. Disease prevents the next Build and is then Removed.

Advance Invader Cards
After Explore, slide all Invader Cards left: Ravage to discard, Build to Ravage, Explore to Build. If multiple cards occupy a space, resolve them bottom-to-top. Advance all cards on a space together.

2.4 Time Passes

Cleanup & Reset
Discard all Power Cards played this turn. All Elements go away. All Damage clears; turn pieces back to upright. Remove Single-Turn Effect reminder tokens.

Blight Card Flipping Rule
If no Fear remains on the Blighted Island space, draw a new Blight Card. It comes into play already flipped.

3. Core Mechanics
3.1 Presence & Sacred Sites

Placement & Destruction
Presence marks lands a Spirit occupies. If Presence is ever Destroyed, it is Removed and placed next to the Island. If any Spirit has no Presence left on the Island, players immediately lose. A land can hold any number of Presence from any number of Spirits. Sacred Sites are lands where a Spirit has more than one Presence. Some Powers can only be used from Sacred Sites.

Isolate & Movement Restrictions
Isolate represents lands hard to travel to, from, or through. It prevents Invader Explore Actions and prevents Invader pieces from using adjacencies unless you allow it. Isolate does not affect Blight cascades or whether a land is Coastal. Isolate only lasts for the current turn. You may let Invaders in an Isolated land use some or all adjacencies for a given Action if you wish.

Return Presence & Tracking
If you need to Return Presence to your tracks, it comes from the Island. You may choose which track it goes to, but it must cover a legal space. If there is no legal place, the Presence is Destroyed. Destroyed Presence is placed next to the Island and cannot be used unless explicitly stated. Removed Presence is kept with your Destroyed Presence.

3.2 Energy & Card Plays

Income & Limits
Each turn, each Spirit gains Energy equal to the highest revealed amount on their Energy Track. Unspent Energy carries over. Card Plays are individual and cannot be shared. Unused Card Plays do not carry over.

Power Costs & Thresholds
The Energy cost of a Power cannot drop below zero unless explicitly stated. Some Powers have an Additional Cost to use; you must pay it to use the effects. Thresholds with Energy costs are optional. Repeats must pay the cost again or do nothing.

Aided by Element Mechanics
For Choice Events, costs are paid jointly. Costs can be aided by Elements: count +1 Energy for each Element in play, +2 for each discarded Power Card with that Element, or +4 for each Forgotten Power Card with that Element. A Power Card may only be used for one bonus. Aided by Energy is only for paying the cost.

3.3 Powers & Elements

Power Anatomy & Speed
Powers have an Energy Cost, Name, Elements Gained, Speed, Range, Target, Effects, and Elemental Thresholds. Fast Powers resolve before the Invader Phase; Slow Powers resolve after. Range is a maximum; you may always use a shorter Range. A Range of 0 means a land where you have Presence.

Gather, Push, and Repeat
Gather pulls things from adjacent lands into the target. Push sends things out of the target to adjacent lands. Range boosts do not affect Gather or Push distance. Repeats activate effects again without granting Elements. Repeats cannot chain. You may make different choices during a Repeat.

Action Type Definitions
An Action is a group of game instructions performed as a unit. Examples include one Power use, one Ravage/Build/Explore in one land, everything a Fear Card does, everything a Main Event does, and Adversary Escalation effects. "Each board/land/player" instructions create separate Actions. Triggered Actions fire when conditions are met but cannot trigger themselves. Modifying Actions happens right away.

3.4 Damage, Destruction & Defend

Health & Damage Tracking
Invaders have specified Health. Explorers have 1 Health, Towns have 2, Cities have 3. Dahan have 2 Health. Damage equal to or exceeding Health Destroys a piece. Damage clears at the end of the turn. Health changes never reduce a piece below 1 Health. If Damage already meets or exceeds new Health, it is immediately Destroyed.

Ravage Participation & Modifiers
Check participation when determining lands and resolving each Ravage Action. Pieces that Do not participate in Ravage neither deal nor take Damage and are ignored for modifiers. Badlands boost Damage to Invaders and Dahan by +1, once per Action. Strife on an Invader blocks its Damage to Dahan/land and is then Removed. Durable Invaders have extra Health; Destroy effects deal Damage equal to base Health instead.

Strife, Badlands & Durable Invaders
Strife is added to a specific Invader. When it deals Damage to Dahan/land, it deals 0 and is Removed. If Damage is boosted, Strife applies after. Badlands boost Damage to Invaders and Dahan by +1 per token, once per Action. They do not change Destroy effects. Durable Invaders take Damage from Destroy effects equal to base Health.

Defend & Destruction Rules
Defend reduces Damage by the specified amount. Destroy generates Fear (1 for Town, 2 for City). Remove or Replace does not generate Fear. Destroy effects for Durable Invaders deal Damage instead. Damage may be combined from multiple Powers. Remaining Damage on survivors heals at turn end.

3.5 Blight & Cascade

Adding & Removing Blight
When you add Blight to a land, Destroy one Presence from each Spirit there. If the land already had Blight, also add one Blight to any one adjacent land (Cascade). Cascade triggers only once per addition, regardless of quantity. Moving Blight does not Destroy or trigger cascades. When you Remove Blight, return it to the Blight Card.

Cascade & Land Damage Rules
Blight cascades only once per addition. Blight does not trigger cascades when moved. Blight on the Island Destroys Presence. If the last Blight comes off the Blight Card, follow its instructions (often loss). Blight is unlimited; use markers if components run out.

Still Healthy Cards & Pool Limits
Some Blight Cards say "Still Healthy" on the reverse with a small pool. When the pool empties, draw a new Blight Card and go straight to its reverse. Blight pools are based on player count or board count per scenario rules.

3.6 Fear & Terror Levels

Generation & Marker Advancement
Fear is generated by Destroying Towns (1) and Cities (2). Advance Fear Markers from the pool. When all markers advance, move the top Fear Deck card to Earned Fear. If a Terror Level divider is revealed, update the level. Move markers back to the pool; leftover markers go to Generated Fear. Fear Cards are resolved during the next Invader Phase.

Choice Events & Card Resolution
Choice Events require joint consensus. Costs are paid jointly and can be aided by Elements. Fear effects last only for the current turn unless stated otherwise. Terror Level victory conditions take effect immediately when revealed.

4. Win & Loss Conditions
4.1 Victory Conditions

Win at Terror Level 1 by having no Invaders on the Island. Win at Terror Level 2 by having no Towns or Cities. Win at Terror Level 3 by having no Cities. Terror Level VICTORY causes immediate victory. Run out of Blight on the Blighted side or empty the Invader Deck during an Explore step causes immediate loss.

4.2 Loss Conditions

You lose if the last Blight comes off the Blight Card and its instructions say so. You lose if any Spirit has no Presence left on the Island. You lose if you need to draw an Invader Card but the deck is empty. Sacrifice Victory applies if a single effect causes both win and loss: you are Destroyed, but the Island and Dahan survive.

4.3 Adversary & Scenario Overrides

Adversaries add loss conditions and modify Invader Actions. Scenarios change victory conditions and board setup. If rules contradict, the Scenario takes precedence. Adversary loss conditions and Scenario victory conditions override standard conditions.

5. Setup & Variants
5.1 Standard Setup Checklist

Place the Invader Board on one side. Put 4 Fear Markers per player in the Fear Pool. Shuffle the Fear Deck and place 9 cards on the Fear Deck space. Divide into 3 groups of 3 using dividers. Place the Invader Deck on the Explore space. Place a random Blight Card on the Blight Space, Healthy side up. Place exactly 1 extra Blight on the card/space. Arrange Island Boards. Populate with Invaders, Dahan, and Blight per icons. Shuffle Minor and Major Power Decks. Place Energy, Cities, Towns, Explorers, and Dahan near the board. Each player takes a Spirit Panel, 4 Unique Power Cards, and a Power Progression Card if new. Place Presence per Spirit Panel. Flip panels and place remaining Presence on tracks. Reveal top Invader Card for initial Explore. Place it in Build space. Play begins with first Spirit Phase.

5.2 Thematic Board & Layout Adjustments

Place one Beasts and one Disease token on each Island Board. Beasts go in the lowest-numbered land with no printed Setup icons; Disease goes in land #2. On Thematic Boards, add Badlands tokens: 1 to NW board land 7, 2 to W board land 9, 1 to E board land 6. If a Spirit's Setup conflicts with a Thematic Board, either not start that Spirit on that board or follow instructions as best as possible.

5.3 Optional Layouts & Archipelagos

Standard layouts exist for 5 and 6 players. You may arrange boards freely. Compact layouts help Spirits combine forces; strung-out layouts help Invaders spread. Archipelagos separate boards into islets. Coastal lands on boards with facing Oceans are Range 2 from each other. Targeting Spirits on other islets costs 1 Energy per Ocean jump. Larger islands add +2 to +4 Difficulty. Extra board setup varies by player count.

5.4 Adversaries & Scenarios

Adversaries are specific colonizing Powers. Choose before Setup. They specify an Escalation Effect and additional loss conditions. Levels are cumulative. Reminder Tiles mark modified Invader Actions. Scenarios change the situation, victory conditions, or Spirit capabilities. They have a difficulty rating. Playing with Scenarios is optional.

5.5 Adversary Combination Rules

Combine two Adversaries by choosing a leading and supporting nation. Use all game changes from both. The leading Adversary applies its Escalation Effect normally; the supporting applies it on all Stage III cards. Make the Fear Deck by combining card counts. Make the Invader Deck by following supporting instructions first, then leading. If ambiguous, remove top-most applicable card, move bottom-most. Treat Brandenburg-Prussia Level 2 as moving the bottom-most Stage III card just below the bottom-most Stage I.

5.6 Rule Simplification Options

Play without Events by leaving the deck in the box. Add tokens to each board. Add 1 extra Fear Card to Terror Level II. Add two Command Beasts Reminder Cards to the Invader Deck. Do not use Blight Cards with 2 Blight per player or immediate effects. Command Beasts during Fast Power Phase for consensus. Play with fewer tokens by deciding at start. If you would add a token you are not playing with, gain 1 Energy and generate 1 Fear. Replace token-hinge Fear Effects with random Fear Cards.

5.7 Scoring

Score Victory as 5x Difficulty +10 bonus +2 per Invader Card remaining. Score Defeat as 2x Difficulty +1 per Invader Card not in deck. Score Victory or Defeat as +1 per X living Dahan and -1 per X Blight, where X is player count.

5.8 Solo Mode

Solo games use a single board. You can target yourself with "Another Spirit" powers. You do not gain extra benefits from powers better on another Spirit. The luck of the draw is high. No fellow Spirits compensate for weaknesses.

6. Symbol & Component Reference
6.1 Term Definitions

Accelerate: Discard topmost card of lowest Invader Stage. Action: Group of instructions performed together. Aided By: Energy cost reduced by 1 for Element, 2 for Discard, 4 for Forget. Badlands: Increases Damage to Invaders/Dahan by 1. Beasts: Harms Invaders and generates Fear. Choice Event: Event offering paths; first is default. Dahan Event: Portion where Dahan act. Disease: Prevents next Build, then Removed. Durable: Extra Health; Destroy deals Damage. Exactly Range away: Land is that Range with no shorter path. Invader Action: One Ravage, Build, or Explore in one land. Isolate: Prevents Explore and adjacency use. Normal Action: Standard Invader Actions from cards. Origin: Land from which Range is measured. Piece: Physical game piece. Reclaim: Return Power Cards to hand. Remove: Take off board, kept with Destroyed. Return Presence: Take from island to tracks. Skip: Don't perform specified Action. Skip one Invader Action: Skip one during turn. Strife: Blocks specific Invader Damage, then Removed. Token Event: Event portion centering on tokens. Triggered Action: Action happening when conditions met. Use: Perform effects. Wilds: Prevents next Explore, then Removed.

6.2 Icon & Token Quick Reference

Explorer: 1 Damage, 1 Health. Town: 2 Damage, 2 Health, 1 Fear when Destroyed. City: 3 Damage, 3 Health, 2 Fear when Destroyed. Dahan: 2 Damage, 2 Health. Blight: Environmental harm. Fear: Fright advancing Markers. Presence: Spirit disk. Sacred Site: Land with 2+ Presence. Fast Power: Resolves before Invader Phase. Slow Power: Resolves after Invader Phase. Beasts: Harms Invaders, generates Fear. Disease: Prevents Build, then Removed. Wilds: Prevents Explore, then Removed. Strife: Blocks Invader Damage, then Removed. Badlands: Boosts Damage by 1.

6.3 Action Type Reference

Spirit Action: Growth, Gain Energy, Play Cards, Use Presence Track Icon, Use Power, Special Rule Trigger. Invader Action: One Ravage, Build, or Explore in one land. Blight Card Action: Everything a flipped Blight Card does. Fear Action: Everything one Fear Card does. Event Action: Everything a Main/Token/Dahan Event does. Adversary Action: Escalation effects and instructions.

6.4 Range & Targeting

0: Land with your Presence. 1: Range 1 from Mountain with Presence. 2: Range 2 from land with Sacred Site. +3: Increases Range by 3. ANY: Target any land. COASTAL: Touching Ocean. INLAND: Not touching Ocean. INVADERS: Land with Invaders. NO INVADERS: Land without Invaders. MOUNTAIN/WETLAND: Specific terrain. ANY SPIRIT: Target any Spirit. OTHER SPIRIT: Target Spirit other than yourself. DAHAN: Land with Dahan. BLIGHT: Land with Blight. NO BLIGHT: Land without Blight.

6.5 Presence Tracks

1 Energy: Gain Energy equal to highest uncovered number. 1 Card Plays: Play no more than highest uncovered number. Reclaim One: Return one Power Card from discard to hand. Bonus Element: Gained while uncovered.

7. Clarifications & FAQs
7.1 General Mechanics

Blight bad effects happen each time Blight is added, regardless of quantity. Events and Fear Actions require following instructions exactly; you cannot skip irrelevant parts by choosing a different target. Illegally Placed Pieces move to closest legal land or remain out of game. Invader Stage is shown by top card; empty deck is Stage III. Presence tracking: Destroyed/Removed goes beside Island. Return comes from island to tracks. Moving Blight does not Destroy or cascade. Sacred Sites affect 2 Presence. Targeting/Using a Power: skipping text means you did not target/use it. Tokens from expansions may be reused if components run out.

7.2 Component-Specific Rulings

Habsburg Monarchy: "Migratory Herders" activates normally even if Event changes Ravage to Build. Durable Invaders: Illegal lands move to nearest legal. Badlands provide bonus Damage normally. Marking Extra Damage: Use Scenario Markers. Tsardom of Russia: Bringer of Dreams order applies. Dissolution Threatens: Refers to choosing Spirit's Presence. Tell the Old Stories: Cannot restore Powers Forgotten prior to start. Bargains of Power: Additive; affects first X Energy gained. Draw Towards a Consuming Void: Execution continues uninterrupted. Dream of Untouched Land: Random board selects from unused. Forests of Living Obsidian: Origin checked when performing. Settle Into Hunting-Grounds + Many Minds: Does not stack. Weave Together Fabric: Conjoined land adjacent to itself. Gift of Nature's Connection: No Elements if randomizer. Dahan Insurrection + Isolate: Military Response ignores Isolate. Despicable Theft + Isolate: Thieves attempt longer journey. Second Wave + Still-Healthy: Rewind cards, learn doesn't change. Great River + French Plantation: Escaped Towns return to pool. Fractured Days: Pour Time Sideways affects each Invader Action. Many Minds: Sacred Sites as Beasts move both. Shifting Memory: Damaging doesn't change pieces unless Destroyed. Shroud of Silent Mist: Skip text means not targeting. Stone's Unyielding Defiance: Last level uses "Destroys" wording. Starlight Seeks: Growth choices sequential. Volcano Looming High: Self-Destroyed Presence can be saved but doesn't count for thresholds.

7.3 Backwards Compatibility & Replacements

A Strange Madness Among the Beasts: Remove from Event Deck. Sea Monsters: Replace with updated version. Guard the Isle's Heart: Exceptionally difficult vs Habsburg. Rituals of Terror: Easier with Stone's Unyielding Defiance. Still-Healthy Island Cards: Rewind if passed, treat as Blighted if reached. Blight Cards: Select freely; omit extreme ones. Reminder Cards: Not Invader Cards; depth changes with deck. Ongoing Effects: Omit if disliked. Token Simplification: Gain Energy/Fear if omitting.
