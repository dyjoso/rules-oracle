Game Overview & Setup

Goal: Collect the most Victory Points and return to the Castle by Turn 15.

[ROOKIE] Use Board Side A. [ELDER] Use Board Side B. Place the board in the center of the table and the Hunt Track to the right. Assemble the Hunt Track with exactly one more row than the number of players.

Each player chooses a Vampire, takes their player sheet, starting deck, Vampire token, and Score token. Place the Score token on the "0" space of the Score Track.

Shuffle the Hunt cards (excluding Roses) to create the Hunt deck. [ROOKIE] Gather Human, Familiar, and Power cards marked 'A'. Shuffle them and take 2 + 2 per player, setting them aside without looking. Shuffle the remaining cards back into the deck, then place the set-aside cards on top.

Shuffle Bonus tokens face down. Place 1 face down on every Chest space. Store the remaining tokens in the box. Reveal the tokens placed on open Chests.

Place the 3 Rose cards face up on the Labyrinth.

Draw 3 cards from the Hunt deck ([ROOKIE] draw from the bottom) and place them face down on the Tavern without looking at them.

Place the Moon token on the first space of the Turn Track.

Place Castle tokens on the Castle based on player count: 2 players (10/6), 3 players (10/6/4), 4 players (10/8/6/4), 5/6 players (10/8/6/4/2).

Prepare Missions: For 2- to 4-player games, return the "5+" Missions to the box. Shuffle the beige-background Missions and place 2 randomly on the Public Mission spaces ([ROOKIE] use white-title ones). Shuffle all remaining Missions together. Each player draws 2 Missions, chooses 1, and discards the other back into the box without revealing it. Place the corresponding number of Missions on each Crypt space: Mountains (6), Plains (5), Forest (4). Return the remaining Missions to the box.

Prepare Turn 1: Each player shuffles their deck and draws 3 cards. Determine initial turn order by adding the Speed values on starting cards. Vampires with the lowest Speed play first; the fastest play last. Break ties by having the oldest player go first. Place your Vampire tokens on the Castle in turn order, with the first player on top.

Prepare the Hunt Track: Draw 1 card for each row and place it face up in column 3.

Reminder: The game lasts exactly 15 turns. Vampires holding a Parasol may play one additional turn after Turn 15 without hunting.

Turn Sequence

Players take turns sequentially. Each player must resolve the effects of all cards in their Playing Area before play passes to the next player.

Turn Order Priority

Turn order depends on board position. Stack Vampire tokens on the Castle with the first player on top and the last player on the bottom. After completing your turn, flip your token to the resting side.

Region Priority

Vampires in the Forest play first, followed by Vampires on the Plains, then Vampires in the Mountains, and finally Vampires in the Cemetery.

Path Priority

If multiple Vampires occupy the same region, turn order follows the path: Vampires on the Road play first, followed by the Railroad, then the Boat path.

Distance & Stack Priority

If Vampires share a region and path, those closest to the Labyrinth play first. If tokens are stacked, the top token plays first.

Turn Steps Step 1: Play and Resolve Cards

Play all cards from your hand to your Playing Area. Resolve each card one at a time in any order you choose. You may discard a card before resolving it to ignore its effect and exclude its Speed from calculation.

Step 2: Calculate Speed

Add the Speed values of all cards in your Playing Area, including Permanent cards. This total is your Speed for the turn.

Step 3: Move

Spend Speed to move your Vampire in one direction. You may change paths at intersections but cannot reverse direction on the same path. Trigger board effects only on the space where movement ends. If you land on a space occupied by other Vampires, you may push them one at a time (starting from the top token) to adjacent spaces. Pushed Vampires do not push others and simply stack on top of existing tokens. You cannot push Vampires in the Castle or Cemetery. If your total Speed is 0 or negative, you cannot move and do not trigger the space you occupy.

Step 4: Hunt

If Speed remains after moving, you may hunt once. Choose 1 card or pile on the Hunt Track and spend the required Speed (1, 2, or 3). Hunting a Rose costs 0 Speed. Take all cards from the chosen space and place them in your discard pile. You cannot move after hunting. All unspent Speed is lost.

Step 5: End Turn

Flip your Vampire token to the resting side. Discard all non-Permanent cards from your Playing Area. Activate any End of Turn effects. Draw 3 cards. If your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck, then finish drawing.

Step 6: Round Cleanup

After all players have acted, move the Moon token one space to the right. If it reaches space 15, the game ends. Flip all Vampire tokens to the active side. Move all Hunt Track cards one column to the right. Cards in column 1 do not move and form a pile that can be checked before hunting. Draw Hunt cards (1 per column 3 space) unless it is Turn 15. Add 1 face-down card to the Tavern if no Vampires are present and it contains fewer than 3 cards.

Board Spaces & Effects
Safe Zones Castle

You cannot hunt in the Castle. Upon returning, take the highest remaining Victory Point tile and immediately score those points. You cannot leave the Castle after returning. You cannot push a Vampire out of the Castle. A Well is located in the Castle for Spicy or Mist effects.

Cemetery

You cannot hunt Humans in the Cemetery. You cannot push a Vampire who is in the Cemetery.

Resource & Action Zones Chest

Take the Bonus token if available. Add it to your player sheet face up and immediately score 2 Victory Points. There is no limit to the number of Bonus tokens you can hold.

Crypt

Gain a Mission.

Labyrinth

Hunt 1 Rose of your choice at no cost. This counts as a hunt, preventing you from hunting on the Hunt Track this turn.

Market, Church, Mansion, Barracks

You may Digest a Human card of the corresponding type from your discard pile or Playing Area. This includes Humans hunted this turn.

Ship

You cannot hunt on a Ship.

Tavern

You may hunt all cards in the Tavern for 2 Speed instead of hunting on the Hunt Track. Do not look at the cards until someone hunts them. If you hunt them, you must take all cards.

Well

You gain an extra hunt this turn, usable only on Column 1 of the Hunt Track.

Missions

Take all available Missions from the space into your hand, then replace all but one. You may replace one or more previously unused Missions this way and take that many new Missions from the stack. You cannot discuss or reveal the content of remaining Missions. [ROOKIE] Look at available Missions, pick 1, and return the rest to the board. You may only return Missions you were just viewing, never ones you previously held.

Movement & Path Rules Push Mechanics

When landing on a space with other Vampires, push them one at a time starting from the top token to adjacent spaces. Pushed Vampires do not push others and simply stack on top of existing tokens. You cannot push Vampires in the Castle or Cemetery.

Region Bonuses

Gain +1 Victory Point for every Human hunted on the Plains. Gain +2 Victory Points for every Human hunted in the Forest.

Card Types & Keywords
Card Categories Humans

Your main objectives. Each Human is worth a base number of Victory Points scored immediately upon hunting, plus region bonuses. Some Humans have special effects or keywords.

Familiars

Assistants that stay in your Playing Area when played. They are Permanent and provide their benefits each turn.

Powers

Abilities that transform your Vampire, blend into darkness, or grant Speed and strength. Powers optimize your deck but should not distract from your objectives.

Items & Roses

Vampires are enamored by Roses. You may run to the Labyrinth to pick a Rose and gain its benefit every turn. Roses are Items.

Keyword Effects Confuse

[Resolves before Speed calculation] Before calculating Speed, move your Vampire 4 spaces toward the Labyrinth. Do not trigger the landing space or push other Vampires. Play as usual afterward. The effect is not cumulative. You cannot move past the Labyrinth.

Digest

Place 1 Human from your Playing Area or discard pile in your Digestion zone. This card still counts for Missions and End of the Game effects but will not be shuffled back into your deck.

Fast

A pile containing one or more Fast cards costs +1 Speed to hunt.

Gregarious

Gain 1 extra card when hunting a Gregarious Human. Take the first card from the Hunt deck, add it to your discard pile, and gain its Victory Points (including region bonus and card effects) if it is a Human.

Holy Water

You cannot hunt while a Human with Holy Water is in your Playing Area.

Inspiring

Pick 1 Mission stack and take a Mission as if you were on its space.

Permanent

[Stays in Playing Area] Do not discard Permanent cards unless an effect forces it. They remain in your Playing Area, and you benefit from their effect and Speed every turn.

Ready

When you hunt or gain a Ready card, you may place it on top of your deck or in your discard pile.

Slow

Slow Humans are placed directly in Column 2 when added to the Hunt Track, potentially creating a pile.

Spicy

You must use your Speed to move to the closest Well, even if it is behind you or on a different path. Spend as much Speed as needed. If you lack sufficient Speed, spend all of it to move and keep this card in your Playing Area until you reach a Well. Upon reaching a Well, stop and hunt if Speed remains. If equidistant between two Wells, choose one. If already on a Well, do not move but you may hunt.

Unique

You cannot have more than one Unique card of the same type and category in your deck. Roses are currently the only Unique cards.

Bonus Tokens

Each token grants 2 Victory Points when picked up. You may play them on your turn. Flip them to their Chest side to mark usage.

Church, Military, Villager, Noble

Each counts as 1 Human of the corresponding type for Missions and majorities.

Human Type Choice

Counts as 1 extra Human of your chosen type (select at game end). It can score multiple Missions, but the type cannot change after selection.

1 or 2

Spend this token to add 1 or 2 to your Speed for the turn.

+1 Hunt

Hunt 1 extra time this turn.

Discard 1 Card Then Draw 1 Card

Discard 1 card from your Playing Area, then draw 1 card.

Draw 1 Card and Add to Playing Area

Draw 1 card and place it directly in your Playing Area.

Gain 1 Mission

Choose from a Mission stack as if you were on its space.

Parasol

Play 1 turn after the final turn without hunting. You may only play 1 extra turn, regardless of how many Parasols you hold.

Velvet Clothing

Immediately gain an extra 2 Victory Points when picked up, for a total of 4.

End Game & Scoring
Game End Trigger

Dawn ends the game when the Moon token reaches space 15. Any Vampire with a Parasol may play a final turn without hunting.

Survival Zones & Penalties Plains or Forest

Vampires here are burned by the sun. They may count their Victory Points for glory only, but do not compete for the win.

Mountains

[ROOKIE] Vampires are burned but take cover and return to the Castle. They lose Victory Points equal to the value of the space they occupy. [ELDER] Vampires are burned to ashes and do not score.

Cemetery

Vampires take cover in the nearest vault. Each loses 5 Victory Points.

Castle

Vampires here are safe and score normally.

Victory Point Sources Base Human Points & Region Bonuses

Score the base Victory Points on each hunted Human immediately. Add +1 per Human hunted on the Plains and +2 per Human hunted in the Forest.

End-of-Game Card Effects

Resolve all End of the Game card effects here. Hunters grant Victory Points equal to the number of Humans of the same type you hold (including the Hunter itself). The Wolf Echo grants Victory Points equal to the number of Wolves you hold (including itself). End-of-Game card effects resolve here.

Bonus Tokens

Score 2 Victory Points for each unflipped Bonus token on your sheet. Velvet Clothing tokens score 4 total.

Mission & Majority Rules Public & Private Missions

Score points on Public Missions if requirements are met. Reveal and score your private Missions. Majority Missions require you to have strictly more of the required type than each other Vampire, not equal to any.

Score Track Management

If you reach 100 Victory Points, flip your Score token to the 100 side and continue counting from 0.

Tiebreakers

The Vampire with the most Victory Points wins. In case of a tie, the tied Vampire who reached the Castle first wins. If none reached the Castle, the Vampire closest to the Castle wins.

Scoring Checklist
  1. Apply zone penalties for survival.
  2. Count Human Victory Points plus region bonuses.
  3. Resolve End-of-Game card effects.
  4. Score Public and Private Missions.
  5. Apply Bonus Token points.
  6. Compare totals to determine the winner.
