Quick Setup

Refer to the scenario book for exact tile placement, marker distribution, and starting hands. Follow this condensed checklist to prepare the game.

Board Layout

Place the tiles, objective markers, and radio marker (if playing scenario 10: Critical Intelligence) exactly as indicated in the scenario book. Each tile has an A and B side with the same number. Return all unused tiles and markers to the box.

Side Selection & Token Placement

Both players choose a side and take all cards, Combat Counters, Control Markers, and Spawn Markers indicated for that side. Place all tokens on the spaces indicated in the scenario book, ensuring they are oriented correctly. Return any unused Spawn Markers to the box.

Initiative Assignment

Give the Initiative Marker to the side indicated in the scenario book.

Card Assembly

Locate the Starting Cards table for your side. Take all cards marked D, shuffle them, and place them face down as your Deck. Take all cards marked S, place them face up nearby as your Supply, and sort them by card title. Return all leftover cards to the box.

Round Structure

Play consists of a series of rounds. Each round follows a strict sequence of phases. You cannot save cards in your Hand for future rounds. The round concludes only after both players have completed their turns and discarded their remaining cards.

Draw Phase Card Drawing

Both players draw four cards from their Deck to form a Hand.

Deck Exhaustion

Whenever you need to draw a card and your Deck is empty, shuffle your Discard Pile into a new Deck. Never shuffle cards in your Play Area into your Deck.

Initiative Phase Secret Selection & Comparison

Both players secretly select one card from their Hand and reveal it simultaneously. The player who selected the card with the highest initiative value takes the Initiative Marker. That player will perform actions first this round.

Tie-Breaking & Cleanup

In the case of a tie, the player who currently holds the Initiative Marker keeps it. Both players discard their chosen card to their Discard Pile.

Player Turn Phase Turn Execution

The player with the Initiative Marker plays cards from their Hand, one at a time, into a Play Area in front of them. Each card played can be used to perform one of the following:

  1. Card action: choose one of the listed actions and perform it in full.
  2. Hunker Down: return the card to your Supply.
Passing & Alternation

Once a player has no more cards in their Hand that they can or wish to play, their turn ends. The player without the Initiative Marker then takes their turn in the same way. Turns alternate until both players have passed.

Round End Mandatory Discarding

After both players have completed their turns, all cards left in each player's Hand and all cards in each player's Play Area are moved to that player's Discard Pile.

Round Loop

The round is now over. Play immediately returns to the Draw Phase to begin the next round.

Core Mechanics & Game States

These rules govern persistent board states and card management. They modify turn execution but are not actions themselves.

Card Management Pile Relationships & Visibility

Your Supply is open information. Your Deck, Discard Pile, and Hand are private to you. Cards in your Play Area are visible to both players.

Shuffling Triggers

Shuffle your Discard Pile into your Deck only when you need to draw a card and your Deck is empty. This applies during the Draw Phase, Command actions, and Recon actions. Cards in your Play Area never shuffle into your Deck.

Fog of War Play Restrictions

You cannot play Fog of War cards during your turn. They are only valid for secret selection during the Initiative Phase.

Removal & Discarding

The only way to remove a Fog of War card from your Deck is through a Scout's Recon action. Normally, a Fog of War card stays in your Hand until the end of your turn, at which point you discard it as normal.

Suppression Suppressed State

If a Combat Counter is face down, showing its suppressed side, that unit is Suppressed and cannot perform actions.

Action Interruption

When you would take an action with a Suppressed unit, flip that counter face up to its ready side, but do not take the action. Place the card in the Play Area as normal.

Interaction with Hunker Down

Suppressed units can still Hunker Down, but doing so does not flip the counter to its ready side.

Hunker Down Returning to Supply

When you Hunker Down, return that card to your Supply. It is removed from your Deck until you choose to take a Bolster action to add it back.

Bolster Warning & Restrictions

Be careful when Hunkering Down with a card that has the Bolster action, as you may not be able to add it to your Deck later. You cannot Hunker Down with Fog of War cards.

Placing Combat Counters Spawn Marker Placement

When you would take a card action with a combat card and the corresponding Combat Counter is not on the board, immediately place the counter on the board on the space indicated by the relevant Spawn Marker. Ensure the counter is placed side-up. Then perform the action as normal.

Hunker Down Exception

Do not place a Combat Counter when you Hunker Down.

Actions Reference

When an action's name is followed by X, that action has a numerical value. For example: Move 1. When an action's name is followed by XA, that action has a numerical value and may also specify a squad (A, B, or C). For example: Bolster 3 or Bolster 1A.

Movement Actions Move X

Move the unit's Combat Counter up to X tiles. The tile you move onto must be scouted or controlled by your side. If you move your Mortar Combat Counter, remove your Target Marker from the board.

Guide X

Move any Combat Counter up to X tiles. The tile you move onto must be scouted or controlled by your side. If you move your Mortar Combat Counter, remove your Target Marker from the board. You cannot move a Suppressed Combat Counter.

Scout X

Move the unit's Combat Counter up to X tiles. If either tile you move onto does not have one of your Control Markers on it, place a Control Marker there on its scouted side. For each Control Marker placed, take a Fog of War card from your Supply and place it in your Discard Pile. If you need to take more Fog of War cards than are available in your Supply, take as many as there are available.

Stalk X

Move the unit's Combat Counter up to X tiles. The tile you move onto does not need to be scouted or controlled by your side.

Support Actions Squad Restrictions

If an action specifies a squad letter (A, B, or C), only cards from that specific squad may be affected by the action.

Bolster X A

Take up to X cards from your Supply and add them to your Discard Pile. If the card specifies a squad, only cards from that squad in your Supply may be taken.

Command X

Draw up to X cards from your Deck and add them to your Hand. Those cards can be played as normal this turn. If your Deck runs out of cards before you have finished drawing, shuffle your Discard Pile into a Deck and continue drawing. Do not shuffle any cards in your Play Area into your Deck.

Conceal

Take a Fog of War card from the opponent's Supply and place it in their Discard Pile. If there are no Fog of War cards in their Supply, this action has no effect.

Control

Take control of the tile that the unit's Combat Counter is on by flipping the Control Marker to the controlled side. If your opponent controls that tile, flip their Control Marker back to its scouted side. You cannot take control of a tile that an enemy Combat Counter is on.

Inspire X A

Choose up to X cards from your Play Area and add them to your Hand. Those cards can be played as normal this turn. If the card specifies a squad, only cards from that squad in your Play Area may be taken.

Recon

Choose a Fog of War card from your Hand and remove it from the game. Then draw a card from your Deck. That card can be played as normal this turn. If your Deck is empty, shuffle your Discard Pile into a Deck and continue drawing. Do not shuffle any cards in your Play Area into your Deck. If you have no Fog of War cards in your Hand, you cannot take this action.

Target

Place your Target Marker on a tile that is three or more tiles away from your Mortar Combat Counter. If your Target Marker is already on a tile, move it to another tile. If you move your Mortar Combat Counter, remove your Target Marker from the board.

Combat Resolution

Combat follows a strict step-by-step workflow. This applies to Attack, Suppress, and Blast actions.

Step 1: Choose Target Standard Targeting

For an Attack or Suppress action, choose any enemy Combat Counter on the board.

Blast Targeting

For a Blast action, all Combat Counters on the tile with the Target Marker are targeted, including any of your own counters. Perform a separate attack against each Combat Counter on that tile, taking the steps below for each counter. If the Target Marker is not on the board, the Blast action cannot be taken.

Step 2: Determine Total Defence Value Base Defence & Cover Bonus

A Combat Counter's total defence value is the sum of its base defence, the tile's cover bonus, and the range bonus. Base defence is the value printed on the Combat Counter. Cover bonus is the value printed on the tile that the Combat Counter is on.

Range Bonus & Hills

Range bonus is the number of tiles away from the attacking Combat Counter that the defender is, not including the attacker's tile. No range bonus is granted against a Blast action. Some tiles have '3/1' listed as their cover bonus. These are hill tiles with a variable cover bonus. If a Combat Counter on a hill tile is attacked by another Combat Counter on a hill tile, or if the attack is a Blast action, the cover bonus is 1. Otherwise, the cover bonus is 3.

Step 3: Roll Attack Dice Dice Resolution

Roll the number of dice equal to the value of the Attack, Suppress, or Blast action. If any of the dice show a number equal to or greater than the defender's total defence value, the attack is successful. How many dice are successful does not matter. A die showing '0' is always successful, no matter how high the defender's total defence value.

Step 4: Determine Casualties Casualty Removal Order

If the attack is successful, you have inflicted casualties on the selected unit. Your opponent must find a card from the attacked Combat Counter's unit and remove it from the game. You only ever remove one card after an attack, no matter how many dice were successful. You can always check cards that you have removed from the game. You cannot check your opponent's removed cards. Follow this removal order strictly:

  1. If possible, remove the card from their Hand.
  2. If they do not have a card of that unit in their Hand, remove it from their Discard Pile.
  3. If they do not have a card of that unit in their Discard Pile, remove it from their Deck. They must then shuffle their Deck.
  4. If they do not have a card of that unit in their Deck, remove the Combat Counter from the board.
Suppress Outcome Split

If the attack was a Suppress action, no casualties are inflicted. Instead, flip the attacked Combat Counter to its suppressed side. If it is already suppressed, the attack has no effect.

Counter Removal & Return

As units suffer casualties, they may have their Combat Counters removed from the board. By bolstering them and later playing them, these units may return to the board. When a Combat Counter returns to the board, it is placed on the tile with the appropriate Spawn Marker, not the tile it was removed from.

Victory & Game End

Exact point thresholds and win conditions are defined per scenario. As soon as a victory condition is reached, the game ends immediately, without completing the round.

Capturing Objectives

The scenario will specify the number of objective points required for victory. To claim this victory, the total objective points on all the tiles you control must equal or exceed the required total.

Pinning the Enemy

If your opponent has no Riflemen Combat Counters on the board, you have successfully pinned the enemy. If both sides are pinned but neither side has won, the game ends and the player with the most objective points wins. In the case of a tie, the player with the Initiative Marker wins.

Beyond All Hope

It is possible, but rare, that you can no longer win the scenario by scoring the required objective points. If this happens, you count as pinned. If your opponent's objective is to pin you, they immediately win. If your opponent's objective is to capture objectives, count how many objective points they currently control. If your opponent controls more than you, they immediately win. Otherwise, keep playing until your opponent controls more objective points than you (at which point they win) or until they are pinned, at which point the winner is decided in the same way as when both sides are pinned.
